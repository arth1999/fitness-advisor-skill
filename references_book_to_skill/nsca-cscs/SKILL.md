# NSCA CSCS 5th Edition — Strength & Conditioning Skill

## Overview

This skill encodes the operational frameworks from the NSCA's *Essentials of Strength Training and Conditioning* (5th Edition), edited by G. Gregory Haff and N. Travis Triplett. The content is structured for direct application: extract named protocols, exact parameter ranges, and decision rules rather than summaries.

## Core Frameworks

### 1. Seven-Step Resistance Training Program Design (Ch18)

Apply these steps **in sequence** for every program:

1. **Needs Analysis** — Evaluate the sport (movement, physiological, injury analysis) + assess the athlete (training status: Beginner/Intermediate/Advanced; physical testing)
2. **Exercise Selection** — Core (structural/power) vs. Assistance; sport-specific; muscle balance
3. **Training Frequency** — By status (2-3 beginner, 3-4 intermediate, 4-7 advanced) AND season (1-3 in-season, 4-6 off-season)
4. **Exercise Order** — Power → Core → Assistance; or Lower/Upper alternated; or Push/Pull alternated
5. **Training Load & Repetitions** — %1RM-rep relationship; goal-specific loading (strength ≥85%, power 75-90%, hypertrophy 67-80%, endurance ≤67%)
6. **Volume** — Multiple sets > single sets for trained athletes; goal-specific set/rep ranges
7. **Rest Periods** — Strength/Power: 2-5 min; Hypertrophy: 2-3 min (multi), 60-90 s (single); Endurance: ≤30 s

### 2. Plyometric Program Design (Ch19)

- **SSC phases**: Eccentric (energy storage) → Amortization (must be short!) → Concentric (energy release)
- **Intensity continuum**: Low (pogos, skips) → Moderate (hurdle jumps, bounds) → High (depth jumps)
- **Volume by experience/intensity**: Beginner: 80-100 (low), not recommended (high); Advanced: 120-200 (low), 20-40 (high)
- **Safety**: Depth jumps contraindicated for prepubescent children; athletes >220 lb use ≤18 in box

### 3. Speed & Agility Training (Ch20)

- **Speed** = step length × step frequency; determined by RFD and impulse
- **COD ability**: Physical capacity to change direction (pre-planned)
- **Agility**: COD + perceptual-cognitive component (reaction to stimulus)
- COD <45° = fast SSC; >45° = slow SSC with longer ground contact

### 4. Aerobic Endurance Training (Ch21)

- **Five types**: Extensive endurance (base), Pace/Tempo (LT), Interval (VO₂max), HIIT (economy/power), Fartlek (varied)
- **Five-zone intensity model**: Zone 1 (50-60% VO₂max) through Zone 5 (94-100% VO₂max)
- **Karvonen method**: THR = (HRR × intensity%) + RHR
- **Rule**: ~80% of total volume at extensive endurance intensity

### 5. Periodization (Ch22)

- **Three models**: Parallel (all factors simultaneously — novices), Sequential (limited factors in blocks — advanced), Emphasis (rotating focus — team sports)
- **Hierarchy**: Multiyear → Annual → Macrocycle → Mesocycle (2-6 weeks) → Microcycle (1 week) → Training day → Session
- **Periods**: Preparatory (off-season) → First Transition (preseason) → Competitive (in-season) → Second Transition (postseason, ≤4 weeks)
- **Fitness–fatigue paradigm**: Fatigue dissipates faster than fitness; vary loads to allow fatigue drop while retaining fitness

### 6. Rehabilitation & Reconditioning (Ch23)

- **Tissue healing phases**: Inflammatory (72h, POLICE) → Fibroblastic repair (72h-2mo, submaximal) → Maturation-remodeling (weeks-years, progressive sport-specific)
- **Five principles**: (1) Don't overstress healing tissue; (2) Criteria-based progression; (3) Evidence-based; (4) Individualized; (5) Team-oriented
- **SAID applies to rehab**: Marathoner = high reps/low intensity; Weightlifter = low reps/high intensity

### 7. Special Populations (Ch7, Ch8)

- **Youth**: Neural adaptations pre-puberty; hypertrophy possible post-PHV; depth jumps contraindicated before skeletal maturity
- **Older adults**: 2-3×/week; avoid Valsalva; must include balance + flexibility (RT alone doesn't prevent falls)
- **Female athletes**: Higher ACL injury risk (neuromuscular deficiency); pre-pubertal training critical; menstrual cycle may influence performance; pregnancy modifications required

## How to Use This Skill

1. **Design a program**: Start with `ch18-program-design-resistance-training` — apply all 7 steps
2. **Add plyometrics**: Reference `ch19-plyometric-training` for exercise progression and volume guidelines
3. **Plan the training year**: Use `ch22-periodization` for macrocycle structure and phase sequencing
4. **Check special considerations**: Reference `ch07`, `ch08` for population-specific adjustments
5. **Rehab an injury**: Reference `ch23-rehabilitation-reconditioning` for phase-appropriate exercise
