## Table 4: Plyometric Intensity Progression

**Low intensity (Beginner: 80-100 contacts):**
Pogo jumps, squat jumps, ankle hops, tuck jumps, split squat jumps, skipping, jump and reach

**Moderate intensity (Intermediate: 20-120 contacts):**
Standing long jumps, vertical jumps, multiple hurdle jumps, lateral barrier jumps, bounds, box jumps (low), linear hops

**High intensity (Advanced: 20-40 contacts):**
Depth jumps, depth hops, depth jump to box jump, depth jump to long jump, depth jump to 180° turn, depth jump into sprint, loaded jump squats

**Key volume rules:**
- Beginner: High intensity NOT recommended
- 48-72 h between sessions
- Work:rest = 1:5 to 1:10
- Depth jump box: 16-42 in (41-107 cm); norm 30-32 in (76-81 cm)
- Athletes >220 lb (100 kg): depth jump box ≤18 in (46 cm)
- Program length: 6-10 weeks

**Strength prerequisites for lower body:**
- In-place jumps: Minimal
- Repeated hops/jumps: ~1.0× BW back squat
- Bounding, depth jumps ≤12 in (30 cm): ~1.5× BW back squat
- Depth/box jumps >12 in (30 cm): ~2.0× BW back squat

## Table 5: Periodization Models Comparison

| Model | How It Works | Best For | Key Concern |
|-------|-------------|----------|-------------|
| **Parallel** | All training factors simultaneously in each session/cycle | Youth, novices, developmental | Total load must continually increase; may exceed tolerance |
| **Sequential** | Limited factors organized in logical pattern toward target | Intermediate/advanced athletes | Requires longer preparation; not for congested schedules |
| **Emphasis** | Multiple factors with rotating emphasis (~2-week cycles) | Team sports, congested schedules | Some factors detrain with minimal dose |

### Traditional Periodization Phases

| Period | Season | Phase | Intensity (%1RM) | Volume (sets × reps) |
|--------|--------|-------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Preparatory (General) | Off-season | Hypertrophy/Endurance | 50-75% | 3-6 × 8-20 |
| Preparatory (Specific) | Off-season | Basic Strength | 80-95% | 2-6 × 2-6 |
| First Transition | Preseason | Strength/Power | 30-95% (varies) | 2-5 × 2-5 |
| Competitive (Peak) | In-season | Peaking | 50% to ≥93% | 1-3 × 1-3 |
| Competitive (Maintain) | In-season | Maintenance | 85-93% | ~2-5 × 3-6 |
| Second Transition | Postseason | Active Rest | Recreational | ≤4 weeks |

## Table 7: Tissue Healing Timeline (Table 23.1)

| Phase | Timeline | Key Events | Exercise Strategy |
|-------|----------|------------|-------------------|
| **Inflammatory** | ~72 h | Pain, swelling, redness; decreased collagen synthesis | No active exercise for injured area; POLICE; maintain uninjured areas |
| **Fibroblastic repair** | ~72 h to 2 months | Type III collagen deposition; decreased organization | Submaximal isometric → isotonic; neuromuscular control training |
| **Maturation-remodeling** | Weeks to years | Type I collagen alignment; increased tissue strength | Sport-specific; velocity-specific; closed → open chain; progressive loading |

## Table 8: SSC Phase Summary (Table 19.1)

| Phase | Action | Physiological Event |
|-------|--------|-------------------|
| I - Eccentric | Stretch of agonist | Elastic energy stored in SEC; muscle spindles stimulated |
| II - Amortization | Brief pause | Type Ia afferents → alpha motor neurons; signal transmission |
| III - Concentric | Shortening of agonist | Elastic energy released; alpha motor neurons stimulate agonist |

### Elastic Energy Loss vs. Amortization Time
- 0.35 s delay → 25% energy loss
- 0.9 s delay → 52% energy loss
- 1.0 s delay → 55% energy loss
- 1.5 s delay → 70% energy loss

## Table 9: Youth vs. Older Adult Training Quick Reference

| Variable | Youth (Prepubescent) | Youth (Adolescent) | Older Adults |
|----------|---------------------|-------------------|--------------|
| Primary adaptation | Neural | Neural + Hypertrophic | Strength, Power, Function |
| Frequency | 2-3×/week | 2-4×/week | 2-3×/week |
| Intensity start | Low; technique first | Moderate, progressing | 40-50% 1RM |
| Intensity goal | Moderate | Up to >80% 1RM | 70-85% 1RM |
| Sets/Reps | 1-3 × varied | 2-4 × varied | 1-3 × 6-12 |
| Key caution | PHV injury risk; focus on technique | Epiphyseal plates (pre-close) | Avoid Valsalva; 48-72 h recovery |
| Plyometrics | Depth jumps contraindicated | Progressive; no depth jumps before PHV | Low-intensity; balance training essential |
