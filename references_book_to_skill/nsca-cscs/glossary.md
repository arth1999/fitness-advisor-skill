# Glossary of NSCA CSCS Terminology (5th Edition)

## Program Design - General

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Specificity (SAID)** | Training in a specific manner to produce a specific adaptation; type of demand dictates type of adaptation |
| **Overload** | Training stimulus exceeding what the athlete is accustomed to, producing adaptation beyond baseline |
| **Progression** | Sequential, specific increase in intensity or volume to promote long-term benefits |
| **Variation** | Manipulation of overload and specificity to remove linearity and provide novel stimuli |
| **Reversibility** | Deterioration or loss of fitness characteristics when training stimulus removed or reduced |
| **Individualization** | Training program suited specifically to each athlete's unique goals, genetics, and training history |
| **1RM (1-repetition maximum)** | The greatest amount of weight that can be lifted with proper technique for only 1 repetition |
| **xRM (multiple RM)** | The most weight lifted for a specified number of repetitions (e.g., 10RM) |
| **Volume-load** | Sets × repetitions × load; a proxy for mechanical work |
| **Repetition-volume** | Total number of repetitions performed in a training session |
| **Set** | Group of repetitions sequentially performed before rest |
| **Rest period (inter-set rest)** | Time dedicated to recovery between sets and exercises |
| **Microdosing** | Subdivision of weekly training volume across multiple short-duration bouts |
| **Split routine** | Training different muscle groups on different days within the training week |
| **RPE (Rating of Perceived Exertion)** | Subjective measurement of work intensity (0-10 CR10 scale for resistance training) |
| **RIR (Repetitions in Reserve)** | Perceived number of repetitions that could theoretically be performed after a set |
| **SRB (Set-Repetition Best)** | Load prescription using relative percentage ranges based on multi-set performances, with 5% loading ranges |
| **2-for-2 rule** | Conservative load increase: add weight when athlete performs ≥2 extra reps in last set for 2 consecutive workouts |

## Exercise Classification

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Core exercise** | Recruits ≥1 large muscle area, involves ≥2 primary joints; receives priority in selection |
| **Assistance exercise** | Recruits smaller muscle areas, involves only 1 primary joint; for injury prevention/rehabilitation |
| **Structural exercise** | Core exercise that loads the spine directly or indirectly; requires muscular stabilization of posture |
| **Power exercise** | Structural exercise performed very quickly or explosively |
| **Multijoint exercise** | Exercise involving ≥2 primary joints |
| **Single-joint exercise** | Exercise involving only 1 primary joint |
| **Agonist** | Muscle or muscle group actively causing the movement |
| **Antagonist** | Muscle or muscle group on the opposite side of the limb |
| **Superset** | Two sequentially performed exercises stressing opposing muscles (agonist-antagonist) |
| **Compound set** | Two sequentially performed exercises for the same muscle group |
| **Circuit training** | Alternating exercises with minimal rest periods (20-30 s) |

## Needs Analysis

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Movement analysis** | Evaluation of body/limb movement patterns and muscular involvement in a sport |
| **Physiological analysis** | Evaluation of strength, power, hypertrophy, and muscular endurance priorities |
| **Injury analysis** | Evaluation of common injury sites and causative factors |
| **Training status** | Athlete's current condition/level of preparedness (Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced) |
| **Exercise technique experience** | Knowledge and skill to perform resistance training exercises properly |
| **Training background (exercise history)** | Training that occurred before beginning a new or revised program |

## Plyometric Training

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Stretch-shortening cycle (SSC)** | Eccentric-concentric coupling phenomenon using elastic energy storage and stretch reflex |
| **Amortization phase** | Pause between eccentric and concentric phases; must be kept short for SSC effectiveness |
| **Series elastic component (SEC)** | Tendons and muscle fiber crossbridges; stores and releases elastic energy |
| **Ballistic plyometrics** | Exercises with minimal neuromuscular SSC contributions (e.g., squat jump) |
| **Rebound plyometrics** | Exercises requiring physiomechanical SSC contributions (e.g., drop jumps) |
| **Fast SSC** | Ground contact time <0.25 s |
| **Slow SSC** | Ground contact time >0.25 s |
| **Depth jump** | Stepping off a box, landing, and immediately jumping vertically, horizontally, or to another box |
| **Complex training** | Combination of resistance training and plyometrics in the same session |
| **Contrast training** | Alternating high-load and high-velocity exercises set-by-set |
| **Postactivation potentiation (PAP)** | Enhanced force production following a conditioning contraction |

## Speed & Agility

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Speed** | Skills and abilities needed to achieve high movement velocities |
| **Change-of-direction (COD) ability** | Skills and abilities to explosively change movement direction, velocity, or modes (predetermined) |
| **Agility** | Skills and abilities to change direction, velocity, or mode in response to a stimulus (perception-action coupling) |
| **Rate of force development (RFD)** | Development of maximal force in minimal time; indicator of explosive strength |
| **Impulse** | Product of force and time (area under force-time curve); dictates change in momentum |
| **Spring-mass model (SMM)** | Mathematical model depicting sprinting as coiling/extension of spring-like leg actions |
| **Step length** | Distance between toe and heel of two consecutive footsteps |
| **Step frequency (step rate)** | Number of steps per second |
| **Stride** | Two steps (right to left, left to right); one complete running cycle |
| **Braking impulse** | Production of braking forces over a period of time for deceleration |
| **Ground contact time** | Duration of stance/plant phase when foot is in contact with surface |

## Aerobic Endurance Training

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **VO₂max (maximal aerobic capacity)** | Maximal rate of oxygen consumption during exercise |
| **Lactate threshold (LT)** | Speed or %VO₂max at which blood lactate begins accumulating above resting |
| **Maximal lactate steady state (MLSS)** | Exercise intensity where lactate production equals lactate clearance; synonymous with critical power |
| **Exercise economy** | Energy cost of activity at a given exercise velocity |
| **Extensive endurance training** | Long duration, low intensity (≤65% VO₂max); develops aerobic base |
| **Pace/Tempo training** | Intensity at LT or slightly above race pace; ~20-30 min; improves LT and economy |
| **Interval training** | Work bouts close to VO₂max (3-5 min); W:R = 1:1 |
| **HIIT (High-intensity interval training)** | Repeated high-intensity bouts above MLSS; W:R ≥ 1:5 |
| **Fartlek** | "Speed play"; varied intensity combining extensive endurance, pace/tempo, and short sprints |
| **Karvonen method** | Target HR = (HRR × intensity%) + RHR; where HRR = APMHR − RHR |
| **Heart rate reserve (HRR)** | Difference between maximal heart rate and resting heart rate |
| **Cross-training** | Alternative mode of training to maintain general conditioning during injury/recovery |
| **Detraining** | Loss of physiological adaptations when training stimulus reduced or stopped |
| **Minimum effective dose (MED)** | Minimum training stimuli required to maximize adaptations while minimizing damage |
| **Maximum tolerated dose (MTD)** | Maximum training workload providing measurable benefits before risk outweighs reward |

## Periodization

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Periodization** | Logical, systematic process of sequencing and integrating training interventions to peak at appropriate times |
| **Programming** | Micro-management of the training process; defining actual modes, methods, and loads |
| **GAS (General Adaptation Syndrome)** | Three-stage stress response: alarm → resistance → exhaustion |
| **Fitness-fatigue paradigm** | Training creates fitness and fatigue aftereffects; fatigue dissipates faster than fitness |
| **Supercompensation** | Adaptive response elevating performance beyond baseline following recovery |
| **Macrocycle** | Several months to a year; contains preparatory, competitive, and transitional periods |
| **Mesocycle** | 2-6 weeks (common: 4); block of training consisting of linked microcycles |
| **Microcycle** | Several days to 2 weeks (common: 1); comprised of multiple workouts |
| **Preparatory period** | Off-season; develops base conditioning; includes general and specific preparatory phases |
| **First transition period** | Preseason; links preparatory to competitive; focuses on strength/power |
| **Competitive period** | In-season; peaking (1-2 weeks) or maintenance (team sports, long season) |
| **Second transition period** | Postseason; active rest; ≤4 weeks |
| **Parallel training model** | All training factors targeted simultaneously; best for novices/youth |
| **Sequential training model** | Limited training factors organized in logical pattern; best for intermediate/advanced |
| **Emphasis training model** | Multiple factors targeted with varying degrees of emphasis rotated over time; best for congested schedules |
| **Residual training effects** | Aftereffects of one training period affecting preparedness in subsequent periods |

## Rehabilitation & Reconditioning

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Macrotrauma** | Specific, sudden overload injury disrupting tissue integrity |
| **Microtrauma** | Overuse injury from repeated abnormal stress with too little recovery |
| **Sprain** | Ligament trauma (Grade I: partial, no instability; Grade II: partial, minor instability; Grade III: complete, full instability) |
| **Strain** | Muscle/tendon trauma (Grade I: partial, strong/painful; Grade II: partial, weak/painful; Grade III: complete, weak/painless) |
| **Tendinitis** | Inflammation of a tendon |
| **Tendinopathy** | Degenerative tendon condition with minimal inflammation |
| **POLICE** | Protection, Optimal Loading, Ice, Compression, Elevation (replaced PRICE) |
| **Mechanotransduction** | Mechanical overload → cellular DNA upregulation → collagen synthesis |
| **Closed kinetic chain** | Distal joint segment fixed; feet/hands stationary during movement |
| **Open kinetic chain** | Distal joint segment free to move |
| **Proprioception** | Sensory feedback from skin, muscles, tendons, ligaments, and joint capsules |
| **Neuromuscular control** | Muscle response to sensory feedback to maintain joint stability |
| **DAPRE** | Daily Adjustable Progressive Resistive Exercise; 4-set protocol with auto-regulated 4th set |
| **Indication** | Form of treatment required by the rehabilitating athlete |
| **Contraindication** | Activity or practice inadvisable or prohibited due to a given injury |

## Youth & Older Adults

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Athleticism** | Ability to repeatedly execute a variety of movements with precision and confidence |
| **Long-term athletic development** | Development of athleticism over time to enhance fitness, reduce injury, improve health |
| **PHV (Peak height velocity)** | Age at maximum rate of growth during pubertal growth spurt |
| **Biological maturation** | Progress toward mature state; measured by skeletal, sexual, or somatic age |
| **Chronological age** | Age in months or years |
| **Sarcopenia** | Progressive, generalized skeletal muscle disorder associated with falls, fractures, disability, mortality |
| **Dynapenia** | Age-related loss of muscle strength distinct from sarcopenia |
| **Osteopenia** | BMD between −1 and −2.5 SD of young adult mean |
| **Osteoporosis** | BMD below −2.5 SD of young adult mean |
| **AMSC (Athletic Motor Skill Competencies)** | Foundational movements underpinning all athletic movements |
| **YPD (Youth Physical Development) model** | Model providing training guidelines for males and females across developmental stages |
| **Early sport specialization** | Intensive, year-round (>8 months) training in a single sport beginning prepubertal years |
| **Activities of daily living (ADL)** | Everyday tasks requiring physical capability (e.g., rising from a chair, stair climbing) |
| **Valsalva maneuver** | Forced expiration against a closed glottis; generally discouraged in older adults |
| **Cocontraction** | Simultaneous activation of agonist and antagonist muscle groups for joint stabilization |
