# Ch18: Program Design for Resistance Training

## Seven-Step Program Design Process (NSCA CSCS 5th Ed.)

Effective resistance training programs coordinate variables in a sequential, systematic fashion. Approach one program element at a time, grounded in the **principles of anaerobic exercise prescription**: specificity (SAID), overload, progression, variation, reversibility, and individualization.

---

### Step 1: Needs Analysis (Two-Stage Evaluation)

**A. Evaluation of the Sport**
- **Movement analysis**: Body/limb movement patterns and muscular involvement
- **Physiological analysis**: Strength, power, hypertrophy, muscular endurance priorities
- **Injury analysis**: Common sites for joint/muscle injury and causative factors

**B. Assessment of the Athlete**
- **Training status**: Classify as Beginner, Intermediate, or Advanced using Table 18.1:

| Training Status | Training Age | Frequency | Training Stress | Technique |
|----------------|-------------|-----------|----------------|-----------|
| Beginner | <2 months | ≤1-2/week | None/Low | None/Minimal |
| Intermediate | 2-6 months | ≤2-3/week | Medium | Basic |
| Advanced | ≥1 year | ≥3-4/week | High | High |

- **Physical testing**: 1RM testing, isometric mid-thigh pull, countermovement jump, rebound jump, body composition
- **Primary resistance training goal**: Based on sport season priorities:

| Season | Sport Practice Priority | RT Priority | RT Goal |
|--------|------------------------|-------------|---------|
| Off-season | Low | High | Hypertrophy/endurance → Strength/Power |
| Preseason | Medium | Medium | Sport/movement specific |
| In-season | High | Low | Maintenance; may alternate strength/power |
| Postseason | Variable | Variable | Active rest |

---

### Step 2: Exercise Selection

**Exercise type classification:**
- **Core exercises**: Recruit large muscle areas, involve ≥2 primary joints, receive priority; further classified as:
  - **Structural**: Load the spine directly or indirectly (e.g., back squat, power clean)
  - **Power**: Structural exercise performed explosively (e.g., snatch, push jerk, jump squat)
- **Assistance exercises**: Recruit smaller muscle areas, single-joint; for injury prevention and rehabilitation

**Selection factors**: Sport movement analysis, specificity (dynamic correspondence), muscle balance (agonist/antagonist ratios), athlete technique experience, equipment availability, time availability.

### Step 3: Training Frequency

**By training status:**

| Training Status | Sessions/Week |
|----------------|---------------|
| Beginner | 2-3 |
| Intermediate | 3-4 |
| Advanced | 4-7 |

**By sport season (trained athlete):**

| Season | Sessions/Week |
|--------|---------------|
| Off-season | 4-6 |
| Preseason | 3-4 |
| In-season | 1-3 |
| Postseason | 0-3 |

**Total-body vs. split routines**: No differences in hypertrophy or strength when volume equated. Split routines allow greater volume-load per muscle group. Use split routines in off-season; total-body in preseason/in-season.

---

### Step 4: Exercise Order

Four arrangement methods, prioritized:

1. **Power → Other Core → Assistance**: Power exercises first while metabolically fresh; greatest maximal strength gains occur in exercises programmed earlier.
2. **Lower/Upper body alternated**: Minimizes rest between body areas; use lower body first.
3. **Push/Pull alternated**: Ensures same muscle group not used successively; ideal for novices.
4. **Supersets** (agonist/antagonist pairs) and **Compound sets** (same muscle group): Time-efficient; not appropriate for unconditioned athletes.

---

### Step 5: Training Load and Repetitions

**%1RM ↔ Repetitions relationship (Table 18.8):**

| %1RM | Reps | %1RM | Reps |
|------|------|------|------|
| 100 | 1 | 83 | 7 |
| 95 | 2 | 80 | 8 |
| 93 | 3 | 77 | 9 |
| 90 | 4 | 75 | 10 |
| 87 | 5 | 70 | 11 |
| 85 | 6 | 67 | 12 |
| — | — | 65 | 15 |

**Caveats**: Curvilinear relationship reported; trained athletes may exceed reps at a given %1RM; exercise-specific and single-set based. Use as guideline only.

**Load assignment by training goal (Table 18.11):**

| Goal | Load (%1RM) | Goal Reps |
|------|------------|-----------|
| Strength (core exercises) | ≥85% | ≤6 |
| Power (single-effort) | 80-90% | 1-2 |
| Power (multiple-effort) | 75-85% | 3-5 |
| Hypertrophy | 67-80% | 8-12 |
| Muscular endurance | ≤67% | ≥12 |

**For power exercises**: Loads are NOT true RMs — lighter loads allow maximal speed. Non-weightlifting power exercises reach peak power at 0-30% 1RM. Weightlifting derivatives: 60-80% 1RM for catching variants; 45-140% 1RM for pulling variants depending on velocity/force emphasis.

**Load prescription methods:**
- **1RM testing**: For intermediate/advanced with technique experience (protocol in Fig 18.8)
- **xRM estimation**: Use Table 18.9 to estimate 1RM from 3RM-10RM
- **RPE/RIR**: Subjective, use ranges (RPE 7-8, RIR 2-3); best combined with objective methods
- **Set-Repetition Best (SRB)**: Uses 5% ranges with autoregulation; accounts for multi-set fatigue

**Load progression:**
- **2-for-2 rule** (conservative): Add load when athlete performs ≥2 extra reps over goal in last set for 2 consecutive workouts
- **Quantity increases:**

| Athlete Type | Upper Body | Lower Body |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|
| Weaker/less trained | 2.5-5 lb (1-2 kg) | 5-10 lb (2-4 kg) |
| Stronger/more trained | 5-10+ lb (2-4+ kg) | 10-15+ lb (4-7+ kg) |

- **Relative increase**: 2.5-10% per week. Apply "heavy day" and "light day" variation.

---

### Step 6: Volume

**Volume = sets × reps**, expressed as volume-load (sets × reps × load). Multiple sets > single sets for trained athletes.

**Volume assignments by training goal (Table 18.13):**

| Goal | Novice Sets | Intermediate Sets | Advanced Sets | Rep Range |
|------|------------|-------------------|---------------|-----------|
| Strength | 1-3 | ≥3 | ≥3 | ≤6 |
| Power (single-effort) | n/a | 1-3 | 3-6 | 1-3 |
| Power (multiple-effort) | n/a | 1-3 | 3-6 | 3-6 |
| Hypertrophy | 1-3 | ≥3 | ≥3 | 3-12 |
| Muscular endurance | 1-3 | ≥3 | ≥3 | 12-25 |

**Heavy-load hypertrophy**: 10 sets × 3 reps at >80% 1RM targets type II fibers more than traditional 3×10.

---

### Step 7: Rest Periods

**By training goal (Table 18.15):**

| Goal | Rest Period Length |
|------|--------------------|
| Strength | 2-5 min |
| Power | 2-5 min |
| Hypertrophy | 2-3 min (multijoint); 60-90 s (single-joint) |
| Muscular endurance | ≤30 s |

Base rest on actual load used per exercise, not just the global training goal. Core exercises with heavy loads demand longer rest.

## Weightlifting Derivatives Loading Guide (Table 18.14)

| Derivative Type | Exercise Examples | Load (%1RM) | Reps |
|----------------|-------------------|-------------|------|
| Catching | Hang clean/snatch, clean from knee | 60-90% | 2-5 |
| Pulling (force-dominant) | Mid-thigh pull, countermovement shrug | 100-140% | 4-6 |
| Pulling (velocity-dominant) | Jump shrug | 30-45% | 4-6 |
| Pulling (moderate) | Pull from floor | 80-120% | 2-6 |
| Pulling (high-velocity) | Hang high pull | 45-60% | 4-6 |
| Overhead pressing | Push press, push jerk, split jerk | 70-90% | 2-5 |
