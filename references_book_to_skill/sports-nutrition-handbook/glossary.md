# Glossary of Sports Nutrition Terms

## A
- **Adenosine Triphosphate (ATP)**: The biologically usable form of energy in the human body; required for all muscle contraction.
- **Adequate Intake (AI)**: Estimated nutrient intake assumed to be adequate when an RDA cannot be determined.
- **Aerobic Metabolism**: Energy production requiring oxygen; uses Krebs cycle and electron transport chain; virtually unlimited ATP supply.
- **Anabolic Resistance**: Reduced muscle protein synthesis response to protein feeding; seen in older adults and with inactivity.
- **Anaerobic Glycolysis**: Breakdown of glucose/glycogen without oxygen; rapid ATP production; produces lactate.
- **Anorexia Nervosa**: Clinical eating disorder characterized by severe energy restriction, fear of weight gain, and distorted body image.
- **Antioxidants**: Compounds that counteract free radical damage; vitamins C, E, A, selenium, polyphenols.
- **ATP-PC System**: Immediate energy system using stored phosphocreatine; lasts 0-10 seconds.

## B
- **β-Alanine**: Amino acid supplement; increases muscle carnosine (intracellular buffer); benefits efforts lasting 1-4 minutes.
- **β-Oxidation**: Mitochondrial process breaking down fatty acids for ATP production.
- **BIA (Bioelectrical Impedance Analysis)**: Body composition method using electrical current; highly sensitive to hydration status.
- **Branched-Chain Amino Acids (BCAAs)**: Leucine, isoleucine, valine; oxidized during exercise; leucine is key anabolic signal.
- **Bulimia Nervosa**: Clinical eating disorder involving binge eating followed by compensatory behaviors.

## C
- **Carbohydrate Loading**: Strategy to maximize muscle glycogen stores (10-12 g/kg/d + 36-48h rest); benefits events >90 min.
- **Cardiac Output**: Volume of blood pumped per heartbeat; product of heart rate × stroke volume.
- **Crossover Concept**: Shift from fat to carbohydrate as predominant fuel as exercise intensity increases.
- **Creatine**: Most evidence-based ergogenic supplement; supports ATP-PC system; 3-5 g/d maintenance.

## D
- **DEXA (Dual-Energy X-ray Absorptiometry)**: Body composition measurement using low-dose X-ray; gold standard for clinical use.
- **DHA (Docosahexaenoic Acid)**: Long-chain n-3 fatty acid; critical for brain and retinal development; anti-inflammatory.
- **Dietary Reference Intakes (DRIs)**: Umbrella term for RDA, AI, EAR, UL; nutrient intake recommendations.
- **Disordered Eating**: Spectrum of abnormal eating behaviors; ranges from mild restriction to clinical eating disorders.

## E
- **EAAs (Essential Amino Acids)**: Amino acids that must be obtained from diet; primary stimulators of MPS.
- **Electron Transport Chain (ETC)**: Final stage of oxidative phosphorylation; produces majority of ATP aerobically.
- **Energy Availability**: Dietary energy intake minus exercise energy expenditure; what remains for physiological functions.
- **EPA (Eicosapentaenoic Acid)**: Long-chain n-3 fatty acid from marine sources; anti-inflammatory.
- **Ergogenic Aid**: Substance or technique that enhances physical performance.
- **Euhydration**: Normal body water status; well-hydrated state.
- **Exercise-Associated Hyponatremia**: Low blood sodium (<135 mEq/L) from excessive water intake; potentially life-threatening.

## F
- **Fat Adaptation**: Dietary strategy using high-fat intake to increase fat oxidation; metabolic adaptations occur but performance benefits unproven.
- **Female Athlete Triad**: Interrelationship of low energy availability, menstrual dysfunction, and low bone mineral density.
- **Free Fatty Acids (FFAs)**: Fatty acids in circulation; major lipid fuel source during exercise, released from adipose tissue.

## G
- **Glycemic Index (GI)**: Ranking of CHO foods by blood glucose response; scale 0-100.
- **Glycemic Load (GL)**: GI × grams of CHO consumed; accounts for both quality and quantity.
- **Glycogen**: Storage form of carbohydrate in liver (75-100g) and muscle (300-400g).
- **Gluconeogenesis**: Production of glucose from non-carbohydrate sources (amino acids, lactate, glycerol).

## H
- **Hepcidin**: Peptide hormone that inhibits iron absorption and sequesters iron in macrophages; increases with intense exercise/inflammation.
- **Homeostasis**: The body's regulation of its internal environment to maintain stable conditions.
- **Hormone-Sensitive Lipase (HSL)**: Enzyme that liberates fatty acids from adipose tissue triglycerides; activated during exercise.
- **Hyperhydration**: Above-normal body water; artificially induced for performance; no proven benefit.
- **Hypohydration (Dehydration)**: Below-normal body water status; impairs cardiovascular, thermoregulatory, and performance functions.
- **Hyponatremia**: Low blood sodium concentration (<135 mEq/L); caused by excessive water intake relative to sodium.

## I
- **IMCL (Intramyocellular Lipid)**: Triglyceride stored within muscle cells; increases with endurance training; local energy source.
- **Iron-Deficiency Anemia**: Advanced iron depletion with reduced hemoglobin; impairs aerobic capacity.

## K
- **Ketogenic Diet**: Very-low-carbohydrate diet (<10-50 g/d) inducing ketosis; may maintain moderate-intensity but impairs high-intensity performance.
- **Krebs Cycle (TCA Cycle)**: Central metabolic pathway for aerobic oxidation of acetyl-CoA from carbohydrates, fats, and proteins.

## L
- **Lactate**: Produced from pyruvate during high-intensity exercise; fuel source for heart, brain, and muscle; cleared via oxidation or Cori cycle.
- **Leucine Trigger Hypothesis**: ~2-3 g leucine per meal needed to maximally stimulate muscle protein synthesis.
- **Lipolysis**: Breakdown of triglycerides into free fatty acids and glycerol.

## M
- **MET (Metabolic Equivalent)**: Ratio of exercise to resting energy expenditure; 1 MET = 3.5 mL O₂/kg/min at rest.
- **Mitochondrial Biogenesis**: Production of new mitochondria; enhanced by endurance training and training with low CHO availability.
- **Motor Unit**: A single nerve and the group of muscle fibers it innervates.
- **MPS (Muscle Protein Synthesis)**: The process of building new muscle protein; stimulated by exercise and protein feeding.
- **MPB (Muscle Protein Breakdown)**: The breakdown of muscle protein; NPB = MPS - MPB.
- **Multiple Transportable Carbohydrates**: Glucose + fructose combination; exceeds SGLT1 absorption limit; enables >90 g/h CHO oxidation.

## N
- **NEAT (Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis)**: Energy expended for everything not sleeping, eating, or formal exercise.
- **Net Protein Balance (NPB)**: MPS minus MPB; positive NPB required for muscle accretion.
- **Nutrition Care Process (NCP)**: Systematic method used by RDNs: Assessment, Diagnosis, Intervention, Monitoring/Evaluation.

## O
- **Overtraining Syndrome (OTS)**: Long-term performance decrement with physiological and psychological symptoms; recovery takes weeks to months.
- **Osmolality**: Concentration of dissolved particles in a solution; key regulator of thirst and fluid balance.
- **Oxidative Phosphorylation**: Aerobic ATP production via Krebs cycle and ETC using oxygen as final electron acceptor.

## P
- **Periodization (Nutritional)**: Planned variation in nutrition to match training phases and goals; e.g., training high vs. training low.
- **Periodization (Training)**: Structured cycles of training intensity/volume; macrocycles, mesocycles, microcycles.
- **Phosphocreatine (PC)**: High-energy phosphate stored in muscle; phosphorylates ADP to ATP via creatine kinase; fuels 0-10 sec efforts.
- **Placebo Effect**: Performance benefit from belief in treatment; measurable in sports (1-3% performance change).
- **Progressive Overload**: Principle of gradually increasing training stimulus to drive continued adaptation.

## R
- **RDA (Recommended Dietary Allowance)**: Nutrient intake level adequate for ~98% of healthy individuals.
- **RED-S (Relative Energy Deficiency in Sport)**: Expanded model of the Female Athlete Triad; affects multiple body systems in both sexes.
- **RER (Respiratory Exchange Ratio)**: Ratio of CO₂ produced to O₂ consumed; indicates substrate utilization (0.7 = fat, 1.0 = CHO).
- **RMR (Resting Metabolic Rate)**: Energy expended at complete rest; 60-75% of TDEE.

## S
- **Sarcopenia**: Age-related loss of muscle mass and function; ~1-2% per year after age 50.
- **SGLT1 (Sodium-Dependent Glucose Transporter 1)**: Intestinal glucose transporter; saturates at ~1 g/min; limiting factor for single-CHO absorption.
- **Specificity**: Training principle: physiological adaptations are specific to exercise mode, intensity, and duration.
- **Sports Foods**: Specialized products (drinks, bars, gels) providing practical nutrient delivery for training/competition goals.
- **Steady-State Heart Rate**: Point where cardiovascular system delivers optimal oxygen and nutrients to working muscle.
- **Strict Liability (WADA)**: Athletes are responsible for any prohibited substance found in their body, regardless of origin.
- **Stroke Volume**: Volume of blood pumped during one heartbeat; increases with endurance training.

## T
- **TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure)**: Sum of RMR + TEF + PAEE; total daily caloric needs.
- **TEF (Thermic Effect of Food)**: Energy cost of digestion, absorption, and metabolism of nutrients; ~10% of TDEE.
- **Training Low**: Exercising with reduced carbohydrate availability to enhance mitochondrial adaptations.
- **Type I Muscle Fibers**: Slow-twitch, high oxidative capacity; used in endurance activities.
- **Type II Muscle Fibers**: Fast-twitch; Type IIa (oxidative/glycolytic), Type IIx (glycolytic); used for force/power.

## U
- **UL (Tolerable Upper Intake Level)**: Highest daily nutrient intake unlikely to cause adverse effects.

## V
- **VO₂max**: Maximum rate of oxygen consumption; gold standard measure of cardiorespiratory fitness.
- **Voluntary Dehydration**: Failure to drink enough to match sweat losses despite fluid availability; common during exercise.

## W
- **WADA (World Anti-Doping Agency)**: International organization governing anti-doping rules in sport; publishes Prohibited List annually.
