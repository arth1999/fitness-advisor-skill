# Chapter 8: Nutrition Assessment

## Key Concepts

Nutrition assessment is the first step in the Nutrition Care Process (NCP), a systematic problem-solving method using standardized language (eNCPT). Data are collected, recorded, and interpreted to identify nutrition-related problems.

## Assessment Categories (per NCP)

1. **Food/Nutrition-Related History**: Dietary intake, supplement use, food allergies, eating patterns, nutrition knowledge/beliefs
2. **Anthropometric Measurements**: Height, weight, BMI, body composition, growth patterns
3. **Biochemical Data, Medical Tests, and Procedures**: Laboratory values, medical history
4. **Nutrition-Focused Physical Findings**: Clinical signs of deficiency/excess, physical exam
5. **Client History**: Personal history, medical/health/family history, social history
6. **Comparative Standards**: DRIs, energy requirements, performance goals

## The Nutrition Assessment Interview

Data sources include: athlete interview, physician, athletic trainer, coach, psychologist, exercise physiologist, family members. Referral forms from other professionals provide initial information.

**Determine athlete motivation**: Self-referred athletes are typically more motivated than those referred by parents/coaches.

## Preexercise Evaluation

ACSM guidelines recommend preexercise evaluation for:
- Physically inactive individuals with known cardiovascular, metabolic, or renal disease
- Active individuals who develop symptoms of these diseases
- High school/collegiate athletes: preseason medical examination

Include results from medical history, physical examination, and laboratory data when available.

## Biochemical Assessment

Key laboratory values for athletes:
- **Iron status**: Serum ferritin, hemoglobin, hematocrit, transferrin saturation
- **Vitamin D**: 25(OH)D (calcidiol) — adequate >20-32 ng/mL depending on criteria
- **Lipid panel**: May be relevant for weight-class athletes
- **Electrolytes**: Especially sodium for heavy sweaters
- **Bone health markers**: Calcium, vitamin D, PTH

## Dietary Assessment Methods

- **24-hour recall**: Quick but subject to memory bias; multiple recalls needed for reliability
- **Food records** (3-7 days weighed or measured): More accurate but burdensome
- **Food frequency questionnaire**: Good for patterns, less for quantities
- **Digital dietary histories**: Emerging tool, especially useful for younger populations

**Common challenge**: Athletes often underestimate energy intake. Self-reported intake frequently differs substantially from estimated energy requirements.

## Energy and Nutrient Analysis

- Compare reported intake to estimated requirements based on training load
- Assess macronutrient distribution in g/kg, not just percentages
- Evaluate micronutrient adequacy, especially for at-risk nutrients
- Consider nutrient timing relative to training sessions
- Account for supplements and sports foods in total intake

## Documenting the Assessment

Use standardized NCP terminology (eNCPT) for:
- Nutrition diagnosis statements (Problem - Etiology - Signs/Symptoms)
- Intervention planning
- Monitoring and evaluation parameters

## Special Assessment Considerations

- **Weight-dependent sports**: Assess weight history, making-weight practices, body image
- **Female athlete triad**: Screen for low energy availability, menstrual dysfunction, low BMD
- **GI symptoms**: Common in endurance athletes; assess food tolerance during exercise
- **Supplement use**: Document all products, doses, frequencies, and reasons for use
