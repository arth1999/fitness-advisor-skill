# Chapter 15: Elite Athletes

## Key Concepts

Elite athletes compete at the highest levels of sport where marginal gains are meaningful. Nutritional strategies must be highly individualized, periodized, and evidence-based. The coefficient of variation in elite performance is small (~1-2%), meaning nutrition interventions producing 1-3% improvements can determine competitive outcomes.

## Characteristics of Elite Athlete Nutrition

### Periodized Nutrition
Nutrition must align with training cycles:
- **Preparatory phase**: Support training adaptations, body composition goals
- **Competition phase**: Optimize fuel stores, hydration, event-specific strategies
- **Transition/off-season**: Recovery, address deficiencies, body composition adjustment

### Energy Demands
- Extremely high during heavy training (4,000-8,000+ kcal/d for some)
- Energy availability must support training quality
- Low energy availability impairs performance, increases injury/illness risk
- Carbohydrate periodization: high availability for key sessions, "training low" selectively

### Precision Nutrition
- Individualized sweat rate testing
- Gut training for competition fueling
- Metabolic testing (RMR, substrate utilization)
- Body composition monitoring with appropriate technology

## Travel and Competition

- Jet lag management: meal timing, hydration, caffeine
- Food safety in foreign environments
- Familiar foods: plan and pack when possible
- Dining in athletes' village or competition venues
- Supplement regulations vary by country

## The Elite Athlete Entourage

Sports dietitian must collaborate with:
- Coaches (training philosophy, performance goals)
- Sports medicine physicians (medical oversight)
- Physiotherapists/athletic trainers (injury management)
- Strength and conditioning coaches (training load)
- Sports psychologists (mental performance, eating behaviors)
- Athlete's family/agents (influence on food choices)

## Professional Conduct and Ethics

- Stay within scope of practice
- Resist pressure to assist with dangerous practices (excessive weight loss, banned substances)
- Maintain confidentiality
- Professional boundaries with athletes
- Document all recommendations

## Supplements in Elite Sport

- WADA compliance is paramount
- Only Group A supplements (AIS framework) should be considered
- Third-party certification essential
- Document all supplement use
- Elite athletes are subject to whereabouts rules and out-of-competition testing

## Emerging Areas

- **Gut microbiome**: Potential for personalized nutrition
- **Nutrigenomics**: Genetic responses to nutrients
- **Sleep nutrition**: Pre-sleep protein, tart cherry juice, magnesium
- **Heat adaptation nutrition**: Fluid/electrolyte strategies during heat acclimation

## Key Takeaways

1. Nutrition must be periodized to match training phases
2. Marginal gains of 1-3% are meaningful at elite level
3. Individualization based on testing and monitoring is essential
4. Collaboration with the full performance team is critical
5. Anti-doping compliance is non-negotiable
6. Professional ethics must guide all decisions
7. Plan for travel, competition environment, and food access
