# Chapter 11: Weight Management

## Key Concepts

Weight management for athletes requires balancing performance goals with health. The process follows four steps: Assessment, Goal Setting, Action Plan, and Evaluation/Reassessment.

## Step 1: Assessment

Comprehensive data collection:
- Anthropometrics: height, weight, body composition, somatotype
- Metabolic rate: measured RMR or calculated
- Dietary intake: 3-7 day records, recall, FFQ
- Physical activity: training volume, intensity, non-exercise activity
- Psychosocial: body image, weight history, eating behaviors
- Medical: medications, injuries, menstrual history

## Step 2: Goal Setting — SMART Goals

**Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Realistic, Time-driven.**

### Weight Loss
- Reasonable goal: ~0.7% body weight loss per week
- For men: baseline intake minus 300-500 kcal/d
- For women: baseline intake minus 200-300 kcal/d
- Target body weight formula: Current weight / (1 - desired % body fat)

### Weight/Muscle Gain
- For men: baseline intake plus 400-500 kcal/d
- For women: baseline intake plus 300-400 kcal/d
- Target 0.5-0.75 lb lean mass gain per week (realistic for most)

### Simultaneous Lean Gain + Fat Loss
- For men: baseline plus ~300 kcal/d
- For women: baseline plus ~200 kcal/d
- Adjust in 50-100 kcal increments; requires close monitoring

## Step 3: Action Plan Components

**Protein**: 1.4-2.0 g/kg/d general; up to 1.7-2.0 g/kg/d for muscle gain. During energy restriction, needs may exceed 2.0 g/kg/d for LBM preservation (definitive amount unknown). 20-25g high-quality protein per meal with ≥2.2g leucine.

**Carbohydrate**: 3-12 g/kg/d based on training load, sufficient to resynthesize glycogen daily.

**Fat**: ~1 g/kg/d or 25-30% total energy. Restricting below 20% is not recommended.

**Nutrient timing**: Three meals + 2-3 snacks; pre-exercise fuel; post-exercise CHO + protein for recovery.

**High-protein diets during weight loss**: May reduce LBM loss but must maintain adequate CHO for training. Mettler et al. showed 2.3 g/kg preserved LBM over 2 weeks.

## Making Weight — Acute Strategies

- Fat restriction 2-3 days pre-weigh-in (fat is energy-dense, low residue)
- Fluid manipulation should be supervised and minimized
- Sauna/exercise-induced sweating should not exceed 2-3% body weight loss
- Rehydration and refueling plan essential for post-weigh-in recovery

## Step 4: Evaluation and Reassessment

- Track weight weekly (same day, time, conditions)
- Measure body composition every 4-8 weeks
- Monitor performance markers (strength, endurance, power)
- Adjust plan based on rate of change and performance feedback
- Reassess goals periodically; adapt to training phase

## Special Considerations

- **Weight-class sports** (wrestling, rowing, jockeys, martial arts): Higher risk of unhealthy weight practices
- **Aesthetic sports** (gymnastics, figure skating, diving): Body image concerns prevalent
- **Female athlete triad**: Always screen when weight management is discussed
- **Adolescents**: Weight management must account for growth and development; calorie restriction may impair maturation
- **Older athletes**: LBM preservation becomes harder; higher protein per meal recommended (30-40g, 0.4 g/kg)

## Practical Rule of Thumb

1 lb fat ≈ 3,500 kcal, but this static equation fails in practice due to the dynamic nature of energy balance. Actual weight change is always less than predicted and highly individual.
