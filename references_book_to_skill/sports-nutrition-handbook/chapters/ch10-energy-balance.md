# Chapter 10: Energy Balance

## Key Concepts

Energy balance = Energy Intake - Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE). A static equation fails to account for homeostatic metabolic and behavioral adjustments to perturbations in intake and expenditure. Weight loss or gain is rarely as predicted by simple caloric arithmetic.

## Components of TDEE

1. **Resting Metabolic Rate (RMR)** — 60-75% of TDEE: Energy needed at rest for vital functions. Estimated via equations (Mifflin-St Jeor, Harris-Benedict) or indirect calorimetry.
2. **Thermic Effect of Food (TEF)** — ~10% of TDEE: Energy cost of digesting, absorbing, and metabolizing nutrients. Protein has highest TEF.
3. **Physical Activity Energy Expenditure (PAEE)** — 15-30% of TDEE:
   - Exercise Expenditure (ExEE): Planned, structured activity
   - Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis (NEAT): All other movement (fidgeting, posture, walking)
4. **Physical Activity Level (PAL)** — TDEE/RMR ratio: Sedentary (1.0-1.4) to vigorously active (2.0-2.4+)

## Dynamic Nature of Energy Balance

### During Energy Deficit (Weight Loss)
- RMR decreases beyond what is explained by loss of respiring tissue (increased metabolic efficiency)
- TEF decreases (less food consumed)
- Energy cost of weight-bearing movement decreases as body mass drops
- Hunger increases, satiety decreases (the "energy gap")
- **Result**: Initial 500 kcal deficit shrinks over time; weight loss plateaus
- ~75% of weight loss is fat, ~25% lean mass

### During Energy Surplus (Weight Gain)
- RMR increases beyond body size changes (decreased metabolic efficiency)
- NEAT can increase dramatically — strongest predictor of attenuated weight gain
- Individual variability: 3-10 fold differences in weight gain with same overfeeding
- Increases in lean mass during overfeeding depend on protein intake and resistance exercise

## Exercise and Weight Loss

Exercise-only approaches typically produce modest weight loss unless large ExEE with no compensatory intake. Reasons:
- Net energy cost of exercise is lower than gross cost (replace sitting/standing)
- Some individuals increase energy intake compensatory to exercise
- NEAT may decrease in some individuals
- Sedentary person walking 40 min, 5x/week nets only ~3,800 kcal/month deficit (~1 lb/month)

## The Energy Gap After Weight Loss

Weight loss triggers an anabolic profile: reduced leptin, increased ghrelin, reduced RMR, increased metabolic efficiency. A **high flux state** (high TDEE + adequate intake via exercise) can attenuate the energy gap by improving satiety and maintaining RMR.

## Gut Microbiome and Energy Balance (Emerging)

Gut microbiota influence energy harvest from diet. Firmicutes-to-Bacteroidetes ratio differs between obese and lean individuals. Bacteria can convert indigestible carbohydrates to absorbable short-chain fatty acids, affecting net energy availability.

## When Exercise Stops

- Insulin sensitivity drops to sedentary levels within 2 days of no exercise
- Retired athletes at high risk for metabolic syndrome (30% of retired NFL players; 60% in linemen)
- Transition from high flux to low flux requires substantial reduction in energy intake
