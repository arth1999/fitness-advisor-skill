# Part E: Performance and Ethics (Chapters 33-37)

## Chapter 33: Nutrition for Performance

### Key Principles
- Individualised nutrition plans based on sport, position, training phase, goals
- Balance of macronutrients (carbohydrate, protein, fat) varies by training demands
- Timing of nutrient intake relative to training/competition critical

### Carbohydrate for Performance
- Primary fuel for high-intensity exercise
- Requirements: 3-12 g/kg/day depending on training load
- Carbohydrate loading: 7-12 g/kg/day for 24-48 hours before endurance events
- During exercise: 30-60 g/hour (events >60-90 min); up to 90 g/hour (ultra-endurance)
- Multiple transportable carbohydrates (glucose + fructose) for higher intakes

### Protein for Performance
- Requirements: 1.2-2.0 g/kg/day for athletes (higher than general population)
- Timing: 20-25 g high-quality protein every 3-4 hours
- Post-exercise: 0.25-0.3 g/kg within 2 hours (with carbohydrate for glycogen resynthesis)
- Before sleep: ~40 g casein for overnight recovery

### Hydration for Performance
- Pre-exercise: 5-7 mL/kg 2-4 hours before
- During exercise: prevent >2% body mass loss through fluid loss
- Post-exercise: 1.5 L per kg body mass lost
- Include sodium for rehydration (>1 hour exercise, hot conditions)
- Avoid over-hydration -> hyponatraemia (especially in slower, longer events)

### Supplements
- Evidence-based: caffeine (3-6 mg/kg ~60 min pre-exercise), creatine (strength/power sports), beta-alanine (high-intensity efforts 1-4 min), nitrate/beetroot juice (endurance), bicarbonate (high-intensity efforts)
- Limited evidence: most other supplements
- Risk of contamination with banned substances: use batch-tested products
- "Food first" approach

## Chapter 34: Drugs and the Athlete

### Anti-Doping Framework
- World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA) Code: global standard
- Prohibited List updated annually
- Strict liability: athlete responsible for any substance found in their body
- Categories: prohibited at all times, prohibited in-competition only, prohibited in particular sports

### Key Prohibited Substances Categories
- Anabolic agents (anabolic androgenic steroids)
- Peptide hormones, growth factors, related substances and mimetics (EPO, hGH, IGF-1)
- Beta-2 agonists (certain; salbutamol, formoterol, salmeterol permitted within dosing limits)
- Hormone and metabolic modulators (aromatase inhibitors, tamoxifen, insulin)
- Diuretics and masking agents
- Stimulants (in-competition)
- Narcotics (in-competition)
- Cannabinoids (in-competition)
- Glucocorticoids (in-competition; by certain routes)

### Therapeutic Use Exemption (TUE)
- Athletes with legitimate medical conditions may apply for TUE to use otherwise prohibited substances
- Criteria: significant health impairment without treatment, no performance enhancement beyond return to normal health, no reasonable therapeutic alternative, necessity not consequence of prior non-therapeutic use

### Clinical Responsibilities
- Check all medications against WADA Prohibited List before prescribing to competing athletes
- Document all treatments
- Advise athletes about supplement contamination risks
- Support clean sport values
- Recognise and manage health consequences of doping

### Health Consequences of Doping
- Anabolic steroids: cardiovascular disease, hepatotoxicity, endocrine disruption (hypogonadism, infertility), psychiatric effects
- EPO/blood doping: thrombosis, hypertension, autoimmune complications
- Stimulants: cardiovascular events, psychiatric effects, addiction
- hGH: acromegaly, cardiomyopathy, diabetes

## Chapter 35: Genetics in Exercise and Sport

### Key Concepts
- Most performance-related traits are polygenic (multiple genes with small effects)
- Current knowledge: >200 genetic variants associated with athletic performance
- Best-studied genes: ACTN3 (speed/power), ACE (endurance)
- Genetic testing: limited predictive value for talent identification or training response at present
- Ethical concerns: genetic discrimination, privacy, psychological impact on children

### Clinical Implications
- Genetic testing not recommended for talent identification
- May have future role in personalised training prescription and injury risk prediction
- Family history remains best current "genetic test" for many conditions
- Pre-participation screening for genetic cardiovascular conditions (HCM, LQTS, Marfan)

## Chapter 36: Medico-Legal Issues

### Key Areas in Sports Medicine
- Duty of care to athletes
- Informed consent for treatments and procedures
- Confidentiality (particularly in team settings)
- Documentation standards
- Pre-participation evaluation and clearance decisions
- Return-to-play decision making (shared decision-making; avoid conflicts of interest)
- Scope of practice (work within competence)
- Medical records ownership and access
- Travel with teams: medical licensing across jurisdictions
- Insurance considerations

### Clinical Risk Management
- Clear, contemporaneous documentation
- Informed consent for all procedures
- Shared decision-making for return to play
- Communication with other treating practitioners
- Follow established guidelines and protocols
- Maintain professional indemnity insurance
- Know legal obligations in your jurisdiction

## Chapter 37: Harassment and Abuse

### Scope of the Problem
- Harassment and abuse occur across all sports, all levels, all countries
- Forms: psychological, physical, sexual, neglect
- Athlete vulnerability factors: age (children/adolescents at highest risk), gender, disability, elite level dependency on coach/system

### Prevention
- Organisational policies and codes of conduct
- Safe recruitment practices (background checks)
- Education for athletes, coaches, parents, administrators
- Multiple reporting pathways
- Clear investigation and disciplinary procedures
- Athlete empowerment and voice in decision-making

### Clinical Role
- Be aware of signs and symptoms of abuse
- Create safe environment for disclosure
- Know reporting obligations in your jurisdiction
- Provide trauma-informed care
- Support athlete psychological recovery
- Refer to appropriate services (child protection, police, psychological support)
