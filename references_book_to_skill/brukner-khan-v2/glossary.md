# Glossary of Key Terms — Brukner & Khan Vol 2

## A
**Aerobic capacity (VO2max/VO2peak)**: Maximum rate of oxygen consumption during incremental exercise. Measured in L/min or mL/kg/min. Indicator of cardiovascular fitness.
**Anaerobic capacity**: Ability to generate maximal power over seconds and sustain it for short periods. Influenced by muscle size, enzymatic activity, glycogen stores.
**Ankylosing spondylitis (AS)**: Inflammatory arthritis primarily affecting the spine and sacroiliac joints. Male:female ratio 3:1. Common in young adults (20s).
**Arrhythmogenic cardiomyopathy (AC)**: Familial heart muscle disorder with fibro-fatty replacement of myocardium. Leading cause of SCD in Italian athletes.
**Autonomic neuropathy**: Diabetes complication affecting autonomic nervous system function; impairs HR, BP, and thermoregulation responses to exercise.

## B
**Basal metabolic rate (BMR)**: Energy expended at rest for basic physiological functions. Accounts for >60% of total energy expenditure. Decreases with caloric restriction.
**Bioelectrical impedance analysis (BIA)**: Method of estimating body composition by measuring resistance to a light electrical current.
**Body mass index (BMI)**: Weight (kg) / height^2 (m). Categories: <18.5 underweight; 18.5-24.9 normal; 25-29.9 overweight; 30-34.9 obesity I; 35-39.9 obesity II; >40 extreme obesity III.
**Borg scale**: Rating of Perceived Exertion (RPE) from 6 (no effort) to 20 (maximal exertion). Moderate exercise = 12-14.
**Brugada syndrome**: Inherited ion channel disorder causing abnormal ECG pattern and risk of ventricular arrhythmias. Cause of SADS.

## C
**Catecholaminergic polymorphic ventricular tachycardia (CPVT)**: Inherited arrhythmia disorder triggered by emotional stress or exercise. Presents with syncope.
**Change talk**: Patient statements indicating desire, reason, ability, need, or commitment to behaviour change (motivational interviewing concept).
**Congenital coronary artery anomalies (CCAA)**: Abnormal origin, course, or destination of coronary arteries. Can cause SCD during exercise via mechanical compression.
**C-peptide**: Marker of endogenous insulin production. Helps distinguish type 1 from type 2 diabetes.
**Cystic fibrosis (CF)**: Autosomal recessive disorder affecting lungs and other organs. Exercise improves aerobic capacity, which is an independent predictor of survival. Increased salt loss requires electrolyte replacement.

## D
**DALY (Disability-Adjusted Life Years)**: One lost year of healthy life. Measures disease burden as gap between current health and ideal health.
**Dilated cardiomyopathy (DCM)**: Left ventricular dilatation with impaired systolic function. Rare cause of SCD (4% of cases). Contraindicates competitive sports except class 1A.
**Dual-energy X-ray absorptiometry (DXA)**: Most accurate method for measuring body composition (bone density, fat mass, muscle mass).
**Duloxetine**: SNRI medication effective for chronic OA pain through central analgesic effects independent of mood improvement.

## E
**Energy balance equation**: Calories in = calories out. Energy in (food) - energy out (BMR + physical activity + thermic effect). Oversimplified; hormonal responses to caloric restriction counteract sustained weight loss.
**Enthesitis**: Inflammation of tendon/ligament insertion sites. Common in spondyloarthropathies. Key differential for sports injury.
**Exercise-induced bronchospasm (EIB)**: Airway narrowing triggered by exercise. >=10% fall in FEV1 post-exercise. Differentiate from vocal cord dysfunction.
**Exercise Rx**: Written exercise prescription modelled on drug prescription with type, dose, frequency, duration, therapeutic goal.

## F
**FITT-VP**: Framework for exercise prescription: Frequency, Intensity, Time, Type, Volume, Progression.
**Familial hypercholesterolaemia**: Genetic disorder causing premature coronary artery disease. Prevalence 1:250. Look for xanthelasma, corneal arcus, xanthomata.

## G
**Green Prescription (Green Rx)**: New Zealand national initiative (since 1998) where clinicians provide written exercise prescriptions with telephone/group follow-up support for up to 6 months.
**Gout**: Monosodium urate crystal deposition causing acute monoarthritis. Affects mainly males and postmenopausal females. Common at great toe and wrist.

## H
**HbA1c**: Glycosylated haemoglobin. Reflects average blood glucose over preceding 2-3 months. Diagnostic threshold: >=6.5%. Target for diabetic athletes: <=7% (adults), <=7.5% (adolescents).
**High-intensity interval training (HIIT)**: Alternating short, intense bursts (70-95% HRmax) with recovery periods (30-50% HRmax). Improves VO2max more than endurance training. Safe for cardiac rehabilitation with appropriate screening.
**Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM)**: Primary myocardial disorder with left ventricular hypertrophy. Prevalence 0.2% general, 0.06-0.07% in athletes. Most common cause of SCD in young US athletes. Diagnosed by echocardiography/CMR (wall thickness >=13mm women, >=15mm men).
**Hypoglycaemia (late-onset post-exercise, LOPEH)**: Blood glucose drop occurring 4-24 hours after exercise in diabetics. Preventable with post-exercise carbohydrate intake (1.5 g/kg within 30 min, repeat at 1-2 hours).

## I
**Idiopathic left ventricular hypertrophy (LVH)**: Increased LV mass >500g without myocyte disarray. More common in athletes of African/Afro-Caribbean origin.
**Insulin resistance**: Reduced sensitivity to insulin action in muscle, liver, and adipose tissue. Both cause and effect of obesity. Drives type 2 diabetes.
**Integrative neuromuscular training**: General (strength-building) and specific (motor control deficit-targeted) conditioning activities to enhance health- and skill-related fitness.

## J
**Joint hypermobility syndrome (JHS)**: Generalised joint hypermobility (Beighton >=4/9) plus joint pain in >=4 joints for >3 months. Common in adolescent females. Increased knee injury risk 5x in contact sports.

## L
**Late-onset post-exercise hypoglycaemia (LOPEH)**: See Hypoglycaemia.
**Long QT syndrome (LQTS)**: Inherited ion channel disorder causing prolonged QT interval. Types: LQT1 (exercise/swimming trigger), LQT2 (stress/noise trigger), LQT3 (rest/sleep trigger).

## M
**Marfan syndrome**: Collagen disorder (fibrillin mutation) causing aortic dilatation, skeletal and ocular abnormalities. Prevalence 1:5000. Contraindicates high-static sports. Annual aortic surveillance.
**McArdle disease (GSD V)**: Autosomal recessive myophosphorylase deficiency. Presents in first 2 decades with exercise intolerance and muscle pain. Characteristic "second-wind" phenomenon at 6-10 min of exercise.
**MET (Metabolic Equivalent)**: Energy cost of sitting quietly = 1 kcal/kg/hour. Light PA: 1.6-3 METs; Moderate: 3-6 METs; Vigorous: >6 METs.
**Metabolic obesity**: Visceral fat accumulation in lean or obese individuals. Better predictor of cardiovascular risk than BMI alone.
**Mitochondrial myopathies (MM)**: Mutations in nuclear/mitochondrial DNA causing reduced oxidative capacity. Exercise testing shows low VO2peak, high RER, elevated lactate. Aerobic + resistance training may induce mitochondrial proliferation and gene shifting.
**Motivational interviewing (MI)**: Patient-centric counselling to resolve ambivalence about change. Non-judgmental, non-confrontational. Core principles: express empathy, support self-efficacy, develop discrepancy, roll with resistance.

## N
**Neurally mediated syncope (vasovagal)**: Most common cause of syncope in young athletes. Triggered by fear, pain, prolonged standing, post-exercise. Premonitory symptoms present. Benign but must be distinguished from cardiac syncope.
**Number needed to treat (NNT)**: For physical activity promotion: NNT = 12 (one additional sedentary adult meeting activity guidelines at 12 months). For tobacco cessation advice: NNT = 50-120.

## O
**OARS**: Open-ended questions, Affirmations, Reflections, Summaries — fundamental skills of motivational interviewing.
**Obesogenic environment**: Environment that promotes weight gain and inhibits weight loss. Includes food environment, built environment, socioeconomic factors, and food marketing.
**Osteoarthritis (OA)**: Heterogeneous disorder affecting entire joint. Defined by OARSI as cell stress and ECM degradation from micro/macro-injury activating maladaptive repair. Risk factors: obesity (2.6x), knee trauma (3.9x), female gender, age.

## P
**Peripheral neuropathy**: Diabetes complication causing symmetric loss of sensation beginning distally. Requires non-weight-bearing exercise if feet are insensate. Regular podiatric review essential.
**Physical Activity on Prescription (PAP/FaR)**: Swedish national program. Written prescriptions based on FYSS book. Five cornerstones. Adherence comparable to long-term drug treatments.
**Physical Activity Vital Sign**: Two-question screening: (1) days/week of moderate-strenuous exercise; (2) minutes/day at that level. Use at every clinical encounter.
**Point-of-decision prompts**: Motivational signs near stairwells/elevators encouraging stair use. Effective, evidence-based nudge for increasing physical activity.
**Post-exertional syncope**: Neurally mediated syncope occurring after exercise cessation (standing in huddle, at finish line). Usually benign. Caused by reduced venous return after muscle pump ceases.
**Proliferative retinopathy**: Diabetes complication. Contraindicates exercise increasing systolic BP >170 mmHg and prolonged Valsalva. Risk of retinal haemorrhage with weightlifting.

## R
**Rating of perceived exertion (RPE)**: See Borg scale.
**Rheumatoid arthritis (RA)**: Chronic inflammatory arthritis. Female:male ratio 3:1. Onset typically 30-60 years. Affects small hand/foot joints with systemic features.

## S
**Second-wind phenomenon**: Characteristic of McArdle disease. Reduction in pain, HR, and RPE at 6-10 minutes of fixed-workload exercise due to switch to extramuscular fuel sources.
**Septic arthritis**: Direct bacterial joint invasion requiring urgent diagnosis. Most common organism: Neisseria gonorrhoea in young adults. Knee and hip most vulnerable.
**SMART goals**: Specific, Measurable, Achievable/Attainable, Relevant, Time-based — framework for setting physical activity goals.
**Sudden arrhythmic death syndrome (SADS)**: Autopsy-negative sudden unexplained death. Accounts for up to 40% of SCD in young athletes. Often due to ion channelopathies or accessory pathways. 50% of families later diagnosed with inherited condition.
**Sudden cardiac death (SCD)**: Leading cause of mortality in young athletes during exercise. Incidence best estimate: 1:40,000 to 1:80,000. Males 5-9x more than females. African-American athletes 3.2x greater risk than white.

## T
**Talk test**: Simple intensity measure. Moderate intensity = can talk comfortably. Vigorous intensity = breathing heavily, cannot talk comfortably.
**Transtheoretical stages of change**: Precontemplation -> Contemplation -> Preparation -> Action -> Maintenance (-> Relapse). Used to assess readiness for physical activity change.

## U
**Ultra-processed foods**: Products with manufactured ingredients not used in home cooking (artificial flavours, colours, sweeteners, preservatives). >50% of US caloric intake. Associated with obesity epidemic.

## V
**Visceral adipose tissue (VAT)**: Fat wrapped around organs. Produces pro-inflammatory cytokines (TNF-alpha, IL-6), reduces adiponectin. Strongly associated with type 2 diabetes and coronary artery disease. Reduced by exercise independent of weight loss.
**Vocal cord dysfunction (VCD)**: Paradoxical vocal cord adduction during inspiration. Presents in high-achieving female adolescent athletes. Often misdiagnosed as asthma. Diagnosis confirmed by flexible laryngoscopy with exercise. Management: breathing exercises, treat GORD/anxiety.

## W
**Waist circumference thresholds**: Increased disease risk: female >=80 cm, male >=94 cm. High disease risk: female >=88 cm, male >=102 cm.
**Waist-to-height (WH) ratio**: Predictor of obesity: >0.53 men, >0.54 women. More accurate than BMI for body fat percentage.
**Waist-to-hip ratio (WHR)**: Excess abdominal fat if >0.8 for women, >0.9 for men.

## Numeric
**5As model**: Assess, Advise, Agree, Assist, Arrange — behaviour change counselling framework.
**10 000 steps/day**: Benchmark for daily physical activity. Translates to ~70-80 min walking. >=7000 steps/day associated with health benefits. >=15,000 steps/day associated with zero cardiovascular risk factors.
**150 minutes**: WHO minimum moderate-intensity aerobic activity per week (or 75 min vigorous).
