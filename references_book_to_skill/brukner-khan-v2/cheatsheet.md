## EXERCISE PRESCRIPTION QUICK REFERENCE

### FITT-VP Template
| Component | Sedentary Start | Moderate Goal | Optimal |
|-----------|----------------|---------------|---------|
| Frequency | 3 days/week | 5 days/week | 5-7 days/week |
| Intensity | Light (Borg 9-11) | Moderate (Borg 12-14) | Mixed (incl. vigorous) |
| Time | 10-15 min | 30-60 min | 45-90 min |
| Type | Walking | Aerobic + Resistance | All 4 types |
| Volume | 150-300 MET-min/wk | 500-1000 MET-min/wk | 3000-4000 MET-min/wk |
| Progression | +5-10 min/1-2 wk | +frequency then +intensity | Periodized |

### Intensity Zones Quick Guide
| Zone | %HRmax | Borg (6-20) | Talk Test | METs | Example |
|------|--------|-------------|-----------|------|---------|
| Light | <55% | 9-11 | Can sing | 1.6-3.0 | Slow walk |
| Moderate | 55-70% | 12-14 | Can talk, not sing | 3.0-6.0 | Brisk walk |
| Vigorous | 70-90% | 15-17 | Can't talk | >6.0 | Jogging/run |
| Maximal | 90-100% | 18-20 | Gasp | — | Sprint |

### HRmax Estimation
- Traditional: HRmax = 220 - age
- More accurate: HRmax = 208 - (0.7 x age)
- Training range: 50-85% HRmax

### Step Count Guide
- <5000 steps/day: Sedentary
- 5000-7499: Low active
- 7500-9999: Somewhat active
- >=10,000: Active
- >=15,000: Zero CVD risk factors observed

## CONDITION-SPECIFIC EXERCISE PRESCRIPTION

### Obesity
| Parameter | Prescription |
|-----------|-------------|
| Key message | "You cannot outrun a bad diet" — exercise maintains loss, nutrition drives loss |
| Aerobic | 150-300 min/week moderate; goal: up to 300+ min for weight maintenance |
| Resistance | 2-3x/week, all major muscle groups |
| Nutrition | Reduce ultra-processed foods and SSBs; focus on food quality |
| Monitoring | Weight, waist circumference, WH ratio |

### Type 2 Diabetes
| Parameter | Prescription |
|-----------|-------------|
| Aerobic | 150 min/week moderate OR 75 min/week vigorous/HIIT (spread >=3 days) |
| Resistance | 2-3x/week non-consecutive days; 1-3 sets, 8-12 reps |
| Balance | 2-3x/week (especially older adults) |
| Pre-exercise BG | 5.5-13.9 mmol/L (100-250 mg/dL) = safe to exercise |
| BG <5.5 | Consume 10-30g CHO before exercise |
| BG >13.9 + ketones | Postpone, take insulin |
| BG >16.5 | Postpone regardless of ketones |
| Use RPE not HR | If autonomic neuropathy present |

### Type 1 Diabetes
| Activity | Pre-exercise insulin adjustment | CHO intake |
|----------|-------------------------------|------------|
| Low intensity (walking, golf) 20-30 min | Minimal to none | 15g CHO before if BG <5.5 |
| Moderate <1hr (jog, swim, cycle) | -25% pre-exercise insulin | 15-30g before, every 30 min |
| Strenuous >1hr (marathon, triathlon) | -30-80% pre-exercise insulin | 30g (100-120 cal) every 30 min |
| Pump therapy | -50% basal rate 1hr before high-intensity | Individualized |
| Post-exercise | — | 1.5g CHO/kg within 30 min; repeat at 1-2hr |

### Osteoarthritis
| Joint | Recommended Exercise | Effect |
|-------|---------------------|--------|
| Hip OA | Land-based (combined), aquatic, Nordic walking | Small-moderate pain/function improvement |
| Knee OA | Land-based (any type), tai chi, cycling, yoga, aquatic | Pain reduction 2-6 months post-program |
| All | Weight loss (5.1kg over 10yr = >50% OA odds reduction) | 10% weight loss = 50% pain improvement |
| Biologic agents | PRP promising but low-quality evidence | Stem cells: not yet proven safe/effective |
| Discourage | Glucosamine, opioids, repeated IA steroids, HA injections | — |

### Osteoporosis / Bone Health
| Parameter | Prescription |
|-----------|-------------|
| Type | Weight-bearing aerobic + progressive resistance |
| Frequency | Most days (aerobic); 2-3x/week (resistance) |
| Intensity | Moderate to high (as tolerated) |
| Balance training | 2-3x/week for falls prevention in older adults |
| Caution | Avoid forward flexion + rotation (vertebral # risk) |

### Depression
| Parameter | Prescription |
|-----------|-------------|
| Aerobic | 150 min/week moderate minimum |
| Evidence | Comparable to pharmacotherapy for mild-moderate depression |
| Resistance | 2x/week as adjunct |
| Group exercise | Added benefit from social interaction |

### Cancer (During & After Treatment)
| Parameter | Prescription |
|-----------|-------------|
| Aerobic | Moderate (Borg 11-14), 5-7 days/week, 20-30 min |
| Resistance | 1-3 sessions/week, 1-4 sets, 6-12 reps to fatigue |
| Modifications | Split into shorter sessions during chemo; check WCC/platelets |
| Benefits | Reduce cancer-related fatigue; improve fitness, strength, QoL |

### Cardiovascular Disease
| Parameter | Stable CAD | Heart Failure |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|
| Aerobic | 150 min/week moderate | 150 min/week moderate (stable) |
| HIIT | Safe post-stress test; may be superior | Effective but requires stress testing |
| Resistance | 2x/week, moderate | Light-moderate, symptom-limited |
| Contraindications | Unstable angina, uncontrolled arrhythmia, severe AS | Decompensated HF |

### Hypertension
| Classification | SBP (mmHg) | DBP (mmHg) |
|---------------|------------|------------|
| Normal | <120 | <80 |
| Elevated | 120-129 | <80 |
| Stage 1 | 130-139 | 80-89 |
| Stage 2 | >=140 | >=90 |
| First-line meds for athletes | ACE inhibitors or ARBs (no aerobic capacity reduction) |
| Avoid | Beta blockers and diuretics (reduce exercise capacity) |

### Children with Chronic Disease
| Condition | Exercise Recommendation |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Type 1 diabetes | Moderate aerobic + resistance 2x/week; insulin adjustment needed |
| Childhood cancer | Aerobic 5-7 days/week + resistance 1-3 sessions/week |
| Cystic fibrosis | Combined aerobic + resistance; replace electrolytes in heat |
| Haemophilia | Regular activity encouraged; prophylactic clotting factor before contact |
| Mitochondrial myopathy | Aerobic + resistance 3x/week, 60 min |
| McArdle disease | Low-mod aerobic 5x/week, 30-60 min; avoid plyometrics and high-intensity |
| Juvenile idiopathic arthritis | Combined anaerobic + aerobic + resistance; does not exacerbate arthritis |
| Joint hypermobility | Aerobic + resistance; contact sports not contraindicated |

## BEHAVIOUR CHANGE QUICK REFERENCE

### 5As Summary
| Step | Action | Time Needed |
|------|--------|------------|
| Assess | Physical Activity Vital Sign + readiness | 1-2 min |
| Advise | "150 min moderate/week = 22 min walking/day" | 1-2 min |
| Agree | SMART goal setting | 2-5 min |
| Assist | Written Rx + barriers + resources | 2-5 min |
| Arrange | Follow-up in 3-6 months | 1 min |

### 30-Second Brief Advice (for time-limited encounters)
1. "On average, how many days/week do you exercise? How many minutes?" (Assess)
2. "Even 22 minutes of walking daily cuts mortality risk 15-40%." (Advise)
3. "Would you like to discuss how to get started?" (Agree — open door)

### Stages of Change — Clinician's Task
| Stage | Patient Says | Clinician Does |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| Precontemplation | "I don't exercise and don't plan to" | Brief advice, plant seeds |
| Contemplation | "I should probably exercise more" | Explore ambivalence |
| Preparation | "I want to start walking next week" | SMART goals, Rx |
| Action | "I've been walking 3x/week" | Affirm, adjust, support |
| Maintenance | "Been exercising regularly for 6 months" | Relapse prevention |
| Relapse | "I stopped exercising last month" | Reframe as learning, recommence contemplation |

### Motivational Interviewing Micro-Skills (OARS)
| Skill | Example |
|-------|---------|
| Open-ended question | "Tell me about your experience with exercise." |
| Affirmation | "I think it's great you came in to discuss this." |
| Reflection | "So exercising in the past left you feeling breathless and discouraged." |
| Summary | "Let me check I've understood everything so far..." |

### Barriers — Common Responses
| Barrier | Response |
|---------|----------|
| "No time" | "10-min walks add up. Active travel. Schedule it." |
| "Bad weather" | "Waterproof jacket + shoes. Or indoor options." |
| "Embarrassed" | "Comfortable clothing. Classes with people like you." |
| "Sore afterwards" | "Temporary. Don't do too much too soon." |
| "Too expensive" | "Walking is free. No equipment needed." |

## RED FLAGS — STOP EXERCISE AND SEEK MEDICAL REVIEW

### Cardiac
- Chest pain/pressure/discomfort during exercise (makes athlete stop)
- Syncope or near-syncope DURING exercise (not post-exercise)
- Palpitations with dizziness/lightheadedness
- Sudden onset severe dyspnoea
- Known HCM, ARVC, LQTS, CPVT, CCAA, severe AS

### Medical
- BG >13.9 mmol/L + ketones (diabetes)
- BG <5.5 mmol/L without CHO supplementation (diabetes)
- Acute febrile illness
- Acute joint flare (inflammatory arthritis)
- New neurological symptoms during exercise
- Signs of heat stroke (core temp >40°C + neurocognitive changes)

### Musculoskeletal
- Acute joint swelling with no trauma (rule out septic arthritis)
- Joint locking or giving way
- Severe pain disproportionate to activity
- Suspected stress fracture

## PRE-PARTICIPATION SCREENING QUICK LIST

Ask the patient (CSEP Get Active Questionnaire — adapted):

In the past 6 months, have you experienced:
1. Chest pain/discomfort during daily activities or exercise?
2. High BP diagnosis or resting BP >=160/90?
3. Dizziness or lightheadedness during physical activity?
4. Shortness of breath at rest?
5. Loss of consciousness / fainting?
6. Concussion?
7. Current pain or swelling affecting physical activity?
8. Has a healthcare provider told you to avoid/modify certain activities?
9. Any medical condition affecting physical activity (diabetes, cancer, osteoporosis, asthma, spinal cord injury)?

**YES to any question → Medical evaluation before starting/increasing exercise.**
**ALL NO → Safe to begin gradual increase in physical activity.**

## NUTRITION QUICK REFERENCE

### For Health (Chapter 6)
- Reduce ultra-processed foods (>50% of calories in Western diets)
- Reduce sugar-sweetened beverages (strongest link to obesity/weight gain)
- Reduce omega-6 to omega-3 ratio (target ~4:1; current typical ~16:1)
- Saturated fat restriction lacks original scientific basis per re-examination of evidence
- Focus on real, whole foods

### For Diabetes
| Timing | Action |
|--------|--------|
| 1-2 hr pre-exercise | Eat meal to ensure ideal glucose levels |
| Pre-exercise (BG <5.5) | 10-30g CHO (depending on intensity) |
| During exercise (>30 min) | 0.5-1 g CHO/kg/hour |
| Post-exercise (within 30 min) | 1.5 g CHO/kg |
| Post-exercise (1-2 hr later) | Additional 1.5 g CHO/kg (prevent LOPEH) |
| Pre-bed after exercise | Consider pre-bed snack + caffeine pre-exercise (T1DM) |
