# SKILL: Brukner & Khan's Clinical Sports Medicine Vol 2

## Source
Brukner & Khan's Clinical Sports Medicine, Volume 2: The Medicine of Exercise, 5th Edition (2019)
Authors: Peter Brukner, Karim Khan

## Purpose
This skill provides evidence-based clinical knowledge on exercise medicine, rehabilitation protocols, chronic disease management through exercise, and special population considerations. It is the reference for prescribing physical activity as medicine.

## When to Use This Skill
- Prescribing exercise for patients with medical conditions (obesity, diabetes, cardiovascular disease, osteoarthritis, osteoporosis, cancer, depression, anxiety)
- Designing rehabilitation protocols and exercise prescriptions
- Managing exercise in special populations (children, adolescents, females, older people, people with disabilities)
- Understanding environmental considerations for exercise (heat, cold, altitude, underwater)
- Applying behaviour change techniques (motivational interviewing, 5As model)
- Evaluating cardiovascular risk for exercise participation
- Providing nutritional guidance for health and disease management

## Architecture
This book is organized into six parts spanning 40 chapters:

### Part A: Exercise and Health (Chapters 1-6)
Foundation of exercise as medicine. Covers the physical inactivity pandemic, physiological benefits of physical activity, clinical assessment and written exercise prescription (FITT-VP), motivational interviewing, and nutrition for health.

### Part B: Managing Medical Problems (Chapters 7-22)
Exercise prescription for specific medical conditions. Each chapter covers pathophysiology, exercise benefits, contraindications, and prescription details.

### Part C: Environment (Chapters 23-27)
How environmental factors affect exercise: heat illness, cold injury, altitude physiology, underwater medicine, and the built environment.

### Part D: Specific Groups (Chapters 28-32)
Special populations: children and adolescents, female-specific considerations, transgender and intersex athletes, older people, and persons with disability.

### Part E: Performance and Ethics (Chapters 33-37)
Nutrition for performance, drugs in sport, genetics, medico-legal issues, harassment and abuse.

### Part F: Practical Sports Medicine (Chapters 38-40)
Emergencies, medical coverage of endurance events, and multisport endurance events.

## Core Methodologies

### Exercise Prescription: FITT-VP Framework
- **F**requency: sessions per week
- **I**ntensity: RPE (Borg 6-20), %HRmax, METs, talk test
- **T**ime: duration per session
- **T**ype: aerobic, resistance, flexibility, balance
- **V**olume: MET-minutes per week (target 500-1000; health gains at 3000-4000)
- **P**rogression: increase duration first, then frequency, then intensity (5-10 min every 1-2 weeks)

### Behaviour Change: 5As Model
1. **Assess**: current activity level, readiness, contraindications
2. **Advise**: personalized benefits, guidelines (150 min moderate or 75 min vigorous/week)
3. **Agree**: SMART goals, patient-driven activities
4. **Assist**: written prescription, barrier management, resources
5. **Arrange**: follow-up every 3-6 months, referrals

### Motivational Interviewing: OARS
- **O**pen-ended questions
- **A**ffirmations
- **R**eflections (active listening)
- **S**ummaries

### WHO Physical Activity Guidelines (Adults 18+)
- 150 min moderate-intensity OR 75 min vigorous-intensity aerobic activity/week
- Muscle-strengthening activities >= 2 days/week
- Adults 65+ with poor mobility: balance training >= 3 days/week
- Minimize sedentary behaviour

### Pre-Exercise Screening: CSEP Get Active Questionnaire
Key red flags: chest pain/discomfort, high BP (>160/90), dizziness during activity, shortness of breath at rest, loss of consciousness, concussion, current pain/swelling affecting activity.
