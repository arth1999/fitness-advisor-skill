# Clinical Patterns & Decision Protocols — Brukner & Khan Vol 2

## Pattern 1: Physical Activity Counselling Flow

```
CLINICAL ENCOUNTER
    |
    v
ASSESS (Physical Activity Vital Sign)
    |-- Days/week of moderate-strenuous exercise?
    |-- Minutes/session?
    |
    v
CLASSIFY ACTIVITY LEVEL
    |-- Meets guidelines (>=150 min moderate or >=75 min vigorous/week)
    |       --> Reinforce, encourage progression beyond minimum
    |-- Below guidelines
    |       --> Proceed with 5As
    |-- Completely sedentary
    |       --> Start low, go slow. Walking prescription.
    |
    v
CHECK READINESS (Stages of Change)
    |-- Precontemplation --> Brief advice, plant seeds, revisit
    |-- Contemplation --> Explore ambivalence, decisional balance
    |-- Preparation --> SMART goals, written Rx
    |-- Action --> Affirm, monitor, adjust
    |-- Maintenance --> Relapse prevention strategies
    |
    v
SCREEN FOR RED FLAGS (CSEP Get Active Questionnaire)
    |-- YES to any --> Medical evaluation before exercise
    |-- All NO --> Safe to prescribe
    |
    v
WRITE PRESCRIPTION (FITT-VP)
    |-- Type: patient-preferred activity
    |-- Time: start 10-15 min, build to 30-60 min
    |-- Intensity: talk test or Borg 11-14 (moderate)
    |-- Frequency: 3-5x/week minimum
    |-- Progression: +5-10 min every 1-2 weeks
    |
    v
ARRANGE FOLLOW-UP (3-6 months)
    |-- Reassess activity level
    |-- Adjust prescription
    |-- Address barriers
    |-- Refer if needed (physio/OT/dietitian/cardiology)
```

## Pattern 2: Cardiovascular Symptom Decision Tree

```
ATHLETE PRESENTS WITH:
    |
    |-- EXERTIONAL SYNCOPE (during exercise)
    |       --> RED FLAG. Comprehensive cardiac evaluation.
    |       --> ECG + Echo + Stress ECG + consider CMR/CT
    |       --> Refer to cardiologist
    |       --> Rule out: HCM, CCAA, ARVC, LQTS, CPVT, aortic stenosis
    |
    |-- POST-EXERTIONAL SYNCOPE (after exercise, standing)
    |       --> Usually benign (neurally mediated)
    |       --> ECG to confirm normal
    |       --> Reassure if classic prodromal symptoms + normal ECG
    |
    |-- NON-EXERTIONAL SYNCOPE
    |       --> ECG
    |       --> If normal + classic history --> neurally mediated
    |       --> If abnormal or atypical --> refer cardiology
    |
    |-- EXERTIONAL CHEST PAIN (makes athlete stop)
    |       --> ECG + Echo + consider CMR/CT coronaries
    |       --> Young (<35): rule out CCAA, HCM
    |       --> Older (>35): stress test for ischaemic heart disease
    |
    |-- PALPITATIONS
    |       |-- Benign (barely noticeable, no associated symptoms)
    |       |       --> Reassure, no investigation needed
    |       |-- Sudden onset OR with lightheadedness/chest pain/dyspnoea
    |       |       --> ECG + Echo + ambulatory monitoring + stress ECG
    |       |       --> Refer cardiology if SVT/VT captured
    |
    |-- EXCESSIVE FATIGUE/DYSPNOEA
            --> ECG + Echo + labs (anaemia, thyroid)
            --> Consider stress ECG, CPET, PFT
            --> Recent viral illness? Rule out myocarditis
```

## Pattern 3: Diabetes Exercise Prescription Algorithm

```
DIABETIC PATIENT
    |
    v
PRE-EXERCISE SCREENING
    |-- Check BG before every session
    |-- Cardiac screen: ECG, BP (including postural drop), peripheral pulses
    |-- Eye exam: retinopathy status
    |-- Foot exam: peripheral neuropathy, ulcers
    |-- Renal: albuminuria status
    |
    v
BG LEVEL DECISION (<5.5 mmol/L = <100 mg/dL)
    |-- BG <5.5: consume 10-30g CHO before exercise (depends on intensity)
    |-- BG 5.5-10 (100-180): safe to exercise
    |-- BG 10-13.9 (180-250): safe, no extra CHO needed
    |-- BG >13.9 (>250) WITH ketones: postpone, take insulin
    |-- BG >16.5 (>300): postpone regardless of ketones
    |
    v
INSULIN ADJUSTMENT (Type 1)
    |-- Low intensity (walking, golf): minimal adjustment
    |-- Moderate <1hr (jog, swim, cycle): -25% pre-exercise insulin
    |-- Strenuous >1hr (marathon, triathlon): -30-80% pre-exercise insulin
    |-- Pump therapy: -50% basal rate 1hr before high-intensity
    |
    v
EXERCISE PRESCRIPTION
    |-- Aerobic: 150 min/week moderate OR 75 min/week vigorous/HIIT
    |-- Resistance: 2-3x/week, non-consecutive days
    |       |-- Start: 1 set, 10-12 reps, moderate weight
    |       |-- Progress: 2 sets -> 3 sets, 8-10 reps, heavier
    |       |-- Target all major muscle groups
    |-- Balance: 2-3x/week (especially older adults, neuropathy)
    |-- Flexibility: 2-3x/week
    |
    v
COMPLICATION-SPECIFIC MODIFICATIONS
    |-- Autonomic neuropathy: avoid high intensity, rapid postural changes, extreme temps
    |       --> Preferred: water activities, stationary cycling
    |-- Peripheral neuropathy: non-weight-bearing exercise
    |       --> Preferred: swimming, cycling, arm exercises
    |       --> Avoid: trauma to feet
    |-- Retinopathy: avoid BP >170 systolic, Valsalva, head jarring
    |       --> Preferred: walking, stationary cycling, swimming
    |-- Nephropathy: light-moderate only
    |       --> Avoid: heavy lifting, high-intensity aerobic
    |
    v
POST-EXERCISE MONITORING
    |-- Check BG immediately after
    |-- Check BG 4-6 hours after (LOPEH risk)
    |-- Consume 1.5 g CHO/kg within 30 min post-exhaustive exercise
    |-- Additional 1.5 g CHO/kg 1-2 hours later
```

## Pattern 4: Exercise Progression Protocol

```
INITIAL ASSESSMENT
    |-- Current activity level
    |-- Medical readiness (CSEP questionnaire)
    |-- Patient goals and preferences
    |-- Barriers to activity
    |
    v
WEEK 1-2: INITIATION PHASE
    |-- Duration: 10-15 min/session
    |-- Intensity: Light (Borg 9-11, "conversational")
    |-- Frequency: 3 days/week
    |-- Type: Walking (lowest barrier to entry)
    |
    v
WEEK 3-6: BUILD PHASE
    |-- Duration: Increase by 5-10 min every 1-2 weeks -> 20-30 min
    |-- Intensity: Moderate (Borg 12-14, "can talk but not sing")
    |-- Frequency: 3-4 days/week
    |-- Add resistance: 1 set, 8-10 exercises, 10-12 reps, 2x/week
    |
    v
WEEK 7-12: MAINTENANCE TRANSITION
    |-- Duration: 30-45 min
    |-- Intensity: Moderate to somewhat hard (Borg 12-15)
    |-- Frequency: 5 days/week
    |-- Resistance: 2 sets, 8-10 exercises, 8-12 reps, 2-3x/week
    |-- Add flexibility and balance components
    |
    v
BEYOND 12 WEEKS: MAINTENANCE
    |-- Duration: 30-60 min
    |-- Intensity: Varied (mix of moderate and vigorous)
    |-- Frequency: 5-7 days/week
    |-- Volume target: 500-1000 MET-min/week (minimum)
    |       --> Optimal health gains: 3000-4000 MET-min/week
    |-- Consider HIIT: 1-2x/week for additional benefits
    |
    v
CONTINUOUS MONITORING
    |-- Reassess every 3-6 months
    |-- Adjust FITT based on progress
    |-- Screen for overtraining/injury
    |-- Address new barriers
    |-- Reinforce non-exercise activity (steps, stairs, standing)
```

## Pattern 5: Syncope Differential — Neurally Mediated vs Cardiac

```
FEATURE                NEURALLY MEDIATED          CARDIAC
Timing                 Post-exercise/standing     During exercise
Onset                  Gradual (prodrome)         Sudden/abrupt
Premonitory symptoms   Yes (lightheaded, nausea,  No or very brief
                       tunnel vision, sweating,
                       paleness)
Duration               Brief (<1 min)             May be prolonged
Appearance             Pale, clammy               Variable
Aftermath              Prolonged fatigue, nausea  Rapid recovery or
                                                  cardiac arrest
Injury                 Rare (slumps to ground)    Common (unconscious
                                                  before hitting ground)
Triggers               Pain, fear, prolonged      Exertion
                       standing, venepuncture
Position               Standing                   Any position
Associated disorder    None                       HCM, CCAA, ARVC,
                                                  LQTS, CPVT, AS
Work-up                ECG, reassure              ECG, Echo, stress ECG,
                                                  CMR, CT, EP study
```

## Pattern 6: Obesity Management — Exercise Plus Nutrition Framework

```
OBESE/OVERWEIGHT PATIENT
    |
    v
ASSESSMENT
    |-- BMI calculation
    |-- Waist circumference + waist-to-height ratio (more accurate than BMI)
    |-- Identify visceral vs subcutaneous fat distribution
    |-- Screen for comorbidities: diabetes, CVD, OA, depression
    |-- Assess readiness to change
    |
    v
KEY PRINCIPLE: "You cannot outrun a bad diet"
    |-- Exercise alone = minimal long-term weight loss
    |-- Exercise helps MAINTAIN weight loss
    |-- Caloric restriction: short-term loss BUT hormonal counter-regulation
    |       --> Increased appetite (ghrelin, leptin changes)
    |       --> Reduced BMR (energy conservation)
    |       --> Weight regain common when restriction ends
    |
    v
EFFECTIVE STRATEGY
    |-- Focus on food QUALITY over quantity
    |       --> Reduce ultra-processed foods
    |       --> Reduce sugar-sweetened beverages (strong evidence)
    |       --> Replace refined carbs with whole foods
    |       --> Consider omega-6 to omega-3 ratio (target ~4:1 from ~16:1)
    |-- Combine with physical activity
    |       --> Exercise maintains weight loss, improves metabolic health
    |       --> High exercise volumes may be needed (~300 min/week)
    |
    v
MONITORING TOOLS
    |-- Regular weight (bathroom scales)
    |-- Waist circumference changes
    |-- Body fat % (DXA/BIA/skinfolds if available)
    |-- Step count (target >=7000, ideally 10,000+)
```

## Pattern 9: Osteoarthritis Exercise Decision Protocol

```
OA DIAGNOSIS CONFIRMED
    |
    v
ASSESS BASELINE
    |-- Pain severity and location
    |-- Joint range of motion
    |-- Muscle strength around affected joint
    |-- Comorbidities (obesity, CVD, diabetes, depression)
    |-- Current activity level and goals
    |
    v
FIRST-LINE: EDUCATION + EXERCISE + WEIGHT MANAGEMENT
    |-- Educate: OA is manageable, not inevitably progressive
    |-- Frame positively: "keep going" attitude improves participation
    |-- Weight loss: 5.1 kg over 10 years = >50% reduced OA odds
    |       10% body weight reduction = up to 50% pain improvement
    |       Each 1 kg lost = 2.2 kg reduction in peak knee load
    |
    v
EXERCISE PRESCRIPTION BY JOINT
    |
    |-- HIP OA
    |       Land-based exercise: small-moderate effect on pain/function
    |       Aquatic exercise: small short-term benefit
    |       Nordic walking: benefits > supervised strength training alone
    |       Pre-op exercise: reduces pain before hip replacement
    |
    |-- KNEE OA
    |       Land-based exercise: reduces pain, improves function for 2-6 months
    |       High-intensity resistance: large sustained strength improvement
    |       Cycling: improves pain in mild-moderate knee OA
    |       Tai chi: similar to physical therapy; better for depression/QoL
    |       Aquatic: small short-term benefit on pain/disability/QoL
    |       Hatha yoga: improves pain and perceived function
    |
    v
COMORBIDITY-ADAPTED PROTOCOLS (de Rooij et al.)
    |-- Physiological adaptations: CVD, heart failure, HTN, T2DM, COPD, obesity
    |-- Behavioural adaptations: chronic pain, low back pain, depression
    |-- Environmental adaptations: visual/hearing impairment
    |
    v
WHAT TO DISCOURAGE
    |-- Glucosamine and chondroitin (no evidence)
    |-- Opioids (small benefit, high adverse events)
    |-- Repeated IA corticosteroids (cartilage volume loss, no long-term benefit)
    |-- Hyaluronic acid injections (not clinically important over placebo)
```

## Pattern 10: Return to Activity After Health Event

```
POST-EVENT/DIAGNOSIS
    |
    v
MEDICAL CLEARANCE
    |-- Condition stabilized
    |-- No contraindications to exercise
    |-- Specific precautions documented
    |
    v
START LOW, GO SLOW
    |-- Begin at 50% of pre-event volume/intensity
    |-- Light intensity (Borg 9-11)
    |-- Short duration (10-15 min)
    |-- Monitor for symptom recurrence
    |
    v
GRADUATED PROGRESSION
    |-- Increase duration first (+5-10 min/week)
    |-- Then increase frequency (+1 day/week)
    |-- Then increase intensity (light -> moderate -> vigorous)
    |-- If symptoms return, regress to previous tolerated level
    |
    v
RED FLAGS DURING EXERCISE — STOP AND SEEK MEDICAL REVIEW
    |-- Chest pain or pressure
    |-- Dizziness or near-syncope
    |-- Severe dyspnoea disproportionate to exertion
    |-- New or worsening joint pain
    |-- Palpitations with lightheadedness
    |-- Signs of hypoglycaemia (if diabetic)
```
