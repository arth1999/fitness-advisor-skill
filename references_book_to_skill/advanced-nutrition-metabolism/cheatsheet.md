# Practitioner's Cheatsheet: Advanced Nutrition & Human Metabolism

## Quick Reference: Metabolic States

| State | Time | Dominant Hormone | Blood Glucose Source | Primary Fuel (Muscle) | Brain Fuel | RQ |
|-------|------|-----------------|---------------------|----------------------|------------|-----|
| Fed | 0-3 h | Insulin | Dietary absorption | Glucose | Glucose | ~0.85-1.0 |
| Postabsorptive | 3-18 h | Glucagon ↑ | Hepatic glycogenolysis | FFA from adipose | Glucose | ~0.80 |
| Fasting | 18 h-2 d | Glucagon ↑↑, Cortisol | Gluconeogenesis (amino acids) | FFA + ketones | Glucose | ~0.75 |
| Starvation | >2 d | Glucagon, Cortisol, GH | Gluconeogenesis (glycerol) | FFA + ketones | Ketones (2/3) | ~0.70 |

## Quick Reference: Exercise Fuel Selection

| Exercise | % VO₂ max | Primary Fuel | Glycogen Depletion? | CHO Ingestion Needed? |
|----------|-----------|-------------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| Light walking | <25% | Plasma FFA | No | No |
| Brisk walk/jog | 50-65% | Plasma FFA + Muscle TAG + Glycogen | Partial | No (if <1 h) |
| Running/cycling | 65-75% | Muscle glycogen dominant | Yes (by 2 h) | Yes (>2 h, 60-80 g/h) |
| High intensity | >85% | Muscle glycogen | Rapid | Yes (for repeated bouts) |
| Sprint/maximal | 100% | ATP-PC + Anaerobic glycolysis | Not limiting | No |

## Key Hormone Actions at a Glance

| Hormone | Glycogenolysis | Gluconeogenesis | Lipolysis | Protein Synthesis | Protein Breakdown |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Insulin | ↓ | ↓ | ↓ | ↑ | ↓ |
| Glucagon | ↑ (liver only) | ↑ | ↑ | — | — |
| Epinephrine | ↑ (liver + muscle) | — | ↑ | — | — |
| Cortisol | ↑ | ↑ | ↑ | — | ↑ |
| GH | ↑ | — | ↑ | ↑ | ↓ |

## Enzyme Regulation: Quick Diagnostic

| Enzyme | Pathway | Activated by | Inhibited by |
|--------|---------|-------------|-------------|
| PFK-1 | Glycolysis | AMP, F-2,6-BP | ATP, Citrate |
| Pyruvate Dehydrogenase | TCA entry | ADP, Pyruvate, Ca²⁺ | ATP, Acetyl-CoA, NADH |
| Acetyl-CoA Carboxylase | Fatty acid synthesis | Citrate (dephosphorylated) | AMPK phosphorylation, Palmitoyl-CoA |
| CAT I | β-oxidation | Low malonyl-CoA | Malonyl-CoA |
| HMG-CoA Reductase | Cholesterol synthesis | Insulin (dephosphorylated) | AMPK phosphorylation, Statins, Cholesterol |
| Glycogen Synthase | Glycogenesis | Insulin (dephosphorylated) | Epinephrine, Glucagon (phosphorylated) |
| Glycogen Phosphorylase | Glycogenolysis | Epinephrine, Glucagon (phosphorylated) | Insulin |

## Vitamin Deficiency Red Flags

| Sign/Symptom | Possible Deficiencies |
|-------------|---------------------|
| Cheilosis, glossitis, angular stomatitis | B2, B3, B6, Folate |
| Megaloblastic anemia | Folate, B12 |
| Peripheral neuropathy | B1, B6, B12, E |
| Dermatitis (sun-exposed) | B3 (pellagra) |
| Bleeding gums, petechiae, poor wound healing | C |
| Night blindness, Bitot's spots | A |
| Bone pain, muscle weakness | D |
| RBC fragility, hemolytic anemia | E |
| Prolonged bleeding/clotting | K |

## Mineral Absorption: Enhancers & Inhibitors

| Mineral | Enhancers | Inhibitors |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| Iron (nonheme) | Vitamin C, meat/fish/poultry factor, acidic pH | Phytates, polyphenols (tea/coffee), calcium, oxalates |
| Calcium | Vitamin D, protein, lactose, acidic environment | Oxalates (spinach), phytates, excess Mg/Zn, unabsorbed FFA |
| Zinc | Animal protein, acidic environment | Phytates, excess iron, excess calcium |

## Critical Lab Values

| Parameter | Normal Range | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Serum glucose (fasting) | 70-100 mg/dL | ≥126 mg/dL = diabetes |
| Serum Ca²⁺ (total) | 8.5-10.5 mg/dL | Correct for albumin |
| Serum Mg²⁺ | 1.7-2.2 mg/dL | Poor indicator of total body status |
| Serum ferritin | 12-300 ng/mL (varies by sex/age) | <12 = iron deficiency; acute phase reactant |
| Serum 25-OH vitamin D | 30-100 ng/mL | <20 = deficiency; 20-30 = insufficiency |
| Serum B12 | 200-900 pg/mL | <200 = deficiency; 200-300 = borderline |
| RBC folate | 140-628 ng/mL | Better indicator than serum folate |
| TSH | 0.4-4.0 mIU/L | Screen for iodine deficiency/thyroid dysfunction |
