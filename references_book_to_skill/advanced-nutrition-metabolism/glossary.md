# Glossary: Advanced Nutrition and Human Metabolism

## A-C
- **Acetyl-CoA**: Two-carbon activated acetate; the central metabolic intermediate connecting carbohydrate, lipid, and protein catabolism; entry point to the TCA cycle.
- **Adenosine Triphosphate (ATP)**: Universal cellular energy currency; hydrolysis of phosphate anhydride bonds releases ~7.3 kcal/mol.
- **Allosteric regulation**: Modulation of enzyme activity by binding of an effector at a site other than the active site; can be positive (activation) or negative (inhibition).
- **AMPK (AMP-Activated Protein Kinase)**: Master energy sensor kinase; activated by low cellular energy (↑ AMP:ATP ratio); stimulates catabolic pathways, inhibits anabolic pathways.
- **Anabolic**: Metabolic pathways that synthesize complex molecules from simpler precursors; require energy input (ATP, NADPH).
- **Apoptosis**: Programmed cell death via intrinsic (mitochondrial) or extrinsic (death receptor) pathways.
- **Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)**: Energy expenditure under strict resting conditions (12-18 h fasted, thermoneutral, supine); primarily determined by lean body mass.
- **Beta-oxidation**: Mitochondrial pathway sequentially cleaving two-carbon acetyl-CoA units from fatty acyl-CoA; primary fat catabolic pathway.
- **BMI (Body Mass Index)**: Weight (kg) / Height² (m²); screening tool for adiposity; normal 18.5-24.9, overweight 25-29.9, obese ≥30.
- **Calcitriol (1,25-(OH)₂ D)**: Active hormonal form of vitamin D; increases intestinal calcium absorption, bone mineralization, renal calcium reabsorption.
- **Catabolic**: Metabolic pathways that break down complex molecules, releasing energy captured as ATP.
- **Chylomicron**: Largest lipoprotein; transports dietary triacylglycerols from intestine through lymph to systemic circulation.
- **Coenzyme**: Organic non-protein molecule (often vitamin-derived) required for enzyme activity; e.g., NAD+, FAD, CoA, PLP.
- **Cori Cycle**: Muscle lactate → blood → liver → glucose (gluconeogenesis) → blood → muscle; recycles glycolytic products.
- **Covalent modification**: Enzyme regulation by addition/removal of chemical groups (most commonly phosphorylation/dephosphorylation).

## D-I
- **DIAAS (Digestible Indispensable Amino Acid Score)**: Protein quality measure using ileal amino acid digestibility; can exceed 1.0.
- **DRI (Dietary Reference Intakes)**: Umbrella term for EAR, RDA, AI, and UL; quantitative nutrient intake recommendations.
- **Eicosanoids**: Signaling molecules (prostaglandins, thromboxanes, leukotrienes) derived from 20-carbon PUFAs (arachidonic acid, EPA).
- **Electron Transport Chain (ETC)**: Series of protein complexes (I-IV) on inner mitochondrial membrane; transfers electrons from NADH/FADH₂ to O₂, generating proton gradient for ATP synthesis.
- **Essential fatty acids**: Linoleic acid (18:2 n-6) and α-linolenic acid (18:3 n-3); cannot be synthesized due to lack of Δ12 and Δ15 desaturases.
- **Fed-fast cycle**: Four metabolic states — fed (0-3 h), postabsorptive (3-18 h), fasting (18 h-2 days), starvation (>2 days) — each characterized by distinct fuel selection and hormonal profiles.
- **Gluconeogenesis**: Synthesis of glucose from non-carbohydrate precursors (lactate, glycerol, glucogenic amino acids); primarily hepatic (renal cortex contributes during prolonged fasting).
- **Glycemic Index (GI)**: Blood glucose response to a food relative to a reference (glucose or white bread).
- **Glycogen**: Branched glucose polymer (α(1-4) + α(1-6) bonds); stored in liver (~100 g) and muscle (~400 g).
- **Glycolysis**: Cytosolic pathway converting glucose (6C) → 2 pyruvate (3C); net 2 ATP + 2 NADH.
- **Hepcidin**: Liver peptide hormone; master regulator of iron homeostasis; binds ferroportin, blocking iron export from enterocytes and macrophages.
- **HDL (High-Density Lipoprotein)**: Mediates reverse cholesterol transport (tissues → liver); higher levels associated with lower CVD risk.
- **Insulin resistance**: Reduced sensitivity of target tissues (muscle, adipose) to insulin; hallmark of type 2 diabetes and metabolic syndrome.

## K-R
- **Ketone bodies**: Acetoacetate, β-hydroxybutyrate, acetone; produced in liver from acetyl-CoA when carbohydrate is scarce; fuel for brain during starvation.
- **Km (Michaelis constant)**: Substrate concentration at half-maximal enzyme velocity; measure of enzyme-substrate affinity (low Km = high affinity).
- **LDL (Low-Density Lipoprotein)**: Primary cholesterol carrier delivering cholesterol to peripheral tissues; elevated LDL is major CVD risk factor.
- **Leptin**: Adipocyte hormone signaling adequate fat stores; suppresses appetite via hypothalamic receptors; leptin resistance common in obesity.
- **Lipolysis**: Hydrolysis of triacylglycerols to free fatty acids + glycerol; catalyzed by hormone-sensitive lipase (HSL) in adipose tissue.
- **Malonyl-CoA**: Three-carbon intermediate in fatty acid synthesis; also allosteric inhibitor of carnitine acyltransferase I (CAT I), blocking fatty acid entry into mitochondria.
- **Metabolic syndrome**: Cluster of ≥3 of: central obesity, elevated TAGs, reduced HDL, hypertension, elevated fasting glucose; associated with insulin resistance.
- **mTOR (mechanistic Target of Rapamycin)**: Protein kinase integrating amino acid (leucine), energy, and growth factor signals to regulate protein synthesis.
- **Oxidative phosphorylation**: ATP synthesis coupled to electron transport via the proton gradient across the inner mitochondrial membrane; produces bulk of cellular ATP.
- **PDCAAS (Protein Digestibility-Corrected Amino Acid Score)**: Protein quality metric comparing essential amino acid profile to reference, corrected for fecal digestibility; truncated at 1.0.
- **Pentose Phosphate Pathway**: Alternative glucose oxidation pathway producing NADPH (for reductive biosynthesis) and ribose-5-phosphate (for nucleotides).
- **PTH (Parathyroid Hormone)**: Secreted when serum Ca²⁺ is low; stimulates bone resorption, renal calcium reabsorption, and calcitriol synthesis.
- **Respiratory Quotient (RQ)**: CO₂ produced / O₂ consumed; reflects fuel mix (CHO = 1.0, Fat = 0.70, Protein = 0.82, Mixed ≈ 0.82).

## S-Z
- **SGLT1 (Sodium-Glucose Linked Transporter 1)**: Active transport protein on enterocyte apical membrane; cotransports glucose/galactose with Na⁺.
- **Short-chain fatty acids (SCFAs)**: Acetate, propionate, butyrate; produced by colonic bacterial fermentation of fiber; butyrate is primary colonocyte fuel.
- **Thermic effect of food (TEF)**: Energy cost of digestion, absorption, transport, metabolism, and storage of ingested nutrients; ~10% of TEE; protein highest (20-30%).
- **TCA Cycle (Tricarboxylic Acid Cycle, Krebs Cycle)**: Mitochondrial cycle oxidizing acetyl-CoA to CO₂; produces NADH, FADH₂, GTP; amphibolic (catabolic + anabolic).
- **Transamination**: Transfer of amino group from amino acid to α-ketoglutarate, forming glutamate and the corresponding α-keto acid; requires PLP (vitamin B6).
- **Triacylglycerol (TAG)**: Glycerol esterified to three fatty acids; primary storage form of lipid energy; comprises ~95% dietary fat.
- **Urea cycle**: Liver-exclusive pathway converting ammonia (from amino acid catabolism) to urea for renal excretion.
- **VLDL (Very Low-Density Lipoprotein)**: Hepatic lipoprotein exporting endogenous triacylglycerols to peripheral tissues.
- **VO₂ max**: Maximum rate of oxygen consumption during exercise; measure of cardiovascular fitness; used to normalize exercise intensity (% VO₂ max).
- **Zymogen (proenzyme)**: Inactive enzyme precursor requiring proteolytic cleavage or conformational change for activation (e.g., pepsinogen → pepsin, trypsinogen → trypsin).
