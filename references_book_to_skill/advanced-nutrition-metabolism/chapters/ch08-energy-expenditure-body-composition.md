# Chapter 8: Energy Expenditure, Body Composition, and Healthy Weight

## Core Idea
Energy balance is determined by energy intake versus total energy expenditure (TEE = BMR + physical activity + thermic effect of food + thermoregulation). Body weight and body composition are the long-term outcomes of energy balance, regulated by a complex neuroendocrine system involving leptin, insulin, ghrelin, and multiple gut hormones.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Components of Total Energy Expenditure**:
  - **Basal/Resting Metabolic Rate (BMR/RMR)** — ~65% of TEE. Energy for basic life processes: respiration, heartbeat, renal function, brain/nerve function, active transport, protein synthesis. Measured under strict conditions (12-18 h fasted, supine, thermoneutral).
  - **Physical Activity** — ~25% (variable, 15-30%). Includes exercise and non-exercise activity thermogenesis (NEAT).
  - **Thermic Effect of Food (TEF)** — ~10%. Energy for digestion, absorption, transport, metabolism, storage. Protein 20-30%, carbohydrate 5-10%, fat 0-5%.
  - **Thermoregulation** — variable. Adaptive thermogenesis to maintain core temperature (~98.6°F).
  - When to use: Estimating daily energy needs or troubleshooting weight management.

- **Respiratory Quotient (RQ) as Fuel Mix Indicator**:
  - RQ = CO2 produced / O2 consumed.
  - Carbohydrate: RQ = 1.0; Fat: RQ = 0.70; Protein: RQ = 0.82.
  - Mixed diet: RQ ~0.82 (40% CHO, 60% fat).
  - RQ <0.7 suggests starvation, ketogenic diet, or alcoholism.
  - RQ >1.0 suggests hyperventilation, acidosis, or net fat synthesis (lipogenesis).
  - When to use: Determining substrate oxidation from indirect calorimetry data; RQ shifts reveal fuel source changes during exercise or dietary interventions.

- **Predictive Equations for RMR**:
  - **Harris-Benedict (1919)**: Overestimates RMR in obese individuals.
    - Men: RMR = 66.5 + (13.7 × W) + (5.0 × H) - (6.8 × A)
    - Women: RMR = 665 + (9.56 × W) + (1.85 × H) - (4.7 × A)
  - **Mifflin-St. Jeor (1990)**: More accurate for obese populations; predicts within ±10% of measured RMR.
    - Men: RMR = (9.99 × W) + (6.25 × H) - (4.92 × A) + 5
    - Women: RMR = (9.99 × W) + (6.25 × H) - (4.92 × A) - 161
    - W = kg, H = cm, A = years.
  - When to use: Clinical nutrition assessment — use Mifflin-St. Jeor for obese patients.

- **Physical Activity Level (PAL) Categories**:
  | Category | PAL Range | PAL Average | Walking Equivalent (miles/day) |
  |----------|-----------|-------------|-------------------------------|
  | Sedentary | 1.0-1.39 | 1.25 | ~0 |
  | Low active | 1.4-1.59 | 1.50 | 2.2 |
  | Active | 1.6-1.89 | 1.75 | 7.3 |
  | Very active | 1.9-2.49 | 2.20 | 16.7 |
  - TEE = RMR × PAL. Walking equivalents based on 70 kg (154 lb) reference individual.
  - When to use: Estimating total daily energy expenditure without direct measurement.

## Key Concepts
- **Direct Calorimetry**: Measures heat production; accurate but impractical.
- **Indirect Calorimetry**: Measures O2 consumption + CO2 production; calculates energy expenditure using caloric equivalent of O2 (~5 kcal/L at RQ 0.82).
- **Doubly Labeled Water**: Gold standard for free-living TEE; uses 2H2O and H218O; measures CO2 production over ~3 weeks.
- **BMI Categories**: Underweight <18.5, Normal 18.5-24.9, Overweight 25-29.9, Obese class I 30-34.9, class II 35-39.9, class III ≥40 kg/m².
- **Body Composition Methods (Two-Compartment Model: Fat Mass + Fat-Free Mass)**:
  - Field: Skinfold thickness (±5% precision), Bioelectrical impedance (affected by hydration).
  - Lab: Underwater weighing (densitometry), Air displacement plethysmography (BOD POD), DEXA (gold standard for bone + fat + lean).
- **Leptin**: Adipocyte hormone; signals adequate fat stores to hypothalamus; suppresses hunger (inhibits NPY/AgRP, stimulates POMC); directly correlated with body fat; leptin resistance common in obesity.
- **Ghrelin**: Stomach hormone; only known orexigenic (hunger-stimulating) hormone; rises before meals, falls after eating; postprandial decline blunted in obesity.
- **Adiponectin**: Adipocyte hormone; inversely correlated with body fat; protects against insulin resistance and inflammation; increased by weight loss, n-3 fatty acids, Mediterranean diet, exercise.
- **Other Anorexigenic Hormones**: CCK (short-term satiety), GLP-1 (stimulates insulin, delays gastric emptying), PYY (long-term satiety, lower in obese), Pancreatic polypeptide.
- **Metabolic Syndrome**: ≥3 of: elevated waist circumference (≥102 cm M, ≥88 cm F), elevated TAGs (≥150 mg/dL), reduced HDL (<40 M, <50 F mg/dL), elevated BP (≥130/85 mmHg), elevated fasting glucose (≥100 mg/dL).

## Mental Models
- **The energy gap**: Small daily surpluses compound — 150 kcal/day excess = ~1 lb fat gain in 23 days = ~16 lb/year.
- **Appetite as hormone-driven homeostat**: Ghrelin (hunger) vs. leptin, insulin, CCK, GLP-1, PYY (satiety) — obesity involves dysregulation of this system, not lack of willpower.
- **Fat cell dynamics**: Weight gain = hypertrophy (cells enlarge) + hyperplasia (new cells form in childhood/puberty); weight loss = cells shrink but number does not decrease.
- **Obesogens**: Endocrine-disrupting environmental chemicals that may promote adipogenesis and alter appetite regulation — an emerging non-caloric contributor to obesity.

## Anti-patterns
- **Using BMI alone as a health indicator**: BMI does not distinguish muscle from fat; intermediate BMI values (25-30) are weakly correlated with actual body fat percentage. Always combine with waist circumference.
- **Relying on Harris-Benedict for obese populations**: It systematically overestimates RMR; use Mifflin-St. Jeor.
- **Treating all weight loss approaches equally**: Calorie restriction alone loses both fat and fat-free mass; adding exercise (especially resistance training) spares lean mass.
- **Assuming the macronutrient composition of a calorie-restricted diet determines success**: Behavior modification intensity is the strongest predictor of weight loss success, not macronutrient ratios.

## Key Takeaways
1. BMR is primarily determined by lean body mass — organs (liver 27%, brain 19%, kidneys 10%, heart 7%) account for ~60% of BMR despite being a small fraction of body weight.
2. The thermic effect of protein (20-30%) is substantially higher than carbohydrate (5-10%) or fat (0-5%) — a metabolic advantage of higher-protein diets.
3. Waist circumference adds critical risk information: for BMI 25-35, waist >40" (M) or >35" (F) indicates high to very high disease risk.
4. Adiponectin is one of the few adipokines that increases with weight loss and is protective against metabolic disease.
5. Intestinal microbiota composition (Firmicutes:Bacteroidetes ratio) differs between lean and obese individuals and changes with weight loss.
6. Exercise alone produces modest weight loss (~2-3 kg) but significantly improves metabolic health markers independent of weight change.
7. Insulin resistance is both cause and consequence of obesity — weight loss improves insulin sensitivity, creating a positive feedback loop.
