# Chapter 10: Fat-Soluble Vitamins

## Core Idea
Fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K) differ fundamentally from water-soluble vitamins: they require dietary fat and bile for absorption, are transported via chylomicrons into lymph (not portal blood), and can be stored in substantial quantities in liver and adipose tissue. This enables intermittent intake but also creates toxicity risk from chronic overconsumption.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Vitamin A Family Hierarchy**: Retinoids (preformed vitamin A: retinol, retinal, retinoic acid, retinyl esters from animal foods) vs. Provitamin A carotenoids (β-carotene, α-carotene, β-cryptoxanthin from plants). β-carotene has the greatest provitamin A activity.
  - When to use: Assessing vitamin A status — preformed sources carry toxicity risk; carotenoid sources have a built-in safety ceiling.

- **Vitamin D Endocrine System**:
  - Synthesis: 7-dehydrocholesterol (skin) → UV-B → Cholecalciferol (D3) → Liver (25-hydroxylase) → 25-OH D → Kidney (1-hydroxylase, regulated by PTH) → 1,25-(OH)2 D (calcitriol, active hormone).
  - Functions: Bone mineral metabolism, calcium/phosphorus homeostasis, cell differentiation, immune modulation.
  - RDA: 15 μg (600 IU) adults ≤70 yr; 20 μg (800 IU) >70 yr.
  - When to use: Evaluating vitamin D status — serum 25-OH D is the biomarker of status, not 1,25-(OH)2 D.

- **Vitamin E Nomenclature**: The term "vitamin E" covers 8 naturally occurring compounds (4 tocopherols + 4 tocotrienols). Only α-tocopherol meets human vitamin E requirements — other forms are not interconverted to α-tocopherol in humans. RDA expressed as mg α-tocopherol.
  - When to use: Reading supplement labels — "mixed tocopherols" provides γ-tocopherol (which has anti-inflammatory properties) but only α-tocopherol counts toward RDA.

## Key Concepts
- **Vitamin A**: Retinal = visual pigment (rhodopsin component); Retinoic acid = gene transcription regulator (RAR/RXR nuclear receptors); cell differentiation, immune function. Deficiency = night blindness, xerophthalmia, Bitot's spots, keratinization. RDA: 900 μg RAE (M), 700 μg RAE (F). UL: 3000 μg preformed.
- **Carotenoids (non-provitamin A)**: Lutein and zeaxanthin (macular pigment, eye health); Lycopene (prostate health).
- **Vitamin D**: Calcitriol increases intestinal calcium absorption (TRPV6 channels, calbindin D9k), bone mineralization, and renal calcium reabsorption. Deficiency = rickets (children), osteomalacia (adults), increased fracture risk. Toxicity (UL: 100 μg/4000 IU): hypercalcemia.
- **Vitamin E**: Primary lipid-soluble antioxidant in cell membranes; breaks chain reactions of lipid peroxidation; regenerated by vitamin C. Deficiency = RBC fragility, hemolytic anemia, peripheral neuropathy. RDA: 15 mg α-tocopherol. UL: 1000 mg.
- **Vitamin K**: Cofactor for γ-glutamyl carboxylase; activates clotting factors (II, VII, IX, X) and bone proteins (osteocalcin). Phylloquinone (K1, plants) vs. Menaquinones (K2, bacterial synthesis). AI: 120 μg (M), 90 μg (F). Deficiency = prolonged clotting time, hemorrhage.

## Mental Models
- **Fat-soluble vitamin absorption as a package deal**: Anything impairing fat digestion (pancreatic insufficiency, bile duct obstruction, cystic fibrosis, gastric bypass) impairs all fat-soluble vitamin absorption simultaneously.
- **Vitamin D as a hormone, not just a vitamin**: Its active form (calcitriol) is synthesized in one organ (kidney) and acts on distant tissues via nuclear receptors — the classic endocrine paradigm.
- **Antioxidant network**: Vitamin E (membrane) + Vitamin C (aqueous) + Glutathione — a redox cascade where oxidized vitamin E is regenerated by vitamin C, which is regenerated by glutathione/NADPH.

## Anti-patterns
- **Assuming β-carotene supplements provide the same benefits as dietary β-carotene**: Clinical trials (ATBC, CARET) showed increased lung cancer risk in smokers taking high-dose β-carotene supplements.
- **Using vitamin D without considering calcium and vitamin K**: Vitamin D increases calcium absorption; adequate calcium intake is required for bone mineralization; vitamin K ensures calcium is directed to bone, not soft tissues.
- **Assuming vitamin E supplements prevent cardiovascular disease**: Multiple large trials (HOPE, GISSI, Women's Health Study) showed no CVD benefit from vitamin E supplementation.

## Key Takeaways
1. Preformed vitamin A toxicity (hypervitaminosis A) causes liver damage, bone fractures, and teratogenicity — avoid supplements >UL, especially in pregnancy.
2. The AI for vitamin K is based on amounts needed to maintain normal clotting; emerging evidence suggests higher intakes benefit bone and vascular health.
3. Vitamin D status assessment uses serum 25-OH D; >30 ng/mL considered sufficient, <20 ng/mL deficient.
4. Vitamin E absorption requires pancreatic esterase and bile; fat malabsorption syndromes cause deficiency of all fat-soluble vitamins.
5. Vitamin K is unique among fat-soluble vitamins in having a relatively small body pool and rapid turnover, making consistent dietary intake important.
