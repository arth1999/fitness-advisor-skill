## 2. Exercise Intensity Classification Pattern

```
Intensity     %HRR/VO2R    %HRmax     RPE(6-20)   METs      Talk Test
-----------   ---------    ------     ---------   ----      ---------
Light         <30%         <57%       <9          <2.0      Easy conversation
Very Light    30-39%       57-63%     9-11        2.0-2.9   Easy conversation
Moderate      40-<60%      64-76%     12-13       3.0-5.9   Can talk, not sing
Vigorous      60-89%       77-95%     14-17       6.0-8.7   Short phrases only
Near-Max      >=90%        >=96%      >=18        >=8.8     Cannot talk
```

## 4. Exercise Progression Pattern for Deconditioned Special Populations

```
Timeline ----------------------------------------------------------------->
Phase        Initiation      Improvement     Consolidation   Maintenance
             (Weeks 1-4)     (Weeks 5-12)    (Weeks 13-24)   (Week 25+)

Frequency    2-3 days/wk     3-4 days/wk     4-5 days/wk     5-7 days/wk
Intensity    Light (30-40%)   Moderate        40-60% HRR      Mixed
                              (40-<60% HRR)
Time         5-15 min         15-30 min       30-45 min       30-60 min
             (multiple bouts) (continuous)    (continuous)    (continuous)
Type         Low-impact,      Add variety,    Diversify       Preference-
             tolerated modes  increase load   challenge       based
Progression  Increase T first Increase F and  Gradually       Periodized
             (duration)       T (minutes)     increase I      variation
                                                  (intensity)
Monitoring:  HR, BP, RPE,     HR, BP, RPE,    HR, BP, RPE,   Periodic
             SpO2 if needed   symptom check   symptom check  reassess

WARNING: Any sign of excessive fatigue, pain, symptom exacerbation,
or exercise intolerance -> regress to previous phase.
```

## 6. Condition-Specific Pre-Exercise Screening Pattern

```
CONDITION            PRE-EXERCISE CHECK                 MONITORING
---------            -----------------                  ---------
Hypertension         Resting BP <160/100                BP before, during, after
Coronary Disease     Medical clearance required          HR/BP/RPE; know ischemic threshold
Diabetes (T1/T2)     Blood glucose level                BG before/after; during if >1hr
Asthma/EIB           Pre-exercise bronchodilator 15min   Symptoms; have rescue inhaler
COPD                 Oxygen saturation (SpO2)            SpO2 >=88-90%; dyspnea scale
Heart Failure        Medical clearance; daily weight     HR/BP/RPE; watch for weight gain
RA/Lupus/FM/CFS      Current pain/fatigue level          Adjust session based on symptoms
Pregnancy            Medical clearance; avoid supine     RPE 12-14; avoid overheating
Older Adults         Medical clearance if symptomatic    RPE; balance; fall risk
Cancer               Medical clearance; labs             Adjust for treatment cycle
```

## 7. Inclusive Exercise Environment Pattern

```
Facility Requirements (ADA 2010 Standards):
├── Entry/Pathways
│   ├── Doorway lever handles or automated doors
│   ├── Continuous path >=36 in (91 cm) wide
│   └── Wheelchair turnaround >=60 in (152 cm) diameter
├── Exercise Stations
│   ├── Open floor space 3x4 ft (0.9x1.2 m) beside >=1 of each type
│   ├── Accessible cardiovascular equipment (arm + leg options)
│   └── Equipment sized for children and larger individuals
├── Safety Adaptations
│   ├── Torso straps for stability
│   ├── Wrist straps/assistance grasping devices
│   └── Treadmills with reduced minimum speed
└── Environmental Controls
    ├── Temperature 66-72°F (19-22°C) for obesity populations
    ├── Reduced noise/visual distractions for cognitive conditions
    ├── Seating in training area for recovery
    └── Accessible bathroom facilities
```

## 8. Medication Timing & Exercise Interaction Pattern

```
DRUG TIMING             ACTION
----------              ------
Beta-blockers           HR blunted at all intensities; RPE-based intensity prescription
Diuretics               Dehydration risk; avoid peak diuretic effect timing for exercise
Insulin (rapid-acting)  Avoid exercise during peak (1-2hr post-injection); use non-injected sites
Insulin (long-acting)   More stable; adjust pre-exercise CHO intake
Statins                 May increase muscle soreness; start low intensity, progress gradually
Bronchodilators (SABA)  Take 15 min pre-exercise; refractory period up to 2hr after warm-up
NSAIDs                  May mask pain signals; potentially attenuate muscle adaptation
Anticoagulants          Contact/collision sports contraindicated; monitor for bruising
Corticosteroids (oral)  Long-term: muscle weakness, osteoporosis; resistance training important
```

## 9. Emergency Response Pattern

```
DURING EXERCISE EMERGENCY:
├── Angina/Chest Pain
│   ├── Stop exercise immediately
│   ├── Client takes nitroglycerin if prescribed
│   ├── If not relieved within 5 min -> EMS
│   └── If relieved -> do not resume exercise that day
├── Hypoglycemia
│   ├── Stop exercise
│   ├── Check BG if possible
│   ├── Administer 15-20g fast-acting CHO
│   ├── Recheck BG in 15 min
│   └── If unresponsive -> EMS (glucagon if available)
├── Asthma Attack/EIB
│   ├── Stop exercise
│   ├── Administer rescue inhaler (SABA)
│   ├── Sit upright; slow breathing
│   └── If no improvement in 5-10 min -> EMS
├── Hypotension/Dizziness
│   ├── Stop exercise
│   ├── Have client sit or lie down
│   ├── Elevate legs if possible
│   ├── Monitor HR/BP
│   └── If not resolving -> EMS
└── Musculoskeletal Injury
    ├── Stop offending activity
    ├── RICE protocol (Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation)
    ├── Refer to medical professional if:
    │   ├── Severe pain
    │   ├── Swelling
    │   ├── Unable to bear weight
    │   └── Joint instability
    └── Document incident
```
