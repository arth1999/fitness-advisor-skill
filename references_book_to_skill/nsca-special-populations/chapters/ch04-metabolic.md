# Chapter 4: Metabolic Conditions and Disorders

## 4.1 Obesity

### Epidemiology
- BMI >=30 kg/m2: >1/3 of U.S. adults; 17% of children/adolescents
- Medical costs: ~$147 billion annually (2008 estimate)
- Associated with hypertension, dyslipidemia, CVD, type 2 diabetes, OA, sleep apnea, certain cancers

### Exercise Recommendations
| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Aerobic Intensity | 40-85% HRR |
| Weekly Duration (prevent weight gain) | 150-300 min |
| Weekly Duration (prevent regain) | 300+ min |
| Caloric expenditure for weight loss | 1,200-2,000 kcal/wk |
| Caloric expenditure to prevent regain | 2,000+ kcal/wk |
| Frequency | 5-7 d/wk |
| Minimum session duration | 10 min (deconditioned) |
| Goal session duration | 30 min (minimum); 45-60 min optimal for weight loss; 60-90 min to prevent regain |
| Modality | Aerobic priority (weight-bearing preferred) + resistance training (preserve lean mass) |

### Precautions & Modifications
- Prone to musculoskeletal injury; low-impact aerobic initially
- Start with 5-15 min intermittent bouts 2-3x/day
- Increase duration/frequency before intensity
- Adaptive equipment may be needed (larger seats, weight-rated machines)
- Adequate fluid intake; thermoneutral environment (66-72°F / 19-22°C)
- Increase NEPA + reduce sitting time; pedometer: aim 11,000-14,000 steps/day
- Monitor BP, BG, lipids every 3-6 months
- Most effective: caloric restriction + exercise + increased daily activity

### Medications (Obesity)
- orlistat (Xenical, Alli): lipase inhibitor; GI side effects
- phentermine/topiramate (Qsymia): may decrease HR; hypoglycemia risk with T2DM
- lorcaserin (Belviq): serotonin receptor agonist; may decrease HR
- naltrexone/bupropion (Contrave): may increase resting HR/BP

## 4.2 Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus

### Epidemiology & Pathophysiology
- ~29.1 million Americans with diabetes (90-95% Type 2); 86 million prediabetic
- Insulin resistance -> progressive beta-cell dysfunction
- Obesity strongly linked: BMI >35 = 30x greater risk vs BMI <23
- 75% of Type 2 diabetes cases occur among obese, inactive adults
- Complications: CAD (70% die of heart disease), CHF, stroke, HTN, kidney disease (44% of new ESRD cases), retinopathy, neuropathy, PAD

### Blood Glucose Classification
| Classification | Fasting BG | Random (2h post 75g glucose) |
|---------------|------------|------------------------------|
| Normal | <100 mg/dL | <140 mg/dL |
| Prediabetes | 100-125 mg/dL | 140-199 mg/dL |
| Diabetes | >125 mg/dL | >199 mg/dL |

### Exercise Recommendations
| Component | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Aerobic Frequency | 2-3 x 10-min bouts -> 30 min continuous, 5-7 d/wk |
| Aerobic Intensity | 50-85% HRR; RPE 12-16; Talk Test applicable |
| Aerobic Duration | >=10 min/session; goal >=30 min; >=150 min/wk (up to 300+ for weight loss) |
| Aerobic Mode | Rhythmic, large muscle groups (walking, biking, swimming) |
| Caloric Expenditure | >=300 kcal/session; >2,000 kcal/wk |
| Resistance Frequency | 2-3 d/wk (non-consecutive, 48h apart) |
| Resistance Duration | 30-60 min; 8-12 exercises; 2-3 sets of 8-12 reps |
| Resistance Intensity | 50-70% 1RM start; progress to nonlinear: 50-65%/65-80%/80-95% 1RM across sessions |
| Flexibility | Daily; 10-30s holds; 20-25 min (important due to reduced ROM in diabetes) |

### Precautions & Modifications
- Medical clearance + clinical exercise test if >10% 10-year cardiac event risk
- Silent ischemia common; may need radionuclide stress test
- **Hypoglycemia** (<70 mg/dL): most common exercise complication
  - Avoid exercise when exogenous insulin peaks
  - Monitor BG before/after exercise; during if >1hr
  - If pre-exercise BG <100 mg/dL: consume 15-30g CHO
  - Avoid injecting insulin into exercising limbs (use abdomen)
- If BG >200 mg/dL + check ketones; if no ketones: light-mod ok, avoid vigorous
- If BG >250-350 + ketones present: DO NOT EXERCISE
- Retinopathy: avoid vigorous intensity + excessive BP elevations
- Autonomic neuropathy: may have blunted HR/BP response -> medically supervised program
- Kidney disease/PAD/neuropathy: medically supervised program
- 75% of Type 2 diabetes patients die from CVD; be cautious with vigorous exercise

### Medications (Type 2 Diabetes)
- Metformin: low hypoglycemia risk alone; GI side effects
- Sulfonylureas (glipizide, glyburide): higher hypoglycemia risk
- DPP-4 inhibitors, GLP-1 agonists, SGLT2 inhibitors: lower hypoglycemia risk
- Insulin: timing/dose adjustment critical for exercise

## 4.3 Type 1 Diabetes Mellitus

### Epidemiology & Pathophysiology
- Autoimmune destruction of pancreatic beta cells; 5-10% of all diabetes
- Requires exogenous insulin; can occur at any age
- Without insulin: hyperglycemia -> ketoacidosis

### HbA1c Diagnostic Criteria
- HbA1c >=6.5% on two occasions indicates diabetes
- Reflects average BG over previous 2-3 months

### Insulin Types
| Type | Onset | Duration |
|------|-------|----------|
| Rapid-acting (lispro, aspart) | 15 min | Several hours |
| Short-acting (regular) | 30 min | 3-6 hours |
| Intermediate-acting (NPH) | 2-4 hours | Up to 18 hours |
| Long-acting (glargine, detemir) | Several hours | ~24 hours |

### Exercise Recommendations
| Component | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Aerobic Frequency | 3-7 d/wk (<=2 days between sessions) |
| Aerobic Intensity | Moderate (40-<60% HRR) to Vigorous (>=60-90% HRR) |
| Aerobic Duration | >=10 min/session; >=150 min/wk |
| Resistance Frequency | 2-3 non-consecutive d/wk |
| Resistance Intensity | 8-10 exercises; 1-3 sets; 6-15 RM |
| Flexibility | 2-3 d/wk; static 10-30s hold |
| Balance | 2-3 d/wk; single-leg stands, tai chi, yoga |

### Precautions & Modifications
**Pre-Exercise BG Decision Algorithm:**
| Pre-exercise BG | Action |
|-----------------|--------|
| <90-100 mg/dL | Consume 15-30g CHO before starting |
| 90-150 mg/dL | May need CHO at start and during (0.5-1 g/kg/hr) |
| 150-250 mg/dL | Can exercise; monitor during |
| 250-350 mg/dL | Check ketones; if moderate-high: DO NOT exercise; if trace/low: light-mod ok, wait until <250 for vigorous |
| >350 mg/dL | Check ketones; if moderate-high: DO NOT exercise; if trace/low: correct insulin per MD, light-mod ok |

- HIIT may cause less hypoglycemia than moderate continuous exercise
- Nocturnal hypoglycemia risk: monitor BG hours after exercise + at bedtime
- Resistance training may reduce exercise-induced hypoglycemia incidence
- Avoid injecting insulin into exercising limbs
- Dehydration risk if hyperglycemic

## 4.4 Dyslipidemia

### Classification (NCEP Guidelines)
| Lipid | Optimal | Near Optimal | Borderline High | High | Very High |
|-------|---------|-------------|-----------------|------|-----------|
| Total Cholesterol | <150 | <200 | 200-239 | 240+ | - |
| LDL | <100 | 100-129 | 130-159 | 160-189 | 190+ |
| HDL | >=60 | - | - | <40 | - |
| Triglycerides | <100 | <150 | 150-199 | 200-499 | 500+ |

### Exercise Recommendations
| Component | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Aerobic Frequency | 1-4 sessions/day; 4-7 d/wk |
| Aerobic Intensity | 40-85% HRR; RPE 12-16 |
| Aerobic Duration | 15-60 min/session |
| Resistance Frequency | 2-3 d/wk |
| Resistance Intensity | 50-85% 1RM |
| Resistance Duration | 20-40 min; 8-12 exercises |
| Flexibility | 3-7 d/wk; 12-16 static stretches; 10-30s hold |

### Key Points
- Exercise 8-12h before high-fat meal maximizes postprandial TG reduction
- Lipoprotein lipase activity peaks 8-12h post-exercise
- >1,200 kcal/wk aerobic exercise for sustained TG decrease and HDL increase
- Accumulated bouts (3x10 min) may be as effective as continuous (30 min)
- High-intensity intermittent exercise may be more effective for postprandial lipids

### Statin Considerations
- 10-25% of statin users experience myalgias (muscle pain)
- Resistance training while taking statins may increase DOMS
- Statins may blunt VO2max improvements (10% vs 1.5% in one study)
- Start exercise at low intensity; progress gradually
- Monitor for unusual/lingering muscle soreness; refer if persistent

### Dyslipidemia Medications
- Statins (atorvastatin, simvastatin, etc.): HMG-CoA reductase inhibitors; myalgia risk
- Ezetimibe: cholesterol absorption blocker; fewer side effects
- PCSK9 inhibitors: newer injectables; no significant exercise effects
- Fibrates, niacin, bile acid sequestrants: less commonly first-line

## 4.5 Hypothyroidism and Hyperthyroidism

### Key Points
- Hypothyroidism: 7x more prevalent in women; fatigue, weight gain, bradycardia, elevated cholesterol
- Hyperthyroidism: 4-5x more prevalent in women; tachycardia, weight loss, heat intolerance, muscle weakness
- Both reduce exercise capacity
- Goal: establish euthyroid state before exercise programming

### Exercise Recommendations (Once Euthyroid)
| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Aerobic Frequency | 5-7 d/wk (if weight loss needed) |
| Aerobic Intensity | 40-85% HRR |
| Aerobic Duration | 30-60 min; >=150 min/wk |
| Caloric Expenditure | >=1,200 kcal/wk; >2,000 for weight loss |
| Resistance | 2-3 d/wk; 8-12 exercises; 60-80% 1RM; periodized over 4-6 months |
| Flexibility | 5-7 d/wk; 8-12 exercises; 20-30s holds; 2-3 reps |

### Abnormal Exercise Responses (Untreated)
| Parameter | Hypothyroidism | Hyperthyroidism |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|
| Exercise Intolerance | Decreased (insufficient muscle blood flow) | Decreased (elevated submax HR, reduced HRR) |
| HR Response | Depressed | Elevated; slow to increase |
| SBP | Depressed | Elevated |
| Recovery HR | Slower | Slower |
| Anaerobic Threshold | Reduced | Reduced |

### Medications
- Hypothyroidism: levothyroxine (synthroid); when properly dosed, normal exercise response
- Hyperthyroidism: PTU, methimazole; beta-blockers for symptom control (use RPE not HR for intensity)

## 4.6 Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)

### Stages
- Stage 1: Normal GFR, kidney damage present
- Stage 2-4: Progressive GFR decline
- Stage 5 (ESRD): GFR <15 mL/min; requires dialysis or transplant

### Epidemiology
- ~13% of U.S. adults; 44% of those >65 years
- Leading causes: diabetes (44% of new ESRD), hypertension

### Exercise Recommendations
| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Aerobic Frequency | Most/all days |
| Aerobic Intensity | Moderate (40-<60% HRR); RPE 12-13 |
| Aerobic Duration | 20-60 min (intermittent or continuous); >=150 min/wk |
| Resistance Frequency | 2-3 d/wk |
| Resistance Intensity | 70-75% 1RM; 1 set of 10-15 reps |
| Flexibility | Daily as tolerated |

### Key Points
- Functional capacity 60-70% of age/sex-matched healthy peers
- Can improve ~17-23.8% with training
- Can exercise during dialysis (stationary cycling)
- Erythropoietin-stimulating agents treat anemia; improve O2 carrying capacity
- Medical clearance essential; medically supervised for stage 5/ESRD
- Caution with vigorous intensity
- Comorbidities (diabetes, hypertension, CVD) common -> manage accordingly
