# Chapter 3: Musculoskeletal Conditions and Disorders

## 3.1 Disorders of the Spine and Associated Musculature

### Low Back Pain (LBP)
- Most common musculoskeletal complaint; 80% of adults experience at some point
- Can be specific (identified pathology) or nonspecific
- Exercise is first-line treatment for nonspecific LBP

**Exercise Recommendations for Low Back Pain:**
- **Aerobic**: 3-5 d/wk, low-moderate intensity, pain-free, 20-60 min
- **Resistance**: 2-3 d/wk, 50-70% 1RM, emphasize core stabilization
- **Flexibility**: Daily; hamstrings, hip flexors, lumbar extensors
- **Contraindications**: Avoid spinal flexion under compressive load; avoid heavy lifting with poor form; avoid activities that aggravate symptoms
- **Key strategy**: Core strengthening (transversus abdominis, multifidus); neutral spine positioning; gradual return to activity; pain as guide

### Posture Conditions
- Include kyphosis, lordosis, scoliosis, forward head posture
- Progressive posture deterioration with inactivity, aging, occupational demands

**Exercise Recommendations:**
- Postural muscle strengthening (scapular retractors, back extensors)
- Stretch shortened muscles (pecs, upper traps, hip flexors)
- Body awareness and postural re-education
- Ergonomic modifications

## 3.2 Disorders of the Skeletal System

### Osteoporosis
- Low bone mineral density (BMD); T-score <= -2.5
- Increased fracture risk; common sites: hip, spine, wrist
- Prevalence: 10 million Americans; 80% women
- Osteopenia: T-score between -1.0 and -2.5 (precursor)

**Exercise Recommendations for Osteoporosis:**
- **Aerobic**: 3-5 d/wk weight-bearing (walking, jogging, stair climbing), moderate-vigorous, 30-60 min
- **Resistance**: >=2 d/wk, 70-80% 1RM, 8-12 reps, 2-3 sets, full-body
- **Balance training**: >=2 d/wk essential for fall prevention
- **Contraindications**: NO spinal flexion exercises (crunch/sit-up type); NO explosive twisting; NO high-impact if fracture risk
- **Key muscles**: Back extensors, hip extensors, core stabilizers
- **Medications**: Bisphosphonates, hormone therapy, SERMs, denosumab, teriparatide; generally no significant exercise interactions

### Osteopenia
- Milder BMD loss; same exercise principles as osteoporosis with more tolerance for impact
- Primary prevention: maximize peak bone mass in youth; weight-bearing exercise throughout life

## 3.3 Disorders of Joint Structures

### Osteoarthritis (OA)
- Most common form of arthritis; degenerative joint disease
- Cartilage breakdown; pain, stiffness, reduced ROM
- Common in weight-bearing joints: knees, hips, spine

**Exercise Recommendations:**
- **Aerobic**: 3-5 d/wk, low/no-impact during flares (water exercise, cycling), 20-30 min
- **Resistance**: 2-3 d/wk, 40-60% 1RM start, ROM through full pain-free range
- **Flexibility**: Daily ROM exercises; water-based exercise excellent
- **Precautions**: Avoid high-impact activities; modify exercise during acute flares; aquatic exercise highly recommended

### Joint Disorders & Replacements
- Total hip arthroplasty (THA), total knee arthroplasty (TKA)
- Post-surgical restrictions: typically avoid extreme ROM, high-impact activities
- Exercise progression must follow surgeon's protocol
- Emphasize ROM restoration first, then strengthening

## 3.4 Disorders of the Muscular System

### Frailty
- Geriatric syndrome: decreased strength, endurance, physiologic function
- Increased vulnerability to stressors; falls, hospitalization, mortality

### Sarcopenia
- Age-related loss of muscle mass and strength
- Begins ~age 30; accelerates after 50

**Exercise Recommendations for Frailty/Sarcopenia:**
- **Resistance training is PRIMARY intervention**
- Frequency: 2-3 d/wk
- Intensity: 40-50% 1RM start, progress to 70% 1RM
- Volume: 1-3 sets of 8-15 reps
- Exercises: Multijoint, functional movements
- **Aerobic**: 3-5 d/wk, low-moderate start, accumulate short bouts
- **Balance training**: Essential daily component
- **Flexibility**: Daily, all major muscle groups
- **Key**: Progressive overload; adequate protein intake (1.2-1.6 g/kg/day)
