# Chapter 5: Pulmonary Disorders and Conditions

## 5.1 Asthma and Exercise-Induced Bronchoconstriction (EIB)

### Pathophysiology
- Asthma: chronic airway inflammation; variable airflow obstruction; bronchial hyperresponsiveness (BHR)
- EIB: transient airway narrowing triggered by exercise; occurs in 90% of asthmatics and 10-15% of general population
- Water loss from airways during exercise -> mediator release -> bronchoconstriction
- Refractory period: after initial exercise bout, less bronchoconstriction in subsequent bout (lasts up to 2h)

### Exercise Recommendations
**Aerobic:**
| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Frequency | 3-5 sessions/wk |
| Intensity | 40-<60% HRR/VO2R |
| Mode | Large muscle mass activities (walking, cycling, swimming) |
| Duration | 20-60 min continuous |

**Resistance:**
| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Frequency | 2-3 sessions/wk |
| Intensity | Moderate (60-80% 1RM) |
| Reps/Sets | 6-12 reps; 2-4 sets |

### Key Modifications & Precautions
- **Pre-exercise SABA** (short-acting beta-2 agonist) 15 min before exercise
- **10-15 min warm-up** (especially variable/high-intensity intervals) produces refractory period up to 2h
- Daily controller medication (ICS or leukotriene receptor antagonist)
- Individualized action plan for symptom exacerbation
- Avoid known triggers (cold/dry air, allergens, pollutants)
- Indoors or mask in cold weather
- Swimming: generally well-tolerated (humid environment)
- Interval training (10-30s work / 30-90s rest) for high-intensity sessions

### Medications
- **Controller (daily)**: ICS (fluticasone, budesonide), LABAs (salmeterol, formoterol), leukotriene modifiers (montelukast)
- **Reliever (rescue)**: SABAs (albuterol, levalbuterol), anticholinergics (ipratropium)
- **Oral corticosteroids** (prednisone): for severe cases; systemic effects (muscle weakness, osteoporosis)

## 5.2 Pulmonary Hypertension (PH)

### Definition & Classification
- Mean pulmonary arterial pressure (PAP) >25 mmHg at rest
- Pulmonary arterial hypertension (PAH): specific clinical type without other precapillary causes
- 6 categories based on pathophysiological mechanisms
- Rare: 1 in 100,000 to 1,000,000 for PAH; up to 1% globally for all PH

### Exercise Testing in PH
- VO2peak <10.4 mL/kg/min: poorer prognosis
- 6-min walk test <500m: less favorable
- SBP failure to exceed 120 mmHg at peak: worse prognosis
- SpO2 drop >10% below resting: poorer prognosis
- Exercise-induced PH may precede resting PH

### Exercise Recommendations
- **Aerobic**: Light-mod intensity; interval training commonly used (30s low / 60s higher at 60-80% HR achieved at max test)
- **HR limit**: <120 BPM; **SpO2 limit**: >85%
- **Mode**: Walking, stationary cycling
- **Duration/frequency**: 10-25 min/day cycling + 60 min walking; 5 d/wk
- **Resistance**: Light (bodyweight, light dumbbells); 30 min, 5 d/wk
- **Respiratory muscle training**: Specific inspiratory/expiratory training; 30 min, 5 d/wk
- **Typical program structure**: 3 weeks in-hospital + 12 weeks at home

### Key Precautions
- Medically supervised testing to determine symptom thresholds BEFORE starting
- Avoid high-intensity that exacerbates symptoms
- Avoid Valsalva maneuver
- Clients should be physically active within symptom tolerance
- No consensus on optimal FITT parameters (limited RCTs)
- Exercise + pharmacotherapy combination
- Respiratory muscle dysfunction common; specific training beneficial

### Medications (PH/PAH)
- **Endothelin receptor antagonists** (ambrisentan, bosentan): improve exercise tolerance; delay clinical worsening
- **Phosphodiesterase inhibitors** (sildenafil, tadalafil): no change in exercise capacity
- **Prostacyclin analogues** (epoprostenol): possible small improvement in exercise capacity
- **Calcium channel blockers**: for vasoreactive patients only

## 5.3 Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD)

### Epidemiology & Pathology
- >11 million diagnosed in U.S.; millions undiagnosed
- Progressive emphysema + chronic bronchitis; airflow obstruction
- 80-90% related to smoking; also environmental exposures
- Third leading cause of death (after heart disease and cancer)
- Women 37% more likely to have COPD than men

### COPD Severity Classification (GOLD Criteria)
| Classification | Post-bronchodilator FEV1 (% predicted) |
|---------------|--------------------------------------|
| Mild | >=80% |
| Moderate | 50-79% |
| Severe | 30-49% |
| Very Severe | <30% |

Requires FEV1/FVC ratio <0.70.

### Exercise Recommendations
**Aerobic:**
| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Frequency | 3-5 d/wk |
| Intensity | 30-80% peak work rate (individualized to symptoms) |
| Mode | Walking or cycling |
| Duration | 20-60 min/session |

**Resistance:**
| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Frequency | 2-3 d/wk |
| Intensity | Light-mod; 40-80% 1RM |
| Reps/Sets | 8-12 reps; 1-4 sets |
| Rest | 2-3 min between sets |
| Exercises | 8-12 mostly large muscle groups, multijoint |

### Key Points
- Resistance + aerobic training: greater strength/mass gains; no additional dyspnea/QOL benefit over aerobic alone
- Program can be designed around client preference (aerobic, resistance, or combination all effective)
- Exercise improves peripheral muscle function, not lung function
- Decreased systemic inflammation with training
- Smoking cessation is ONLY intervention proven to slow lung function decline
- 15-20% also have comorbid asthma (asthma-COPD overlap): faster progression

### Medications (COPD)
- **SABA** (albuterol): rescue; pre-exercise use beneficial
- **LABA** (salmeterol, formoterol): daily controller; 12-24h bronchodilation
- **LAMA** (tiotropium): 24h anticholinergic
- **ICS+LABA combinations** (Advair, Symbicort): twice daily; reduce exacerbations
- **Oral corticosteroids**: short-term for exacerbations; long-term: muscle weakness, weight gain, osteoporosis

## 5.4 Chronic Restrictive Pulmonary Disease (CRPD)

### Classification
- **Intrinsic**: Interstitial lung disease (idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis/IPF, sarcoidosis, pneumoconiosis, radiation fibrosis)
- **Extrinsic**: Chest wall/pleural/neuromuscular disorders (kyphosis, obesity-related restriction)
- Characterized by decreased TLC; FEV1/FVC ratio >80%

### Exercise Recommendations
- Medically supervised testing ESSENTIAL before starting
- 8-week pulmonary rehabilitation minimum; >=2 supervised sessions/wk
- Minimum 30 min aerobic per session
- Supplemental oxygen if SpO2 drops during exercise
- Joint pain (connective tissue disease) may limit weight-bearing
- Comorbidity and psychological management integrated

### Key Points
- Exercise safe and beneficial for dyspnea and QOL
- Cardiopulmonary exercise testing (CPET): reveals exercise tolerance, comorbidities, prognosis
- 6-min walk test: valid, responsive endpoint; correlates with lung function and QOL
- 12-week program: 54% of IPF patients improved 6-min walk test by 10%
- Excess body weight increases dyspnea
- Severe dyspnea: shorter sessions with rest breaks

### Medications (CRPD)
- **Corticosteroids** (prednisone): immunosuppression; long-term use -> muscle weakness, fracture risk
- **Immunosuppressive agents** (cyclophosphamide, azathioprine): for fibrotic ILD
- **Antifibrotic agents** (nintedanib, pirfenidone): slow IPF progression
- **Supplemental oxygen**: maintain SpO2 >90%

## 5.5 Cystic Fibrosis (CF)

### Epidemiology & Pathology
- Autosomal recessive disease; most common life-limiting genetic disorder in Caucasians
- CFTR gene mutation -> thick secretions in lungs, pancreas, intestines, bile duct, sweat glands
- ~29,000 living with CF in U.S. (2015); median survival: 41.7 years (up from 10 years in 1960s)
- Pulmonary disease is leading cause of mortality

### Exercise Recommendations
**Aerobic:**
| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Frequency | 3-5 d/wk |
| Intensity | 40-70% peak HR |
| Mode | Walking, treadmill, cycling |
| Duration | 20-30 min |

### Key Precautions & Modifications
- **Hydration critical**: excessive NaCl loss in sweat; hyponatremic hypochloremic dehydration risk; diminished thirst drive
- Exercise during cooler hours or air-conditioned facility
- OMNI 0-10 scale to regulate intensity (children capable of using it)
- Terminate if Borg 0-10 scale reaches 7
- Monitor HR, respiratory rate, SpO2, RPE initially
- Baseline CPET recommended before starting
- Postural assessment (kyphosis, scapular position common)
- Exercise as additional chest physiotherapy session (mechanical clearance)
- Reassess exercise tolerance after pulmonary exacerbations
- Higher physical activity and VO2max positively related to survival
- Pancreatic insufficiency common -> nutritional support (fat-soluble vitamins A, D, E, K)

### Medications (CF)
- Inhaled hypertonic saline: mucolytic; cough
- Inhaled antibiotics (tobramycin): for pseudomonas colonization
- Dornase alfa: mucolytic (hydrolyzes DNA in mucus)
- Ivacaftor: CFTR potentiator; improves lung function
- Pancreatic enzymes (pancrelipase): for malabsorption
- Fat-soluble vitamins: A, D, E, K supplements
