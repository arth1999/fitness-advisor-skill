# Chapter 6: Cardiovascular Conditions and Disorders

## Common Theme: Atherosclerosis
Atherosclerosis is the underlying mechanism for most cardiovascular conditions covered in this chapter. It is defined as the development of plaque in arteries (coronary, peripheral, cerebral). Risk factors include non-modifiable (age, sex, family history) and modifiable (lipid profile, exercise, smoking, diabetes, sedentary behavior).

---

## 6.1 Hypertension (HTN)

### Definition
- SBP >=140 mmHg OR DBP >=90 mmHg on >=2 occasions
- Prehypertension: SBP 120-139 or DBP 80-89
- "Silent killer": often asymptomatic until significant organ damage
- ~1 in 3 U.S. adults affected
- 90-95% primary (essential) hypertension; cause unknown

### Exercise Recommendations
**Aerobic:**
| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Frequency | 5-7 d/wk (preferably) |
| Intensity | 40-<60% HRR/VO2R; RPE 12-13 |
| Mode | Large muscle groups (walking, cycling) |
| Duration | >=30 min/day; may be 3-6 bouts of 10 min |
| Total Weekly | 150-250+ min |

**Resistance:**
| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Frequency | 2-3 d/wk (preferably every other day) |
| Intensity | 60-80% 1RM (may start at 40%) |
| Reps/Sets | 8-12 (up to 15); 1-3 sets |
| Rest | 30-60 s |
| Exercises | 8-12, multijoint, large muscle groups |
| Progression | 2-5 lb upper body; 5-10 lb lower body |

### Key Precautions
- Monitor BP before, during, after exercise
- Exercise termination: SBP >220 or DBP >105 mmHg
- Avoid Valsalva maneuver during resistance training (educate breathing)
- Postexercise hypotension: BP reduced up to 4h after exercise
- Repeated bouts throughout day enhance BP and fitness benefits
- Circuit weight training format recommended (time-efficient)
- Expected BP reductions: ~5 mmHg SBP, 2-3 mmHg DBP with 8-week aerobic program

### Medications (HTN)
| Class | Examples | Exercise Effects |
|-------|----------|-----------------|
| Diuretics (Thiazides) | HCTZ, chlorthalidone | Reduce blood volume; dehydration risk; heat intolerance |
| Beta-blockers | metoprolol, atenolol | Blunted HR response -> use RPE for intensity |
| ACE Inhibitors | lisinopril, enalapril | Postural hypotension possible |
| ARBs | losartan, valsartan | Similar to ACE inhibitors |
| Calcium Channel Blockers | amlodipine, nifedipine | Postural hypotension; dizziness |

## 6.2 Peripheral Arterial Disease (PAD)

### Definition & Key Facts
- Narrowing of non-cardiac arteries reducing blood flow; affects ~8.5 million U.S. adults >=40
- Classic symptom: Intermittent Claudication (IC) -- aching/cramping in legs during exercise, relieved by rest
- IC occurs in only 10-40% of PAD cases; many asymptomatic
- Main risk factors: smoking, hyperlipidemia, diabetes

### Exercise Recommendations
**Aerobic:**
| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Frequency | 3-7 d/wk |
| Intensity | 40-<60% HRR; pain scale <3 out of 4 |
| Mode | Walking (works gastrocnemius) |
| Duration | 30-60 min/day; may be intermittent |

**Resistance:**
| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Frequency | 2-3 d/wk |
| Intensity | 60-80% 1RM (intensity determined by pain for IC) |
| Reps/Sets | 10-12; 1-2 sets each upper/lower body |

### Key Strategy: Intermittent Training for IC
- Walk to pain level 3/4 (0=no pain, 1=onset, 2=moderate, 3=intense, 4=maximal)
- Rest until pain completely subsides
- Repeat until 30 min of accumulated walking tolerated
- Goal: increase amount of work before IC pain onset

### Medications (PAD)
- Antiplatelet/vasoactive (cilostazol/Pletal): may increase exercise capacity
- Statins: cholesterol lowering
- Anticoagulants (Plavix, aspirin): bleeding risk
- Antihypertensives: as for HTN above

## 6.3 Angina (Stable)

### Definition
- Chest pain from myocardial ischemia (reduced blood flow to heart)
- Stable: predictable onset at specific stress level; relieved by rest or nitroglycerin
- Unstable: unpredictable, occurs at rest; NOT a candidate for independent exercise
- ~9 million Americans; >50% women; women more likely to feel symptoms in neck, jaw, throat, abdomen, back

### Key Concept: Ischemic Threshold
- The heart rate and blood pressure (rate-pressure product) at which angina occurs is highly predictable and reproducible
- Exercise programming: train BELOW this threshold

### Exercise Recommendations
**Aerobic:**
| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Frequency | 4-7 d/wk (preferably every day) |
| Intensity | 10-15 bpm BELOW ischemic threshold |
| Mode | Large muscle groups (walking, jogging, cycling) |
| Duration | 20-60 min continuous or accumulated |

**Resistance:**
| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Frequency | 2-3 d/wk |
| Intensity | Light; 40-60% 1RM |
| Reps/Sets | 8-12; 1-2 sets |
| Exercises | Initially one per large muscle group, multijoint |

### Key Precautions
- Medically supervised graded exercise test REQUIRED before program
- Client MUST carry nitroglycerin during all exercise
- If angina occurs: slow/stop -> rest -> if not relieved -> take nitroglycerin -> if still not relieved -> EMERGENCY ROOM
- Discuss symptom mitigation plan BEFORE any exercise
- Longer warm-up and cooldown necessary
- Postural hypotension risk with vasodilating medications

### Medications (Angina)
- Nitrates (nitroglycerin): vasodilation; carry during exercise
- Beta-blockers: lower HR; blunted exercise HR
- Calcium antagonists: vasodilation; postural hypotension
- Ranolazine: non-hemodynamic mechanism

## 6.4 Chronic Heart Failure (CHF)

### Definition
- Inability of heart muscle to pump blood at rate consistent with metabolic needs
- Systolic HF: reduced ejection fraction (<35%); impaired contractility
- Diastolic HF: normal EF; impaired relaxation/filling
- Prevalence: ~5.1 million and increasing in U.S.

### Exercise Recommendations
**Aerobic:**
| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Frequency | 4-7 d/wk |
| Intensity | 40-70% HRR/VO2R; RPE 12-14 |
| Mode | Walking, cycling |
| Duration | 20-60 min/day (continuous or accumulated) |

**Resistance:**
| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Frequency | 2-3 d/wk |
| Intensity | Light-mod; 40-80% 1RM |
| Reps/Sets | 10-15; 1 set per exercise; circuit format |
| Rest | <=30 s between exercises |

### Key Precautions
- Medically supervised maximal exercise test + VO2 measurements (anaerobic threshold valuable)
- Extensive warm-up and cooldown required
- Daily weight monitoring: significant rapid gain = fluid retention = decompensation = EMERGENCY
- Difficulty with temperature extremes (medication-related)
- HIIT for appropriately screened clients with higher exercise capacity

### Medications (CHF)
- Antiarrhythmics (digoxin): increase contractility
- Beta-blockers: block SNS; lower HR
- Diuretics: reduce volume overload
- ACE inhibitors/ARBs: reduce afterload
- Aldosterone antagonists: reduce remodeling
- CRT (biventricular pacing): restore synchrony; increase exercise capacity

## 6.5 Myocardial Infarction (MI)

### Key Facts
- ~720,000 heart attacks/year in U.S.
- Clot formation at atherosclerotic lesion site
- Severity depends on tissue damage and collateral blood flow

### Exercise Recommendations
**Aerobic:**
| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Frequency | >=3 d/wk |
| Intensity | 40-80% HRR/VO2R; RPE 12-16 |
| Mode | Walking, jogging, cycling |
| Duration | 20-60 min continuous or accumulated |

**Resistance:**
| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Frequency | 2-3 d/wk |
| Intensity | 40-80% 1RM |
| Reps/Sets | 10-15; 1 set per exercise; circuit format |
| Exercises | 8-10 |

### Key Precautions
- Medically supervised exercise test REQUIRED before program
- Initial exercise in monitored cardiac rehabilitation setting (36 sessions typical)
- Extended cooldown CRITICAL: period after exercise is most vulnerable for arrhythmias
- Arrhythmia threshold increases with training
- Post-MI medications: beta-blockers, ACE inhibitors, antiplatelets, statins

## 6.6 Atrial Fibrillation (AF)

### Definition
- Chaotic, rapid atrial depolarizations; irregular ventricular response
- Atria fire at >300 BPM; AV node protects ventricles
- Estimated 1.2-2.6 million cases (2010-2030 projected)

### Exercise Recommendations
**Aerobic:**
| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Frequency | 4-7 d/wk |
| Intensity | 50-85% VO2peak; RPE 13-16 |
| Mode | Walking, jogging, cycling |
| Duration | 30-60 min/day |

**Resistance:** 2-3 d/wk; 40-80% 1RM; circuit format

### Key Precautions
- **HR unreliable for intensity monitoring -- USE RPE**
- Variable ventricular response makes HR-based prescription invalid
- Atrial contribution to CO lost (~20%); may cause early fatigue
- Anticoagulant use common (warfarin): bleeding risk with falls/contact
- Programming most affected by underlying comorbidity (HF, HTN, CAD)

## 6.7 Pacemakers and ICDs

### Types
- Rate-responsive, biventricular (bundle branch block), AV (atrial-ventricular conduction), combined with ICD
- Purpose: correct abnormal conduction; restore normal rhythm

### Exercise Recommendations
**Aerobic:**
| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Frequency | 4-7 d/wk |
| Intensity | 40-80% VO2peak |
| Duration | 20-60 min/day |

**Resistance:** 2-3 d/wk; 40-60% 1RM; lower body emphasis initially

### Key Precautions
- Post-implant: avoid upper body exercises for several weeks (lead integrity)
- ICD: target HR >=10-15% BELOW defibrillation threshold to prevent inappropriate shocks
- Medically supervised exercise test to evaluate pacemaker response under exercise
- Know pacemaker type and settings for each client
- Watch for symptoms of malfunction: syncope, dyspnea, dizziness, palpitations, tachy/bradycardia

## 6.8 Valvular Disorders (VHD)

### Definition
- Damage to heart valves: stenotic (narrowed) or incompetent (regurgitation)
- ~5 million U.S. adults moderate-severe VHD annually
- Causes: rheumatic fever, congenital, infection, aging

### Exercise Recommendations
**Aerobic:**
| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Frequency | >=4 d/wk |
| Intensity | 40-80% VO2peak; RPE 12-16 |
| Duration | 20-60 min (>=10 min bouts) |

**Resistance:** 1-2 d/wk; 30-50% 1RM (very light)

### Key Precautions
- Wide range of recommendations based on valve affected and severity
- Symptomatic aortic or pulmonary stenosis: may contraindicate ALL resistance training
- Supervised exercise testing recommended before training
- Longer warm-up/cooldown
- Comorbidities (AF, CAD) common; manage accordingly
- Modest/asymptomatic VHD: near-normal exercise response and benefits

## 6.9 CABG and PTCA (Revascularization)

### Definition
- CABG: open heart surgery; graft vessel bypasses blocked coronary artery
- PTCA/PCI: catheter-based balloon/stent to open artery
- >700,000 PTCA procedures/year in U.S.

### Exercise Recommendations
**Aerobic:**
| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Frequency | 4-7 d/wk |
| Intensity | 40-80% VO2peak; RPE 12-16 |
| Mode | Brisk walking, jogging, cycling |
| Duration | 20-60 min/day |

**Resistance:** 2-3 d/wk; 40-80% 1RM; circuit format

### Key Precautions
- Initial programming for CABG MUST be in cardiac rehabilitation setting
- **CABG**: chest wall healing required (2-4 weeks) before upper body exercises
- Post-CABG flexibility and ROM exercises critical for chest wall
- Return of angina symptoms = possible vessel reocclusion -> immediate medical attention
- **Education**: surgery/intervention is TREATMENT not CURE
  - Saphenous vein graft lifespan: 5-7 years
  - Arterial graft lifespan: 20-25 years
  - Lifestyle changes essential to maximize graft life
- HIIT may be tolerated for selected clients as program progresses
