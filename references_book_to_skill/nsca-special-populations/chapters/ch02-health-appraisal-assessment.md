# Chapter 2: Health Appraisal and Fitness Assessments

## 2.1 Preparticipation Screening

Purpose: Identify and classify concerns related to exercise participation, such as known/unknown chronic diseases or conditions.

### Screening Tools

**PAR-Q+ (Physical Activity Readiness Questionnaire for Everyone)**
- 7 core health questions (heart condition, chest pain, dizziness, chronic conditions, medications, musculoskeletal issues, medical supervision)
- Follow-up questions for specific conditions: arthritis/back problems, cancer, cardiovascular conditions, hypertension, metabolic conditions, mental health, respiratory disease, spinal cord injury, stroke
- If ALL NO: cleared for physical activity; sign participant declaration
- If ANY YES: complete follow-up pages; may need ePARmed-X+ or medical referral

**Medical History Questionnaire**
- Demographics, past hospitalizations
- 12-month history: medications, weight changes, faintness, sleep, vision, headaches, cough, speech, anxiety, palpitations
- Current: shortness of breath, numbness, cold extremities, swelling, leg cramps, chest pain, blood pressure, cholesterol, diabetes, stress
- Family history: diabetes, heart disease, stroke, hypertension

**Informed Consent Form**
- Explains test procedures
- Discloses risks and discomforts
- States benefits
- Confirms voluntary participation and right to stop anytime

**Medical Clearance Form**
- Completed by health care professional
- Confirms physical examination within last year
- Classifies client: Class I (unsupervised program ok), Class II (supervised program), Class III (medically supervised only)
- Lists medications and restrictions

## 2.2 Medical Clearance Process

### When to Refer for Medical Clearance

**No clearance needed if:**
- Physically active + no known disease + no signs/symptoms: continue moderate-vigorous, increase as tolerated
- Physically inactive + no disease/symptoms: start light-moderate, progress as tolerated

**Clearance recommended if:**
- Physically inactive + known disease (even if asymptomatic): clearance before any exercise
- Any client with new signs/symptoms: discontinue exercise, obtain clearance
- Physically active + known disease + asymptomatic + no clearance in past 12 months

### Diseases Triggering Clearance Referral
- Cardiovascular disease (cardiac, peripheral vascular, cerebrovascular)
- Metabolic disease (Type 1 and Type 2 diabetes)
- Renal disease

### Signs/Symptoms Suggestive of Disease
1. Pain/discomfort in arms, neck, chest, jaw (possible ischemia)
2. Shortness of breath at rest or mild exertion
3. Dizziness or syncope
4. Orthopnea or paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea
5. Ankle edema
6. Heart palpitations or tachycardia
7. Intermittent claudication
8. Heart murmur
9. Unusual fatigue or shortness of breath with usual activities

### Exercise Intensity Classifications
- **Light**: 30-<40% HRR/VO2R, 2-<3 METs, RPE 9-11
- **Moderate**: 40-<60% HRR/VO2R, 3-<6 METs, RPE 12-13
- **Vigorous**: >=60-90% HRR/VO2R, >=6 METs, RPE >=14

## 2.3 Fitness Assessment

### Test Order
1. Non-fatiguing: height, weight, resting HR, resting BP, flexibility, body composition, anthropometrics, neuromuscular
2. Agility tests (T-test, hexagon)
3. Maximal muscular strength (1RM)
4. Local muscular endurance (partial curl-up)
5. Cardiovascular endurance (graded exercise test)

### Assessment Categories
1. Cardiovascular endurance (treadmill, cycle, arm ergometer, step test)
2. Muscular strength (1RM bench press, 1RM leg press)
3. Local muscular endurance (partial curl-up)
4. Flexibility (sit-and-reach, back scratch)
5. Anthropometric (BMI, girths, skinfolds, BIA)
6. Neuromuscular (balance, coordination)
7. Functional performance (8-foot up-and-go)
8. Condition-specific tests (6-min walk test, 2-min step, 30-sec chair stand, 30-sec arm curl, chair sit-and-reach)

### Exercise Test Termination Criteria
1. Onset of angina or angina-like symptoms
2. SBP drops >10 mmHg from baseline with increasing work rate
3. Extreme BP increase; SBP >250 or DBP >115 mmHg
4. Shortness of breath, wheezing, leg cramps, claudication
5. Ataxia, dizziness, pallor, cyanosis, cold/clammy skin, nausea
6. Failure of HR to increase with increasing work rate
7. Change in heart rhythm
8. Client requests to stop
9. Physical/verbal indication of severe fatigue
10. Equipment malfunction

### Special Population Testing Considerations
- Reduced-intensity protocols (RAMP rather than Bruce) preferred
- Ramp protocols provide better submaximal data for exercise prescription
- Arm ergometry for those unable to use legs; VO2 values ~20-30% lower than treadmill
- Recumbent stepper test alternative for those with balance issues
- No normative fitness standards exist for diseased/disabled populations
- Longitudinal comparison to own baseline is critical

## 2.4 SMART Goal Setting

Goals should be: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound
- Short-term, medium-term, and long-term goals established collaboratively

## 2.5 Motivation and Coaching Techniques
- Positive feedback on goal attainment
- Education on benefits and expected outcomes
- Building self-efficacy through progressive success
- Social support and group exercise options
