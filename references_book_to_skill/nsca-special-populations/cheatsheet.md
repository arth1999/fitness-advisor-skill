# NSCA Special Populations -- Comprehensive Cheatsheet

> FITT-VP Summary, Contraindications, Medication Interactions for All Conditions

## 一、Musculoskeletal Conditions (Ch3)

| Condition | Aerobic F | Aerobic I | Aerobic T | Resistance F | Resistance I | Key Precautions |
|-----------|----------|----------|----------|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Low Back Pain | 3-5 d/wk | Low-mod, pain-free | 20-60 min | 2-3 d/wk | 50-70% 1RM | Avoid spinal flexion under load; emphasize core stability; pain as guide |
| Osteoporosis | 3-5 d/wk weight-bearing | Moderate-vigorous | 30-60 min | >=2 d/wk | 70-80% 1RM | Avoid spinal flexion, explosive twisting; strengthen back extensors; balance training essential |
| Osteopenia | 3-5 d/wk weight-bearing | Moderate | 30-60 min | >=2 d/wk | 60-80% 1RM | Similar to osteoporosis; bone-loading impact activities if tolerated |
| Osteoarthritis | 3-5 d/wk | Low-mod | 20-30 min (accumulate) | 2-3 d/wk | 40-60% 1RM start | Low/no-impact when joints inflamed; ROM exercises; water-based exercise excellent |
| Joint Replacement | Post-clearance | Low-mod | Gradual | Post-clearance | Light start | Follow surgeon restrictions; avoid high-impact; ROM priority post-surgery |
| Frailty/Sarcopenia | 3-5 d/wk | Low-mod start | Accumulate bouts | 2-3 d/wk | 40-50%->70% 1RM | Progressive resistance training is primary; functional exercises; balance training critical |

## 二、Metabolic Conditions (Ch4)

| Condition | Aerobic F | Aerobic I | Aerobic T | Resistance F | Resistance I | Key Precautions |
|-----------|----------|----------|----------|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Obesity** | 5-7 d/wk | 40-85% HRR | 150-300+ min/wk (wt loss >250) | 2-3 d/wk | 50-80% 1RM | Low-impact initially; multiple 5-15 min bouts; avoid hyperthermia; adaptive equipment (larger seats) |
| **Type 2 Diabetes** | 3-7 d/wk (<=2d gap) | 50-85% HRR; RPE 12-16 | >=150 min/wk, 30-60 min/session | 2-3 d/wk (non-consecutive) | 50-85% 1RM | BG monitoring pre/post exercise; hypoglycemia risk with insulin/sulfonylureas; avoid high-intensity if BG >250+ketones; foot care with neuropathy |
| **Type 1 Diabetes** | 3-7 d/wk | 40-<60% or >=60% HRR | >=150 min/wk | 2-3 d/wk non-consecutive | 6-15 RM | Pre-exercise BG check mandatory; carbohydrate timing essential; adjust insulin timing/dose; ketone check if BG >250; HIIT may stabilize BG better than moderate |
| **Dyslipidemia** | 4-7 d/wk | 40-85% HRR; RPE 12-16 | 15-60 min/session | 2-3 d/wk | 50-85% 1RM | Statin myalgia risk; start low intensity, progress gradually; exercise 8-12h before high-fat meal for max TG benefit; 1,200+ kcal/wk target |
| **Hypo/Hyperthyroidism** | 5-7 d/wk | 40-85% HRR | 150-300 min/wk | 2-3 d/wk | 60-80% 1RM | Ensure euthyroid state first; hyperthyroid: tachycardia, reduced HRR; hypothyroid: bradycardia, reduced contractility |
| **Chronic Kidney Disease** | Most days | Moderate; RPE 12-13 | 20-60 min, >=150/wk | 2-3 d/wk | 70-75% 1RM, 10-15 reps | Caution with vigorous intensity; medical environment for stage 5/ESRD; can exercise during dialysis; anemia management |

## 三、Pulmonary Conditions (Ch5)

| Condition | Aerobic F | Aerobic I | Aerobic T | Resistance F | Resistance I | Key Precautions |
|-----------|----------|----------|----------|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Asthma/EIB** | 3-5 d/wk | 40-<60% HRR/VO2R | 20-60 min | 2-3 d/wk | 60-80% 1RM, 6-12 reps | Pre-exercise SABA 15 min before; 10-15 min warm-up (produces refractory period up to 2h); avoid cold/dry/polluted air triggers; have rescue inhaler |
| **Pulmonary Hypertension** | 3-5 d/wk | Light-mod; HR <120 BPM; SpO2 >85% | As tolerated, interval approach | 2 d/wk | Light (body weight/ light dumbbells) | Medically supervised testing first; avoid high-intensity; avoid Valsalva maneuver; respiratory muscle training; 6-min walk test monitoring |

| **Cystic Fibrosis** | 3-5 d/wk | 40-70% peak HR | 20-30 min | Individualized, progressive | Postural muscles focus | Adequate hydration critical (NaCl loss); chest physiotherapy; OMNI 0-10 scale for intensity; infection control; reassess after exacerbations |

## 四、Cardiovascular Conditions (Ch6)

| Condition | Aerobic F | Aerobic I | Aerobic T | Resistance F | Resistance I | Key Precautions |
|-----------|----------|----------|----------|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Hypertension** | 5-7 d/wk | 40-<60% HRR; RPE 12-13 | >=30 min/day; >=150 min/wk | 2-3 d/wk | 60-80% 1RM; 8-12 reps | Monitor BP before/during/after; avoid Valsalva; terminate if SBP>220 or DBP>105; beta-blockers: use RPE not HR |
| **PAD** | 3-7 d/wk | 40-<60% HRR; pain scale <3/4 | 30-60 min (interval to claudication -> rest -> repeat) | 2-3 d/wk | 60-80% 1RM; 10-12 reps | Walk to moderate pain (3/4) -> rest until subsides -> repeat; weight-bearing preferred (walking works gastrocnemius); comorbidities may emerge with fitness gains |
| **Angina (Stable)** | 4-7 d/wk | 10-15 bpm BELOW ischemic threshold | 20-60 min | 2-3 d/wk | 40-60% 1RM (light) | Know ischemic threshold; client carries nitroglycerin; if pain unrelieved by rest+nitro -> ER; longer warm-up/cooldown |
| **Chronic Heart Failure** | 4-7 d/wk | 40-70% HRR/VO2R; RPE 12-14 | 20-60 min/day | 2-3 d/wk | 40-80% 1RM; 10-15 reps; circuit format | Medically supervised max test first; extensive warm-up/cooldown; daily weight (rapid gain=decompensation); temperature extremes risky |
| **Myocardial Infarction (Post)** | >=3 d/wk | 40-80% HRR/VO2R; RPE 12-16 | 20-60 min | 2-3 d/wk | 40-80% 1RM; 10-15 reps; circuit | Cardiac rehab first 36 sessions; medically supervised test required; extended cooldown (vulnerable period for arrhythmias); HIIT for selected clients |
| **Atrial Fibrillation** | 4-7 d/wk | 50-85% VO2peak; RPE 13-16 | 30-60 min/day | 2-3 d/wk | 40-80% 1RM; 10-15 reps | HR unreliable -> use RPE; anticoagulant bleeding risk; underlying comorbidity drives programming |
| **Pacemaker/ICD** | 4-7 d/wk | 40-80% VO2peak | 20-60 min | 2-3 d/wk | 40-60% 1RM; lower body emphasis initially | Post-implant: avoid upper body for several weeks; ICD: target HR >=10-15% below defibrillation threshold; know pacemaker type |
| **Valvular Disorders** | >=4 d/wk | 40-80% VO2peak; RPE 12-16 | 20-60 min | 1-2 d/wk | 30-50% 1RM (very light) | Symptomatic aortic/pulmonary stenosis: may contraindicate resistance training; longer warm-up/cooldown; management of comorbidity key |
| **CABG/PTCA (Post)** | 4-7 d/wk | 40-80% VO2peak; RPE 12-16 | 20-60 min | 2-3 d/wk | 40-80% 1RM; circuit format | CABG: chest wall healing 2-4 weeks before upper body exercise; educate: surgery is not a cure (saphenous graft lifespan 5-7 yr); return of angina = vessel reocclusion |

| **Lupus (SLE)** | 3-4 d/wk | 60% VO2peak or 70-80% MHR | 30 min (start: 3x10min bouts) | 2-3 d/wk | 40-60% -> 65-75% 1RM; 10-12 reps | Photosensitivity: avoid sun/UV exposure; avoid overexertion (flare trigger); extra adaptation time needed; fatigue management |

## 八、Cancer (Ch10)

| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Aerobic Frequency | 3-5 d/wk |
| Aerobic Intensity | 40-<60% HRR start; progress as tolerated |
| Aerobic Time | 20-30 min start -> 150+ min/wk |
| Resistance Frequency | 2-3 d/wk |
| Resistance Intensity | Light-mod start; 40-60% 1RM; progress slowly |
| Flexibility | Daily; ROM exercises |
| **Key Precautions** | Bone metastases: avoid high-impact, high-load; lymphedema: compression sleeve, gradual upper body progression, stop if symptoms; immunosuppression (chemo): avoid public facilities during nadir; anemia/thrombocytopenia: modify intensity; cardiotoxicity (certain chemo): cardiac monitoring; radiation: avoid chlorine pools for irradiated skin; post-surgery: surgeon restrictions; exercise during treatment = safe and beneficial for most |
| **Contraindications** | Platelets <50,000; Hb <10 g/dL; WBC <3,000; severe pain; acute infection; severe nausea/vomiting; ataxia/dizziness; bone pain; unusual fatigue |

## 九、Age-Specific Populations

### Children & Adolescents (Ch11)

| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Aerobic | >=60 min/day moderate-vigorous; 3+ d/wk vigorous |
| Resistance | >=3 d/wk; focus on technique; bodyweight -> light loads |
| Bone Strengthening | >=3 d/wk (running, jumping, sports) |
| **Key Points** | Growth plate safety: avoid maximal loads; qualified supervision essential; fun and variety for adherence; RPE/OMNI scales appropriate; limit screen time <2h/day; hydration and heat management crucial; avoid overuse/burnout; periodized programs safe for youth |

### Older Adults (Ch12)

| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Aerobic Frequency | >=5 d/wk moderate or >=3 d/wk vigorous |
| Aerobic Intensity | RPE 5-6 (0-10) moderate; 7-8 vigorous |
| Aerobic Time | 30-60 min/day; >=150 min/wk |
| Resistance Frequency | >=2 d/wk |
| Resistance Intensity | 40-50% 1RM start -> 60-80% 1RM; power: 30-60% 1RM |
| Flexibility | >=2 d/wk; hold 30-60s |
| Balance Training | >=2-3 d/wk |
| **Key Points** | Comprehensive program (aerobic + resistance + balance + flexibility); slow progression; fall risk assessment; polypharmacy awareness; osteoporosis considerations; functional exercises priority; social engagement enhances adherence |

## 十一、Absolute Contraindications to Exercise (All Populations)

- Unstable angina or acute coronary syndrome
- Uncontrolled hypertension (resting SBP >180 or DBP >110)
- Uncontrolled arrhythmias causing symptoms or hemodynamic compromise
- Severe symptomatic aortic stenosis
- Acute heart failure or decompensated CHF
- Acute pulmonary embolism or pulmonary infarction
- Acute myocarditis, pericarditis, or endocarditis
- Suspected or known dissecting aneurysm
- Acute systemic infection
- Resting SpO2 <88% (without supplemental O2)
- Untreated deep vein thrombosis
- Acute musculoskeletal injury limiting safe movement
- Blood glucose >300 mg/dL with ketones (Type 1 diabetes) or >350 with ketones
- Platelets <50,000/mm3 (cancer patients)
- Severe pain limiting movement
- Recent concussion (until medically cleared)

## 十二、General Exercise Termination Criteria

- Onset of angina or angina-like symptoms
- Drop in SBP >10 mmHg from baseline despite increased workload
- Excessive rise in BP: SBP >250 mmHg or DBP >115 mmHg
- Shortness of breath, wheezing, leg cramps, or claudication
- Signs of poor perfusion: ataxia, dizziness, pallor, cyanosis, cold/clammy skin, nausea
- Failure of HR to increase with increasing workload
- Noticeable change in heart rhythm
- Subject requests to stop
- Physical or verbal manifestation of severe fatigue
- Failure of testing equipment
