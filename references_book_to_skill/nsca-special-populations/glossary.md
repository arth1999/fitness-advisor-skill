# Glossary -- NSCA's Essentials of Training Special Populations

## Core Concepts

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Special Populations | Groups that differ from the general, apparently healthy adult population regarding exercise programming recommendations due to chronic disease, disability, age, or sex-specific conditions |
| Scope of Practice | The professional boundaries within which the exercise professional may operate; includes health appraisals, fitness assessments, and designing/supervising exercise programs |
| Medical Clearance | Authorization from a licensed health care professional for a client to participate in exercise testing and training |
| Inclusive Fitness | An approach where persons with special needs train in mainstream environments rather than specialized secluded settings |
| Supervised Program | Exercise program overseen by a certified fitness professional for clients with medically identified conditions |
| Medically Supervised Program | Exercise program directed and monitored by a health care professional in a clinical setting for high-risk clients |
| ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act) | Legislation prohibiting discrimination against persons with disabilities in workplaces, public accommodations, and exercise facilities |

## Exercise Prescription Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| FITT-VP | Frequency, Intensity, Time, Type, Volume, Progression -- the six elements of exercise prescription |
| HRR (Heart Rate Reserve) | HRmax - HRrest; used to prescribe exercise intensity |
| 1RM (One-Repetition Maximum) | The maximum weight that can be lifted once with proper form |
| RPE (Rating of Perceived Exertion) | Subjective rating of exercise effort, typically Borg 6-20 or OMNI 0-10 scale |
| MET (Metabolic Equivalent) | 1 MET = 3.5 mL O2/kg/min; measure of exercise intensity |
| Ischemic Threshold | The heart rate and blood pressure level at which angina symptoms occur |
| Intermittent Claudication (IC) | Aching/cramping in legs during exercise due to insufficient blood flow (PAD), relieved by rest |
| Talk Test | The intensity at which a person can "just barely respond in conversation"; considered safe for aerobic improvement |
| Graded Exercise Therapy (GET) | Gradual, progressive exercise starting at very low levels, used in CFS |
| HIIT (High-Intensity Interval Training) | Alternating high-intensity exercise with rest/low-intensity periods |

## Medical Conditions & Pathophysiology

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Atherosclerosis | Narrowing and hardening of arteries due to plaque buildup; underlying cause of most cardiovascular conditions |
| Insulin Resistance | Reduced sensitivity of cells to insulin; precursor to type 2 diabetes |
| HbA1c (Glycated Hemoglobin) | Blood test reflecting average blood glucose over 2-3 months; >=6.5% indicates diabetes |
| Ketoacidosis | Dangerous condition where ketones accumulate from fat/protein metabolism due to lack of insulin |
| Hypoglycemia | Blood glucose <70 mg/dl; common exercise risk in diabetes |
| Silent Ischemia | Insufficient blood flow to heart without clinical signs or symptoms; common in diabetes |
| Hypertension (HTN) | SBP >=140 or DBP >=90 mmHg on two separate occasions |
| Prehypertension | SBP 120-139 or DBP 80-89 mmHg |
| Ejection Fraction (EF) | Percentage of blood pumped out of left ventricle per beat; <35% indicates systolic heart failure |
| EIB (Exercise-Induced Bronchoconstriction) | Transient airway narrowing triggered by exercise |
| BHR (Bronchial Hyperresponsiveness) | Exaggerated bronchoconstriction response to stimuli |
| FEV1 (Forced Expiratory Volume in 1 second) | Lung function measure; key diagnostic indicator for obstructive vs. restrictive disease |
| FVC (Forced Vital Capacity) | Total air exhaled after maximal inhalation |
| COPD | Progressive lung disease with emphysema and chronic bronchitis; airflow obstruction |
| CRPD (Chronic Restrictive Pulmonary Disease) | Decreased total lung capacity; FEV1/FVC ratio >80% |
| CF (Cystic Fibrosis) | Multiorgan genetic disease from CFTR mutation; thick secretions, pulmonary infection |
| PH/PAH (Pulmonary Hypertension/Arterial) | Mean PAP >25 mmHg at rest; right heart strain |
| RA (Rheumatoid Arthritis) | Autoimmune inflammatory disease primarily affecting joints |
| SLE (Systemic Lupus Erythematosus) | Multisystem autoimmune disease with acute/chronic inflammation |
| CFS (Chronic Fatigue Syndrome) | Persistent medically unexplained fatigue >=6 months, unrelieved by rest |
| FM (Fibromyalgia) | Chronic widespread musculoskeletal pain with fatigue |
| HIV/AIDS | Retrovirus destroying CD4 T-cells; progressive immune deficiency |
| MS (Multiple Sclerosis) | Demyelinating neurological disease; progressive or relapsing-remitting |
| SCI (Spinal Cord Injury) | Damage to spinal cord causing sensory/motor loss below injury level |
| ASD (Autism Spectrum Disorder) | Neurodevelopmental disorder affecting communication and behavior |
| Female Athlete Triad | Interrelationship of low energy availability, menstrual dysfunction, and low BMD |

## Medication Classes & Exercise Effects

| Drug Class | Common Examples | Primary Action | Exercise Considerations |
|------------|-----------------|----------------|------------------------|
| Beta-blockers | metoprolol, atenolol, carvedilol | Lower HR and BP; block sympathetic nervous system | Blunted HR response; use RPE for intensity; watch for postural hypotension |
| ACE Inhibitors | lisinopril, enalapril, ramipril | Reduce BP via vasodilation; reduce afterload | May cause postural hypotension; monitor BP |
| ARBs | losartan, valsartan | Block angiotensin II; vasodilation | Similar to ACE inhibitors |
| Calcium Channel Blockers | amlodipine, diltiazem, nifedipine | Vasodilation; some reduce HR | May cause postural hypotension; dizziness possible |
| Diuretics (Thiazides) | hydrochlorothiazide, chlorthalidone | Reduce blood volume; lower BP | Dehydration risk; heat intolerance; electrolyte imbalance |
| Statins | atorvastatin, simvastatin, rosuvastatin | Lower LDL cholesterol | Myalgia/muscle damage risk, especially with exercise; 10-25% of users affected; may blunt VO2max improvements |
| Nitrates | nitroglycerin, isosorbide | Vasodilation; reduce myocardial O2 demand | Postural hypotension; carry rescue nitro during exercise |
| Antiarrhythmics | digoxin, amiodarone | Control heart rhythm | May alter exercise HR response |
| Anticoagulants | warfarin, clopidogrel, apixaban | Prevent clot formation | Bleeding risk with trauma/falls; monitor for bruising |
| Insulin (Rapid) | lispro, aspart, glulisine | Lower blood glucose within 15 min, duration 2-4h | Hypoglycemia risk during exercise; adjust timing/dose/CHO intake |
| Insulin (Short) | regular insulin | Onset ~30 min, duration 3-6h | Avoid exercise during peak action |
| Insulin (Long) | glargine, detemir | Steady 24h coverage | Lower nocturnal hypoglycemia risk vs intermediate |
| Metformin | Glucophage | Reduce hepatic glucose output; improve insulin sensitivity | Low hypoglycemia risk alone; GI side effects |
| Sulfonylureas | glipizide, glyburide | Stimulate pancreatic insulin secretion | Higher hypoglycemia risk during exercise |
| SABA (Short-Acting Beta-Agonists) | albuterol, levalbuterol | Rapid bronchodilation; rescue medication | Use 15 min pre-exercise; tachycardia possible |
| LABA (Long-Acting Beta-Agonists) | salmeterol, formoterol | 12-24h bronchodilation; controller medication | Not for acute rescue; daily maintenance |
| ICS (Inhaled Corticosteroids) | fluticasone, budesonide | Airway anti-inflammation | No direct exercise effect; rinse mouth after use |
| Anticholinergics | ipratropium, tiotropium | Bronchodilation via muscarinic blockade | Dry mouth; no significant exercise effect |
| Oral Corticosteroids | prednisone, prednisolone | Systemic immunosuppression; anti-inflammatory | Muscle weakness, weight gain, osteoporosis risk, hypertension with long-term use |
| NSAIDs | ibuprofen, naproxen, celecoxib | Anti-inflammatory; pain relief | May reduce exercise-induced muscle soreness; possible inhibition of muscle protein synthesis adaptation |
| DMARDs | methotrexate, sulfasalazine | Disease-modifying; slow RA progression | Immunosuppression; infection risk |
| Biologics | etanercept, adalimumab, infliximab | Block specific immune pathways (TNF-alpha) | Immunosuppression; infection risk |
| Antidepressants (SSRIs/SNRIs) | fluoxetine, duloxetine | Affect serotonin/norepinephrine | May affect HR response; fatigue/drowsiness possible |
| Stimulants | modafinil, methylphenidate | Increase alertness | May increase HR/BP; chronotropic incompetence risk |
| Erythropoietin-Stimulating Agents | epoetin alfa | Treat anemia in CKD | Improved O2 carrying capacity; improved exercise tolerance |
| Thyroid Hormone Replacement | levothyroxine | Normalize thyroid levels | When euthyroid: normal exercise response; over-treatment: tachycardia, elevated BP |
| ART (Antiretroviral Therapy) | NRTIs, NNRTIs, PIs, integrase inhibitors | Suppress HIV viral load | Lipodystrophy, insulin resistance, dyslipidemia, CVD risk; exercise beneficial for metabolic side effects |
