# Chapter 15: Cardiorespiratory Fitness Training

## Learning Objectives
- Summarize the importance of cardiorespiratory fitness training for different client types
- Outline the general guidelines for cardiorespiratory fitness training
- Explain stage training and its use to improve cardiorespiratory endurance
- Identify cardiorespiratory training considerations for clients with postural conditions

## Components of Health-Related Fitness
1. Cardiorespiratory fitness
2. Muscular strength
3. Muscular endurance
4. Flexibility
5. Body composition

Cardiorespiratory fitness is a strong predictor of morbidity and mortality. Poor cardiorespiratory fitness is related to a marked increase in premature death risk.

## FITTE-VP Principle
- **Frequency**: Number of training sessions per time period
- **Intensity**: Level of demand placed on the body
- **Type**: Mode of activity
- **Time**: Duration of each session
- **Enjoyment**: Activities the client likes
- **Volume**: Total amount of work (frequency × time)
- **Progression**: Rate at which these variables are increased

### General Recommendations
- Moderate intensity: At least 5 days/week, 150 minutes/week
- Vigorous intensity: At least 3 days/week, 75 minutes/week
- Combination: 3–5 days/week of moderate + vigorous

## Intensity Monitoring Methods
- **VO2max** / **VO2R** (oxygen uptake reserve)
- **%HRmax** and **%HRR (Karvonen method)**: HRR = HRmax − RHR; Target HR = (HRR × %intensity) + RHR
- **Metabolic equivalents (METs)**: 1 MET = 3.5 ml O2/kg/min (resting)
- **Rating of Perceived Exertion (RPE)**: 1–10 modified scale or 6–20 Borg scale
- **Talk test**: Below VT1 = comfortable talking; At VT1 = talking is first challenged; Above VT2 = unable to talk comfortably

## Three-Zone Training Model
Cardiorespiratory training zones based on ventilatory thresholds:

### Zone 1 (Below VT1)
- **Intensity**: Low-to-moderate; walking, light cycling
- **Heart rate**: < ~75% HRmax (varies by individual)
- **Fuel usage**: Mostly fat; some carbohydrate
- **RPE**: 1–4 (modified 1–10 scale)
- **Talk test**: Comfortable conversation
- **Purpose**: Build aerobic base, recovery, general health

### Zone 2 (Between VT1 and VT2)
- **Intensity**: Moderate-to-vigorous; jogging, steady running
- **Heart rate**: ~75–85% HRmax
- **Fuel usage**: Progressive shift from fat -> 50/50 mix -> more carbohydrate
- **RPE**: 5–6
- **Talk test**: Can speak in short sentences; not comfortable conversation
- **Purpose**: Improve aerobic capacity, lactate threshold

### Zone 3 (Above VT2)
- **Intensity**: Vigorous-to-maximal; sprinting, high-intensity intervals
- **Heart rate**: > ~85% HRmax
- **Fuel usage**: Primarily carbohydrate
- **RPE**: 7–10
- **Talk test**: Unable to speak comfortably
- **Purpose**: Increase anaerobic capacity, speed, power, peak performance

## Stage Training for Cardiorespiratory Programming

### Stage I (Aerobic Base)
- Use Zone 1 primarily; may include brief Zone 2 intervals
- Focus: Build a baseline; develop cardiorespiratory endurance
- Frequency: 3–5 days/week
- Walk-jog progression for beginners

### Stage II (Aerobic Efficiency)
- Introduce Zone 2 intervals; build up Zone 2 duration
- Begin interval training: 1 minute Zone 2 / 3 minutes Zone 1 recovery
- Focus: Improve aerobic capacity and efficiency

### Stage III (Aerobic Power)
- Include Zone 3 high-intensity intervals
- Example: 30–60 seconds Zone 3 / 2–4 minutes active recovery
- Focus: Increase anaerobic capacity and peak performance
- Only appropriate for advanced clients

## Postural Considerations
- **Forward head / rounded shoulders**: Avoid excessive stationary cycling (promotes forward posture); prefer treadmill walking/jogging (promotes upright posture)
- **Lower crossed syndrome**: Avoid stair climbers that may reinforce hip flexor dominance
- Implement flexibility and corrective exercises for postural issues alongside cardio training

## Cool-Down
- Gradually reduce intensity for 5–10 minutes
- Allows cardiovascular system to return to resting state
- Prevents blood pooling
- Best time for static stretching

## Key Definitions
- **Cardiorespiratory fitness**: Ability of the circulatory and respiratory systems to provide the body with oxygen during activity
- **Rate of progression**: The process and speed at which frequency, intensity, time, and type are increased
- **VT1**: Ventilatory threshold 1 -- first point where ventilation increases disproportionately to oxygen consumption; shift to more carb usage
- **VT2**: Ventilatory threshold 2 -- second disproportionate increase; highest sustainable intensity before respiratory compensation
