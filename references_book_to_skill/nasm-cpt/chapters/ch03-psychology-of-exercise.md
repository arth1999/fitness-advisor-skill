# Chapter 3: The Psychology of Exercise

## Learning Objectives
- Describe common reasons why people avoid regular exercise
- Evaluate various social influences on exercise adherence
- Recommend the best forms of support to help clients adhere to exercise
- Identify the psychological benefits of regular exercise

## Psychology and Exercise
**Psychology** is the area of science focusing on how the mind and feelings influence behaviors. Understanding psychology is essential for CPTs to help clients adopt and maintain exercise habits.

## Motivation
### Types of Motivation
- **Amotivation**: Complete lack of motivation; person sees no reason to exercise
- **Extrinsic Motivation**: Engaging in exercise for external rewards (appearance, praise, avoiding guilt)
- **Intrinsic Motivation**: Engaging in exercise for inherent satisfaction and enjoyment. Associated with long-term adherence.

### Understanding Motivation Differences
- Motivation is dynamic, not static
- Socioeconomic status influences access and motivation
- CPTs should foster autonomy and competence to build intrinsic motivation
- People progress along a motivation continuum

## Common Barriers to Exercise
1. **Time** — perceived or real lack of time; most cited barrier
2. **Unrealistic Goals** — outcome goals (lose 30 lb) vs. process goals (exercise 3x/week); process goals are more effective
3. **Lack of Social Support** — no accountability or encouragement
4. **Social Physique Anxiety** — fear of judgment in exercise settings
5. **Convenience** — access to facilities, equipment, or safe spaces
6. **Ambivalence** — mixed feelings about change; wanting and not wanting simultaneously

## Social Influences on Exercise

### Types of Social Support
- **Instrumental Support**: Tangible assistance (childcare, transportation, equipment)
- **Emotional Support**: Empathy, caring, encouragement, active listening
- **Informational Support**: Directions, advice, suggestions from credible sources
- **Companionship Support**: Exercising with others; social connection

### Group Influences
- **Family**: Powerful influence on exercise habits; both positive and negative
- **Social networks**: Peer behavior shapes individual behavior
- **Exercise groups**: Provide all four types of support simultaneously

## Psychological Benefits of Exercise
- Reduced depression and anxiety symptoms
- Improved mood and stress management
- Enhanced cognitive function
- Improved self-esteem and body image
- Better sleep quality
- Release of endorphins (natural mood elevators)

## Key Definitions
- **Adherence**: Level of commitment to a behavior or plan of action
- **Outcome goals**: Focus on end results (e.g., "lose 20 pounds")
- **Process goals**: Focus on behaviors (e.g., "exercise 3 times per week")
- **Empathy**: The ability to understand and share the feelings of another
- **Ambivalence**: A person's state of mixed feelings about a situation
