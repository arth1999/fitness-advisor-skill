# Chapter 23: Chronic Health Conditions and Special Populations

## Learning Objectives
- Identify common chronic health conditions and special populations
- Summarize exercise considerations and modifications for each condition
- Apply appropriate programming within the OPT model for special populations
- Recognize when to refer to medical professionals

## Scope and Referral
CPTs work with apparently healthy clients or those medically cleared for exercise. Always:
- Obtain medical clearance when indicated by PAR-Q+ or health screening
- Refer to appropriate healthcare professionals when outside scope
- Communicate with the client's healthcare team when appropriate (with consent)
- Understand contraindications for each condition

## Chronic Health Conditions

### Cardiovascular Disease and Hypertension
- **SBP ≥ 130 or DBP ≥ 80** = Hypertension
- **Considerations**: Avoid Valsalva maneuver (breath-holding), monitor RPE and HR, avoid isometric exercises, progress slowly
- **Program**: Start Phase 1; Zone 1 cardio initially; avoid heavy lifting early; monitor blood pressure response
- **Contraindicated**: Heavy resistance training, isometric exercises, rapid position changes

### Type 2 Diabetes
- **Considerations**: Monitor blood glucose before/during/after exercise; have fast-acting carbs available; check feet for blisters/sores
- **Program**: Phase 1; focus on cardiorespiratory and resistance training; consistency is critical; post-meal exercise may help control glucose
- **Warning signs**: Hypoglycemia (shakiness, confusion, sweating); stop exercise and treat

### Obesity (BMI ≥ 30)
- **Considerations**: May have orthopedic limitations; avoid high-impact initially; focus on adherence and enjoyment
- **Program**: Phase 1; long warm-up/cool-down; water-based exercise for reduced joint stress; gradual progression; Zone 1–2 cardio
- **Emphasis on**: Non-exercise activity thermogenesis (NEAT), lifestyle modification alongside training

### Osteoporosis and Osteopenia
- **Considerations**: Avoid spinal flexion (crunching), excessive spinal rotation, high-impact activities for severe cases
- **Program**: Weight-bearing exercise; resistance training (especially for spine and hips); balance training to prevent falls; Phase 1–2
- **Key**: Impact and loading stimulate bone density; walking, resistance training, balance work

### Arthritis (Osteoarthritis and Rheumatoid Arthritis)
- **Considerations**: Avoid exercise during acute flare-ups; respect pain; warm up thoroughly
- **Program**: Low-impact; water-based exercise; ROM exercises; Phase 1; muscle strengthening around affected joints
- **Key**: Motion is lotion; maintain joint mobility without exacerbating pain

### Cancer
- **Considerations**: Highly variable based on treatment status, type, and individual response; always work with medical team
- **Program**: Light-to-moderate intensity; focus on maintaining function and quality of life; Phase 1
- **Key**: Exercise improves fatigue, quality of life, and treatment tolerance

### Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD)
- **Considerations**: Shortness of breath; use RPE and dyspnea scale; pursed-lip breathing
- **Program**: Low-to-moderate intensity; interval-style cardio (work:rest); upper body endurance exercises; Phase 1
- **Key**: Upper body strength improves breathing efficiency; avoid exercising in polluted air/cold

### Pregnancy (Pre- and Post-Natal)
- **Considerations**: Medical clearance required; avoid supine exercises after first trimester; avoid Valsalva; monitor thermoregulation
- **Program**: Phase 1 primarily; moderate intensity; avoid contact sports, fall risk, excessive heat
- **Post-natal**: Gradual return to exercise; address diastasis recti; pelvic floor rehabilitation

## Special Populations

### Youth (Ages 6–17)
- **Considerations**: Focus on fun, skill development, and variety; avoid maximal lifts before skeletal maturity
- **Program**: Body weight and light resistance; SAQ; sports-specific skill; Phase 1 with supervision
- **Key**: Build motor skills, confidence, and positive relationship with exercise

### Older Adults / Active Aging
- **Considerations**: Polypharmacy (multiple medications); decreased balance; decreased recovery ability; slower adaptation
- **Program**: Phase 1–2; emphasis on balance training, functional strength, bone-loading activities; Zone 1–2 cardio
- **Key**: Fall prevention is a top priority; maintain independence in ADLs

### Low Back Pain
- **Considerations**: Identify cause; avoid painful movements; strengthen core stabilizers
- **Program**: Phase 1 stabilization emphasis; core drawing-in maneuver; avoid loaded spinal flexion; walking for cardio
- **Key**: The LPHC stabilization system is the focus; address muscle imbalances in lower crossed syndrome

## General Programming Guidelines for Special Populations
1. **Always start with Phase 1** (Stabilization Endurance)
2. **Progress more slowly** than with apparently healthy clients
3. **Prioritize form and safety** over intensity
4. **Monitor closely** and adjust based on individual response
5. **Communicate** with the client's healthcare team
6. **Emphasize consistency** and long-term adherence over rapid results
7. **Celebrate small wins** to build self-efficacy
