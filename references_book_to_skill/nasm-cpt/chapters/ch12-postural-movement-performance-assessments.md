# Chapter 12: Postural, Movement, and Performance Assessments

## Learning Objectives
- Identify the rationale for posture, movement, and performance assessments
- Identify the steps for conducting posture, movement, and performance assessments
- Interpret results from posture, movement, and performance assessments
- Summarize the use of fitness assessments as a tool to build rapport and credibility

## Importance of Posture
**Static posture**: Positioning of the musculoskeletal system while standing still
**Dynamic posture**: Alignment of the body while in motion
**Optimal movement**: Moving in a biomechanically efficient manner that maximizes muscle recruitment and minimizes injury risk

Poor posture from lack of awareness and excessive sitting affects the length-tension relationships of muscles and increases stress on joints.

## Muscle Imbalances
Altered length-tension relationships between muscles on each side of a joint:
- **Overactive muscles**: Elevated neural drive; held in chronic state of contraction; force compensation
- **Underactive muscles**: Inhibited neural drive; pulled into chronically elongated state; allow compensation

A postural deviation identified in static assessment will typically also appear during movement assessments.

## Static Postural Assessment
Client stands in natural, relaxed stance. View from three positions:

### Anterior View
- Head: tilted or neutral?
- Shoulders: level or uneven?
- Arms: same distance from body on both sides?
- Feet: pronated (flat), supinated (high arch), or neutral?
- Knees: adducted, abducted, or neutral?

### Lateral View
- Head: forward head posture?
- Cervical spine: excessive lordosis?
- Thoracic spine: excessive kyphosis?
- Shoulders: rounded forward?
- Lumbar spine: excessive lordosis or flat back?
- Pelvis: anterior or posterior pelvic tilt?
- Knees: hyperextended or flexed?

### Posterior View
- Head: tilted or neutral?
- Shoulders: elevated, depressed, or neutral?
- Scapulae: winged or protracted?
- Spine: scoliosis (side-to-side curvature)?
- Hips: level or uneven?
- Feet: Achilles tendon straight or bowing?

## Common Postural Distortion Patterns
1. **Upper Crossed Syndrome**: Forward head, rounded shoulders, increased thoracic kyphosis
   - Overactive: Upper trapezius, levator scapulae, sternocleidomastoid, pectorals
   - Underactive: Deep cervical flexors, lower trapezius, serratus anterior, rhomboids

2. **Lower Crossed Syndrome**: Anterior pelvic tilt, increased lumbar lordosis
   - Overactive: Hip flexors (iliopsoas, rectus femoris), erector spinae
   - Underactive: Gluteus maximus, hamstrings, abdominals

3. **Pes Planus Distortion Syndrome**: Flat feet, knee valgus, internally rotated femurs
   - Overactive: Peroneals, gastrocnemius, soleus, adductors, TFL, iliopsoas
   - Underactive: Posterior tibialis, anterior tibialis, gluteus medius/maximus

## Movement Assessments

### Overhead Squat Assessment (OHSA)
Client performs bodyweight squat with arms overhead. Assess from anterior, lateral, and posterior views.

**Key observations and common compensations:**
- **Feet turn out**: Overactive gastrocnemius/soleus, biceps femoris (short head), TFL
- **Knees move inward (valgus)**: Overactive adductors, TFL, biceps femoris (short head), vastus lateralis
- **Excessive forward lean**: Overactive hip flexors, gastrocnemius/soleus, abdominals
- **Low back arches**: Overactive hip flexors, erector spinae, latissimus dorsi
- **Arms fall forward**: Overactive pectorals, latissimus dorsi, teres major

### Single-Leg Squat Assessment
Client performs squat on single leg. Assesses unilateral stability and balance. Key observations: knee valgus, hip drop, excessive trunk lean.

### Pushing Assessment
Standing cable chest press or push-up. Assess from lateral view. Key observation: shoulder elevation/scapular winging, forward head, lumbar extension.

### Pulling Assessment
Standing cable row. Assess from lateral view. Key observation: shoulder elevation, lumbar extension, forward head.

## Performance Assessments
- **Push-up test**: Muscular endurance (max reps in 60 seconds)
- **Bench press or squat strength test**: Estimate 1RM from submaximal reps
- **Vertical jump test**: Lower-body power
- **40-yard dash**: Speed
- **Pro agility (5-10-5)**: Agility and change of direction
- **Davies test**: Upper-body agility and stabilization (timed alternating hand touches)

## Key Definitions
- **Pes planus**: Collapsed arch of the foot (flat feet)
- **Overactive**: When elevated neural drive causes a muscle to be held in a chronic state of contraction
- **Underactive**: When a muscle experiences neural inhibition and limited neuromuscular recruitment
- **Movement assessment**: Assessment of movement patterns and postural alignment during movement (dynamic postural assessment)
- **Mobility**: Optimal flexibility and joint range of motion; ability to move freely
