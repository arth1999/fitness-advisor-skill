# Chapter 4: Behavioral Coaching

## Learning Objectives
- Summarize client expectations of a Certified Personal Trainer and the fitness environment
- Assess a client's stage in changing unwanted behaviors
- Structure initial client sessions for optimal effectiveness
- Communicate effectively and build client rapport
- Implement behavioral change techniques to enhance exercise adherence

## Client Expectations
Clients expect CPTs to be:
- Positive, professional, and prepared
- Strong communicators who actively listen
- Educators who explain the "why" behind exercises
- Motivators who foster confidence and competence
- Respectful of client autonomy and preferences

### The Third Space
A communal space separate from home or work where the client experiences their own sense of identity and relationship to others. The gym/fitness environment serves as a third space for many clients.

## First Impressions
Essential elements:
- Eye contact (if culturally accepted)
- Professional appearance and hygiene
- Punctuality and preparedness
- Active listening and use of client's name
- Positive body language and energy

## Stages of Change (Transtheoretical Model)
1. **Precontemplation** — no intention to change in the next 6 months
2. **Contemplation** — intends to change within 6 months; aware of pros but also cons
3. **Preparation** — intends to take action within 30 days; has a plan
4. **Action** — has made specific behavior changes within the past 6 months
5. **Maintenance** — has sustained change for 6+ months; working to prevent relapse

### CPT Strategies by Stage
- **Precontemplation**: Educate on risks; plant seeds of awareness
- **Contemplation**: Reinforce pros; address perceived barriers
- **Preparation**: Help create a concrete, realistic plan
- **Action**: Provide accountability, feedback, and support
- **Maintenance**: Celebrate successes; develop relapse prevention strategies

## Structuring Initial Sessions
1. **Pre-session preparation**: Review intake forms, PAR-Q+, goals
2. **Rapport building**: Get to know the client personally
3. **Assessment**: Conduct fitness and movement assessments
4. **Goal setting**: Collaborative, SMART goals
5. **Program overview**: Explain the "what" and "why"
6. **First workout**: Light to moderate; build confidence
7. **Debrief and next steps**: Schedule, expectations, homework

## Communication Skills
- **Active listening**: Full attention, paraphrasing, clarifying questions
- **Nonverbal communication**: Body language, facial expressions, tone
- **Empathy**: Understanding client perspective without judgment
- **Motivational interviewing**: Client-centered approach to explore and resolve ambivalence

## Behavior Change Techniques (BCTs)
- **Self-monitoring**: Tracking exercise, food, progress
- **Goal setting**: SMART (Specific, Measurable, Attainable, Relevant, Time-bound)
- **Feedback**: Regular, specific, constructive
- **Social support**: Leveraging family, friends, workout partners
- **Stimulus control**: Managing environmental triggers
- **Cognitive restructuring**: Reframing negative thoughts

## Key Definitions
- **Self-efficacy**: One's belief they can complete a task; also called self-confidence
- **Autonomy**: Acting in accordance with how one wants to behave
- **Behavior change techniques (BCTs)**: Client interventions used to change determinants of behavior
- **Physical activity**: Bodily movement resulting in energy expenditure; not necessarily structured exercise
