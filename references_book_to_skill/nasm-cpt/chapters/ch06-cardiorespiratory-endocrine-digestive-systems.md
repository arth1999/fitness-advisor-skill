# Chapter 6: The Cardiorespiratory, Endocrine, and Digestive Systems

## Learning Objectives
- Identify the integrated structure and function of the cardiorespiratory system
- Categorize the basic structure and function of the endocrine system
- Summarize the basic structure and function of the digestive system

## The Cardiorespiratory System
The combination of the **cardiovascular system** (heart, blood vessels, blood) and the **respiratory system** (airways, lungs). These systems work together to deliver oxygen and nutrients and remove waste products like CO2.

### The Cardiovascular System
#### Heart Structure
- Four chambers: right atrium, right ventricle, left atrium, left ventricle
- **Pulmonic side (right)**: Receives deoxygenated blood from body, pumps to lungs
- **Systemic side (left)**: Receives oxygenated blood from lungs, pumps to body
- Cardiac muscle is **involuntary** and striated
- Located in the mediastinum within the thoracic cavity

#### Blood Flow Path
Body → Superior/inferior vena cava → Right atrium → Tricuspid valve → Right ventricle → Pulmonary valve → Pulmonary arteries → Lungs → Pulmonary veins → Left atrium → Mitral valve → Left ventricle → Aortic valve → Aorta → Body

#### Cardiac Output (Q)
Q = Heart Rate (HR) x Stroke Volume (SV)
- **Heart rate**: Beats per minute
- **Stroke volume**: Amount of blood pumped per beat
- **Cardiac output**: Total blood pumped per minute

#### Blood Vessels
- **Arteries**: Carry blood away from heart; thick, muscular walls
- **Arterioles**: Small branches of arteries; control blood flow
- **Capillaries**: Microscopic vessels; site of gas/nutrient exchange
- **Venules**: Small veins collecting blood from capillaries
- **Veins**: Carry blood back to heart; contain valves

### The Respiratory System
#### Structures
- **Conducting zone**: Nose, pharynx, larynx, trachea, bronchi, bronchioles — transport air
- **Respiratory zone**: Alveoli — gas exchange occurs here

#### Respiration Process
1. **Inspiration (inhalation)**: Diaphragm contracts (flattens), external intercostals lift ribs, thoracic volume increases, air enters
2. **Expiration (exhalation)**: Diaphragm and intercostals relax, volume decreases, air exits

#### Key Respiratory Volumes
- **Tidal volume**: Normal breathing volume
- **Vital capacity**: Maximum exhaled after maximum inhalation
- **Residual volume**: Air remaining after maximal exhalation

#### Oxygen Transport
- ~98% of O2 transported bound to hemoglobin in red blood cells
- CO2 transported as bicarbonate ions (majority), dissolved in plasma, or bound to hemoglobin

## The Endocrine System
Produces hormones that regulate bodily functions including metabolism, growth, reproduction, and stress response.

### Key Endocrine Glands
- **Hypothalamus**: Master controller; links nervous and endocrine systems
- **Pituitary gland**: "Master gland"; controls other glands
- **Thyroid**: Metabolism regulation (T3, T4)
- **Adrenal glands**: Cortisol (stress), aldosterone (fluid balance), epinephrine/norepinephrine (fight/flight)
- **Pancreas**: Insulin (lowers blood glucose), glucagon (raises blood glucose)
- **Gonads**: Testosterone (testes), estrogen/progesterone (ovaries)

### Exercise-Related Hormones
- **Testosterone**: Anabolic; promotes muscle growth and strength
- **Growth hormone**: Tissue growth and repair
- **Cortisol**: Catabolic when chronically elevated; stress response
- **Insulin**: Nutrient storage; anabolic
- **Glucagon**: Mobilizes stored energy
- **Epinephrine/norepinephrine**: Increase heart rate, blood flow to muscles

## The Digestive System
Breaks down food into absorbable nutrients.

### Structures (in order)
Mouth → Esophagus → Stomach → Small intestine (duodenum, jejunum, ileum) → Large intestine (colon) → Rectum → Anus

### Accessory Organs
- **Liver**: Produces bile; processes nutrients; detoxification
- **Gallbladder**: Stores and concentrates bile
- **Pancreas**: Produces digestive enzymes; bicarbonate to neutralize stomach acid

### Nutrient Absorption
- **Carbohydrates**: Broken down to monosaccharides; absorbed in small intestine
- **Proteins**: Broken down to amino acids; absorbed in small intestine
- **Fats**: Emulsified by bile; absorbed as fatty acids and glycerol in small intestine

## Key Definitions
- **Mediastinum**: Space in the chest between lungs containing the heart
- **Cardiac muscle**: Involuntary, striated muscle of the heart
- **Sinoatrial (SA) node**: Heart's natural pacemaker
- **Arteriovenous oxygen difference (a-vO2 diff)**: Difference in O2 content between arterial and venous blood; reflects tissue O2 extraction
