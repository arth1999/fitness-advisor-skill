# NASM CPT 7th Edition — Quick Reference Cheatsheet

## OPT Model at a Glance

| | Phase 1 | Phase 2 | Phase 3 | Phase 4 | Phase 5 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Name** | Stabilization Endurance | Strength Endurance | Muscular Development | Maximal Strength | Power |
| **Reps** | 12–20 | 8–12 | 6–12 | 1–5 | 1–5 / 8–10 |
| **Sets** | 1–3 | 2–4 | 3–5 | 4–6 | 3–5 |
| **Intensity** | 50–70% | 70–80% | 75–85% | 85–100% | 85–100% / 30–45% |
| **Rest** | 0–90 sec | 0–60 sec | 0–60 sec | 3–5 min | 3–5 min |
| **Tempo** | 4/2/1 | 2/0/2 + 4/2/1 | 2/0/2 | X/X/X | X/X/X |
| **Duration** | 2–6 weeks | 4–8 weeks | 4–8 weeks | 4–8 weeks | 4–8 weeks |
| **Method** | High reps, light load, unstable | Superset (strength + stability) | Moderate reps, mod-heavy load | Heavy, low reps, full rest | Superset (heavy + explosive) |

## Integrated Training Components by Phase

| Component | Phase 1 | Phases 2–4 | Phase 5 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Flexibility** | SMR → Static | SMR → Active → Dynamic | SMR → Dynamic |
| **Core** | Stabilization (plank, bridge) | Strength (ball crunch, cable rotation) | Power (med ball throws) |
| **Balance** | Stabilization (SL balance) | Strength (SL squat, SL RDL) | Power (hops with stabilization) |
| **Plyometric** | Stabilization (jump + hold) | Strength (continuous jumps) | Power (depth jumps, box-to-box) |
| **SAQ** | Stabilization (controlled drills) | Strength (directional changes) | Power (timed sprints, reactive) |
| **Resistance** | See table above | See table above | See table above |
| **Cardio** | Zone 1, Stage I | Zone 1–2, Stage II | Zone 2–3, Stage III |

## Cardiorespiratory Zones

| Zone | HR | RPE | Talk Test | Fuel | Stage |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Zone 1** | < ~75% HRmax | 1–4 | Comfortable talking | Mostly fat | Stage I |
| **Zone 2** | ~75–85% HRmax | 5–6 | Short sentences only | Mixed/More carb | Stage II |
| **Zone 3** | > ~85% HRmax | 7–10 | Cannot speak | Nearly all carb | Stage III |

**Heart Rate Formulas:**
- HRmax = 208 − (0.7 × age) _(Tanaka formula)_ or 220 − age
- Target HR = (HRmax − RHR) × %intensity + RHR _(Karvonen / HRR method)_

## Three Postural Distortions

| Distortion | Overactive | Underactive |
|---|---|---|
| **Upper Crossed** | Upper traps, levator scap, SCM, pecs | Deep cervical flexors, lower traps, serratus anterior, rhomboids |
| **Lower Crossed** | Hip flexors, erector spinae, lats | Glute max, hamstrings, abdominals |
| **Pes Planus** | Peroneals, gastroc/soleus, adductors, TFL | Post. tibialis, ant. tibialis, glute med/max |

## Overhead Squat Assessment — Quick Reference

| Compensation | Likely Overactive | Likely Underactive |
|---|---|---|
| Feet turn out | Gastroc/soleus, biceps femoris (short), TFL | — |
| Knees cave in | Adductors, TFL, biceps femoris (short), vastus lat | Glute med/max, VMO |
| Forward lean | Hip flexors, gastroc/soleus, abs | Glute max |
| Low back arches | Hip flexors, erector spinae, lats | Glute max, hamstrings, abs |
| Arms fall forward | Pecs, lats, teres major | Lower traps, serratus anterior, post. deltoid |

## Flexibility Continuum

| Technique | Mechanism | Hold/Reps | Best Phase |
|---|---|---|---|
| **SMR (Foam Roll)** | Autogenic inhibition | 30–60 sec tender areas | All phases |
| **Static Stretching** | Autogenic inhibition + creep | 30+ sec hold | Phase 1; cool-down all phases |
| **Active Stretching** | Reciprocal inhibition | 5–10 reps × 1–2 sec | Phase 2–4 warm-up |
| **Dynamic Stretching** | Reciprocal inhibition + temp | 10–15 reps per movement | Phase 2–5 warm-up |

## Macrocycle/Mesocycle/Microcycle

- **Macrocycle**: Annual plan (preparatory → competitive → transition)
- **Mesocycle**: Monthly plan (~4 weeks; can be 3–6 weeks)
- **Microcycle**: Weekly plan (~7 days; can be up to 10 days)

## CPT Scope of Practice

**CAN Do:**
- Design safe, effective, individualized exercise programs
- Conduct fitness assessments
- Provide general nutrition guidance (for healthy populations)
- Offer basic hydration strategies
- Refer to healthcare professionals
- Coach behavior change, goal setting, and lifestyle modification

**CANNOT Do:**
- Diagnose or treat illness, injury, or disease
- Prescribe specific meal plans
- Prescribe supplements for disease treatment
- Provide physical therapy or rehabilitation
- Advise against healthcare professional recommendations
- Work with clients who have medical conditions without clearance

## Program Design Quick Checklist

```
□ PAR-Q+ completed and reviewed
□ Medical clearance obtained if needed
□ Goals identified (SMART)
□ Static postural assessment done
□ Movement assessment done (OHSA minimum)
□ Performance assessment done (if applicable)
□ OPT phase selected based on goal and ability
□ Acute variables set for resistance training
□ Core, Balance, Plyometric, SAQ exercises assigned at correct level
□ Cardio zone and stage selected
□ Flexibility protocol selected (warm-up and cool-down)
□ Reassessment date set (every 4–6 weeks)
```
