# Chapter 7: ISSN Position Stand on Beta-Hydroxy-Beta-Methylbutyrate (HMB) (2013)

**Source**: Wilson JM, et al. J Int Soc Sports Nutr. 2013;10:6.

## ISSN Position Statements

1. HMB can enhance recovery by attenuating exercise-induced skeletal muscle damage in trained and untrained populations.
2. Consume HMB in close proximity to workout for greatest benefit.
3. HMB is most effective when consumed for 2 weeks prior to a damaging exercise bout.
4. 38 mg/kg/day (~3 g/day for an 80 kg person) enhances skeletal muscle hypertrophy, strength, and power with appropriate exercise prescription.
5. Two forms exist: HMB-Ca (calcium salt) and HMB-FA (free acid). HMB-FA may increase plasma absorption and retention to a greater extent. Research with HMB-FA is in its infancy; insufficient evidence to declare one form superior.
6. HMB increases lean body mass and functionality in elderly, sedentary populations.
7. HMB with a structured exercise program may result in greater declines in fat mass.
8. Mechanisms: Inhibits proteolysis and increases protein synthesis.
9. Chronic consumption is safe in both young and old populations.

## HMB Metabolism

### Endogenous Production
```
Leucine → α-Ketoisocaproate (KIC) → HMB
         (BCAT enzyme)              (KIC dioxygenase, cytosol)
```
- Only ~5% of dietary leucine is converted to HMB
- The remaining ~95% of KIC is metabolized to isovaleryl-CoA in mitochondria
- To obtain 3 g HMB endogenously: would require ~60 g leucine, equivalent to >600 g high-quality protein
- Therefore: dietary supplementation is the only practical way to achieve effective HMB doses

### Absorption Kinetics

| Parameter | HMB-Ca (1 g) | HMB-Ca (3 g) | HMB-FA (0.8 g)* |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tmax | 120 min | 60 min | 30 min |
| Peak plasma | 120 nmol/mL | 487 nmol/mL | ~2x HMB-Ca peak |
| AUC (180 min) | Baseline | Higher (dose) | +91-97% vs. HMB-Ca |
| Half-life | ~2.5 h | ~2.5 h | ~3 h |
| Urinary loss | 14% | 28% | Not different from HMB-Ca |
| Plasma clearance | Baseline | Higher | +25% greater tissue uptake |

*0.8 g HMB-FA provides equivalent HMB to 1.0 g HMB-Ca

**Key pharmacokinetic findings**:
- HMB-FA: Faster absorption (30 vs. 120 min), higher peak (2x), greater AUC (+91-97%), greater tissue clearance (+25%)
- Glucose co-ingestion with HMB-Ca: Delays Tmax by 1 hour; lowers peak concentration (likely due to slowed gastric emptying)
- Despite pharmacokinetic advantages, clinical superiority of HMB-FA over HMB-Ca not yet established

## Safety

- Rats: NOAEL of 3.49-4.16 g/kg/day for 91 days (equivalent to ~50 g/day for 81 kg human, normalized to body surface area)
- Humans: 6 g/day for 1 month → no effect on cholesterol, hemoglobin, white blood cells, blood glucose, liver or kidney function
- Two meta-analyses: HMB alone and HMB + glutamine/arginine → safe, no adverse effects
- Elderly: 2-3 g/day HMB-Ca + amino acids for 1 year → no changes in blood/urine markers of hepatic or renal function, or blood lipids
- One rodent study: 320 mg/kg/day for 1 month → increased fasting insulin (possible decreased insulin sensitivity) — NOT replicated in humans; warrants further investigation

## Evidence by Outcome

### Skeletal Muscle Damage and Recovery

**Overall**: HMB attenuates exercise-induced muscle damage in a dose-dependent and timing-dependent manner.

**Key findings**:
- Nissen 1996 (3 weeks, 1.5 or 3 g/day): CK, LDH, and 3-MH decreased 20-60% in dose-dependent manner
- Jowko 2001 (3 weeks, 3 g/day HMB-Ca + creatine): 26-46% decrease in serum/urine urea nitrogen; CK lowered 189%
- Knitter 2000 (6 weeks, 3 g/day in trained runners): CK decreased ~50% after 20 km run
- Panton 2000 (4 weeks, trained and untrained): CK increased 16% (men) and 46% (women) on placebo; ~0-12% with HMB
- Van Someren 2005 (14 days, 3 g/day + KIC): Completely prevented CK rise; blunted soreness increase

**Factors affecting effectiveness**:
1. **Training status**: Both untrained and trained benefit, but the exercise stimulus must be sufficient to cause damage. Untrained: most exercise programs qualify. Trained: need high-intensity/volume, compound movements.
2. **Duration of pre-supplementation**: 2 weeks minimum; Nissen et al. showed HMB not significant until week 3.
3. **Acute timing**: Wilson 2009 — HMB-Ca 60 min PRE-exercise prevented LDH rise and decreased soreness; POST-exercise HMB had no effect on these markers. Suggests timing matters for acute anti-catabolic effect.
4. **Dose**: 3 g/day superior to 1.5 g/day. 6 g/day not further superior to 3 g/day (one study only).

### Skeletal Muscle Hypertrophy, Strength, and Power

**Untrained Populations**:
- Gains in FFM and strength in as little as 3 weeks
- Effect magnitude diminishes as training adaptations slow (non-periodized programs)
- Nissen 1996 (3 weeks): FFM +0.6%; strength +2.6-17.4% (depending on lift)
- Gallagher 2000 (8 weeks): FFM +3%; FM -1.6%; isometric/isokinetic strength +2-3.5%
- Panton 2000 (4 weeks, men/women, trained/untrained): FFM +0.5 kg; FM -0.6%; bench press +3-15%
- Kraemer 2009 (12 weeks, periodized program, HMB-Ca + arginine + glutamine): LBM +40% greater vs. placebo; FM -40% more; squat strength +50% more; Wingate power +85% more

**Trained Populations**:
- Slower adaptation rate requires longer interventions (>6 weeks) and novel/high-intensity stimuli
- Studies ≤6 weeks: Generally no significant effect (Kreider 1999, 2000; Hoffman 2004; Slater 2001; O'Connor 2007; Ransone 2003)
- Studies >6 weeks: Positive effects (Nissen 1996 7-week: bench press +4.5 kg, squat +3.2 kg vs. placebo in highly trained; Thomson 2009 9-week: FFM +0.4 kg, FM -3.8%, strength +1.1-9.0%)
- **Program design critical**: Periodized programs with constantly changing loading patterns optimize HMB's effect. Linear programs with trained athletes produce minimal results.

**Adolescent Athletes**:
- Portal 2011 (7 weeks, 3 g/day, elite volleyball players 13-18 yrs): FFM +3.7% (placebo no change); FM -6.6% (placebo +3.5%); Wingate peak power +13.5% (placebo +3%); strength +15.7-23.5% (placebo 0-6.7%)
- No changes in hormone status or inflammatory mediators

**Elderly Populations**:
- HMB alone (without exercise): Flakoll 2004 (12 weeks, HMB + arginine + lysine) → increased LBM, leg strength, handgrip strength, decreased "timed up and go" test time
- Baier 2009 (1 year, HMB + arginine + lysine) → significant increase in lean mass (none in placebo); increased protein turnover
- HMB + exercise: Vukovich 2001 (8 weeks, age ~70) → trend toward increased lean mass (p=0.08); 8% decrease in fat mass; strength +15-20% but no difference between HMB and placebo
- Aged rats (Wilson 2012, 16 weeks): HMB prevented age-related body fat gain (~50% increase in controls); preserved muscle fiber size

### Aerobic Performance and Fat Metabolism

- Vukovich & Dreifort 2001: 3 g/day HMB-Ca in elite master cyclists (300 miles/week training):
  - Time to reach VO2peak: +8% (leucine and placebo did not change)
  - VO2 at 2 mM blood lactate (OBLA): +9.1% (HMB), +2.1% (leucine), no change (placebo)
- **Proposed mechanisms**: HMB increases fatty acid oxidation, AMPK activity, Sirt1/Sirt3 activity, mitochondrial biogenesis (shown in adipocytes and skeletal muscle cells)
- Caloric restriction: Female judo athletes on 3-day energy restriction — HMB group lost more body fat; preserved peak power better (placebo -11% vs. HMB -5%)

## Mechanisms of Action

### Protein Synthesis (Anabolic)
1. **mTOR pathway**: HMB increases phosphorylation of mTOR, p70S6K, and 4E-BP1 → stimulates mRNA translation initiation → increases MPS
2. **GH/IGF-1 axis**: HMB increases pituitary GH mRNA, liver IGF-1 mRNA, and serum IGF-1 in rats. May require large change in plasma HMB; threshold response not established.
3. **Satellite cell activation**: HMB increases MyoD (proliferation marker), myogenin and MEF2 (differentiation markers) expression; increases myoblast number. Promotes muscle regenerative capacity.

### Protein Breakdown (Anti-Catabolic)
1. **Ubiquitin-proteasome pathway**: HMB decreases proteasome expression and activity during catabolic states → attenuates muscle protein degradation
2. **Caspase pathway**: HMB attenuates caspase upregulation; reduces myonuclear apoptosis (demonstrated in inflammatory cytokine-challenged myoblasts and during unloading)

### Metabolic Effects
- Increases AMPK, Sirt1, and Sirt3 activity → improved mitochondrial biogenesis, fat oxidation, energy metabolism, ROS defense
- May have implications for obesity, insulin resistance, diabetes

### Net Effect
HMB shifts muscle protein turnover toward net positive balance: stimulates synthesis (mTOR) + inhibits degradation (ubiquitin-proteasome and caspase) + enhances regenerative capacity (satellite cells).

## Practical Recommendations

1. **Dose**: 3 g/day (38 mg/kg/day for precise dosing), divided into 2-3 servings
2. **Timing relative to exercise**: 
   - HMB-Ca: 60-120 min pre-exercise (longer if consumed with glucose)
   - HMB-FA: 30-60 min pre-exercise (faster absorption allows shorter window)
3. **Pre-loading**: Begin 2 weeks before novel or intensified training programs
4. **Duration**: 
   - Untrained: Benefits in as little as 3 weeks
   - Trained: Minimum 6+ weeks with periodized, high-intensity training
5. **Training program**: Must provide adequate stimulus (high-intensity, compound movements, periodized/constantly varying loads)
6. **Form selection**: 
   - HMB-Ca: More studied; established safety and efficacy
   - HMB-FA: Pharmacokinetic advantages (faster, higher peak, greater tissue uptake); clinical superiority not yet established
7. **Special applications**:
   - Caloric restriction: May preserve lean mass and power output
   - Elderly: Increases LBM and functionality even without exercise; combines well with resistance training
   - Adolescent athletes: Preliminary evidence for benefits during growth and training
8. **Contraindications**: None established at recommended doses. Monitor for theoretical insulin sensitivity effects (based on single rodent study).
9. **Combinations**: Limited data on HMB + creatine (Jowko 2001 found additive effects in active males; O'Connor 2007 found no effect in trained rugby players)
