# ISSN Supplements Evidence Cheatsheet

## Evidence Level Key
- **A (Strong)**: Multiple meta-analyses and consistent RCTs supporting efficacy
- **B (Moderate)**: Several RCTs with generally positive but some mixed findings
- **C (Preliminary)**: Limited research; emerging evidence; mechanistic rationale exists
- **D (Insufficient)**: Inconsistent or insufficient evidence for recommendation

## Supplement Evidence Summary Table

| Supplement | Evidence Level | Recommended Dose | Loading Protocol | Sport/Activity Applicability | Primary Mechanism | Safety Profile |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Creatine Monohydrate** | A (Strong) | 3-5 g/day maintenance | 20-25 g/day (0.3 g/kg/day) for 5-7 days (optional) | Sprint/power sports, resistance training, team sports, combat sports, aging populations, neuroprotection | Increases PCr stores; enhances ATP resynthesis; CK/PCr energy shuttle; neuroprotection; anti-oxidant | Excellent. No kidney damage in healthy individuals at recommended doses. Only side effect: weight gain (1-3 kg). Long-term studies up to 5 years show no adverse effects. |
| **Caffeine** | A (Strong) - endurance B (Moderate) - strength/power | 3-6 mg/kg body mass; lower bound ~2 mg/kg | Single dose 60 min pre-exercise (capsule); 30 min (gum) | Aerobic endurance (most consistent), muscular endurance, strength, power, sport-specific actions, cognitive performance | Adenosine receptor antagonism (CNS primary); increased neurotransmitter release; reduced RPE; enhanced calcium mobilization; pain suppression | Generally safe. Side effects increase with dose (>9 mg/kg): anxiety, tachycardia, insomnia, GI distress. Genetic variation (CYP1A2, ADORA2A) affects response. Avoid late-day dosing for sleep quality. |
| **Beta-Alanine** | A (Strong) | 4-6 g/day in divided doses (≤1.6 g per dose) | Minimum 2-4 weeks for effect; 4 weeks for 40-60% carnosine increase | High-intensity exercise 60-240 s; time to exhaustion tasks; neuromuscular fatigue reduction; tactical athletes; older adults | Rate-limiting precursor to carnosine synthesis; enhances intracellular pH buffering (pKa 6.83); antioxidant via ROS scavenging and metal chelation | Safe at recommended doses. Primary side effect: paraesthesia (tingling), attenuated by sustained-release formulas and divided doses. Long-term safety data (>1 year) limited. Theoretically reduces taurine uptake (no human significance demonstrated). |
| **Protein Supplementation** | A (Strong) | 1.4-2.0 g/kg/day total; 20-40 g per meal (0.25 g/kg/meal) | Not applicable (daily distribution pattern) | All athletes; particularly resistance training, recovery from endurance exercise, weight management | Stimulates MPS via EAA delivery; leucine-mTOR signaling; positive protein balance; muscle damage mitigation when added to carbohydrate during endurance exercise | Safe in healthy individuals. Higher intakes (>3.0 g/kg/d) well-tolerated in studies. Pre-existing kidney disease warrants caution. |
| **HMB** | B (Moderate) | 3 g/day (38 mg/kg/day); divided 2-3 servings | 2 weeks prior to novel/damaging exercise | Untrained initiating training; trained during novel high-intensity programs; elderly sarcopenia prevention; caloric restriction; recovery from muscle-damaging exercise | Inhibits proteolysis (ubiquitin-proteasome pathway, caspase); stimulates MPS (mTOR pathway); satellite cell activation; mitochondrial biogenesis and fat oxidation | Safe in both young and old populations. No adverse effects at up to 6 g/day for 1 month or 3 g/day for 1 year. Possible effect on insulin sensitivity warrants further study. |
| **Sodium Bicarbonate (adjunct)** | B (Moderate) | 0.3-0.5 g/kg acutely pre-exercise | Acute single dose before event | High-intensity exercise with metabolic acidosis; possible additive effect with beta-alanine | Extracellular bicarbonate buffering; increases blood pH | GI discomfort common; individual tolerance testing recommended. 64-78% probability of additive benefit with beta-alanine. |

## Protein Source Comparison

| Source | Leucine Content | Digestion Rate | PDCAAS | Key Characteristics |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Whey Protein | ~11% | Fast | 1.0 | Highest leucine; rapid aminoacidemia; maximally stimulates MPS |
| Milk (whole) | ~10% | Moderate (blend) | 1.0 | Blend of whey + casein; sustained amino acid delivery |
| Casein | ~9.3% | Slow | 1.0 | Sustained release; pre-sleep use for overnight MPS; anti-catabolic |
| Egg | ~8.6% | Moderate-Fast | 1.0 | Complete amino acid profile; reference standard for protein quality |
| Beef/Meat | ~8% | Moderate | ~0.92 | Micronutrient-rich (iron, B12, selenium); carnitine source |
| Soy | ~8% | Intermediate | ~0.91 | Complete plant protein; phytoestrogens may inhibit mTOR; lower BCAAs |
| Rice | ~8% | Medium-Slow | ~0.47-0.65 | Plant alternative; similar body composition benefits to whey in some studies |
| Wheat | ~6.8% | Varies | ~0.25-0.42 | Low leucine; requires ~60 g dose to match MPS of 35 g casein |

## Daily Protein Intake Recommendations by Population

| Population | g/kg/day | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Sedentary adults (RDA) | 0.8 | Minimum to prevent deficiency; insufficient for athletes |
| Recreational exercisers | 1.0-1.4 | Meets general fitness needs |
| Endurance athletes | 1.2-1.6 | Higher during intensified training; add protein to carbohydrate during prolonged exercise for recovery |
| Strength/power athletes | 1.4-2.0 | Higher end during energy restriction or high-volume training |
| Weight loss / energy restriction | 2.0-3.0 | Preserves lean mass; 2-3x RDA recommended |
| Elderly (>65 years) | 1.2-2.0 | Counteract anabolic resistance; per-meal dose of ~40 g may be needed |

## Timing Summary

| Supplement | Timing Strategy |
|---|---|
| Creatine | Any time of day; co-ingest with carbohydrate/protein for enhanced uptake; consistency matters most |
| Caffeine | 60 min pre-exercise (capsule); 30 min pre-exercise (gum); mid/late exercise for longer events; avoid within 6 h of sleep |
| Beta-Alanine | Divided doses throughout day (≤1.6 g per dose); with meals to enhance carnosine loading; 4-week minimum for effect |
| Protein | Within 3-4 h windows across day; pre- or post-exercise both effective; pre-sleep casein (30-40 g) for overnight MPS |
| HMB | 60-120 min pre-exercise (HMB-Ca); 30-60 min pre-exercise (HMB-FA); 2-week pre-loading recommended |

## Safety and Contraindications Quick Reference

| Supplement | Contraindications / Cautions |
|---|---|
| Creatine | Pre-existing kidney disease (consult physician); use only German-sourced Creapure for purity; avoid other forms (ethyl ester, serum, buffered) |
| Caffeine | Anxiety disorders; cardiac arrhythmias; pregnancy (reduced metabolism); sleep disorders; CYP1A2 CC genotype (slow metabolizers may experience performance impairment at 4 mg/kg); near bedtime |
| Beta-Alanine | No known contraindications at recommended doses; paraesthesia managed by sustained-release or divided dosing |
| Protein (high intake) | Pre-existing kidney disease (consult physician); ensure adequate hydration |
| HMB | Limited safety data in pregnancy/lactation; possible concern for insulin sensitivity (based on one rodent study) |
