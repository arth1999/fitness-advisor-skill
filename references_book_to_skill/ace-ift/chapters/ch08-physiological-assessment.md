# Chapter 8: Physiological Assessments

## Purpose
Physiological assessments provide objective data about cardiorespiratory fitness, body composition, and health-related physiological markers. They are used to establish baselines, set appropriate training intensities, and track progress over time.

## Body Composition Assessment

### Anthropometric Measurements
- **Body Mass Index (BMI)**: Weight (kg) / Height (m2). Categories: Underweight (< 18.5), Normal (18.5-24.9), Overweight (25-29.9), Obese (>= 30).
- **Waist Circumference**: Measured at narrowest point between ribs and iliac crest. Risk thresholds: Men > 102 cm (40 in); Women > 88 cm (35 in).
- **Waist-to-Hip Ratio**: Circumference ratio. Risk: Men > 0.95; Women > 0.86.

### Skinfold Measurements
- Sites: Chest/pectoral, midaxillary, triceps, subscapular, abdomen, suprailiac, thigh (men); triceps, suprailiac, thigh, abdomen (women).
- Three-site or seven-site formulas estimate body fat percentage.

### Bioelectrical Impedance Analysis (BIA)
- Measures body's resistance to low-level electrical current to estimate body water and fat-free mass.
- Limitations: Affected by hydration status, food intake, and exercise.

## Cardiovascular Assessments
Resting heart rate, resting blood pressure, and exercise testing protocols.

### Heart Rate and Blood Pressure
- **Resting Heart Rate (RHR)**: Measured in the morning before rising; average adult 60-100 bpm. Athletes may be 40-60 bpm.
- **Blood Pressure Categories (ACSM)**:
  - Normal: SBP < 120 and DBP < 80 mmHg
  - Elevated: SBP 120-129 and DBP < 80
  - Hypertension Stage 1: SBP 130-139 or DBP 80-89
  - Hypertension Stage 2: SBP >= 140 or DBP >= 90

### Ventilatory Thresholds
- **VT1 (First Ventilatory Threshold)**: The intensity where blood lactate begins accumulating faster than it is cleared. Talk test: point at which continuous talking first becomes difficult. For trained individuals, sustainable for 1-2 hours. Represents the cross-over from predominantly fat to predominantly carbohydrate fuel.
- **VT2 (Second Ventilatory Threshold / Respiratory Compensation Threshold)**: Point where ventilation increases disproportionally; hyperventilation occurs. Talk test: absolute inability to speak comfortably. For trained individuals, sustainable for 30-60 minutes.

### Submaximal Exercise Tests
Submaximal tests estimate VO2max without the risk and need for maximal testing.

1. **YMCA Cycle Ergometer Test**: 3-minute stages at progressive workloads based on HR response. Plot HR against workload to estimate VO2max. HR must reach steady state (within 5 bpm between minutes 2 and 3 of each stage). Requires at least two stages with HR between 110 bpm and 85% MHR.

2. **Rockport 1-Mile Walk Test**: Client walks one mile as fast as possible on a track or treadmill. VO2max estimated from: time, ending HR, age, sex, and body weight.

3. **YMCA Submaximal Step Test**: 3-minute step test at 96 bpm cadence on 12-inch step. Recovery HR measured for 1 minute post-test.

### MHR Estimation Formulas (ACSM-recommended)
- **Gellish et al. (2007)**: 206.9 - (0.67 x age) -- standard deviation ~7 bpm
- **Tanaka, Monahan, & Seals (2001)**: 208 - (0.7 x age)
- **Avoid 220-age**: Standard deviation ~12 bpm; overestimates in young, underestimates in old

## Test Termination Criteria
Stop exercise testing immediately for: onset of angina or chest pain, significant drop in SBP (>10 mmHg) with increased workload, excessive rise in BP (SBP >250, DBP >115), dizziness or near syncope, signs of poor perfusion, subject requests to stop, failure of testing equipment, significant arrhythmia.

## Rating of Perceived Exertion (RPE)
- **6-20 Scale (Borg)**: "Somewhat hard" (13) ~70% HRR; "Hard" (15) ~85% HRR; "Very hard" (17) ~90% HRR.
- **0-10 Category-Ratio Scale**: "Moderate" (3) ~70% HRR; "Somewhat strong" (4) ~75% HRR; "Strong" (5) ~80% HRR.

**Session RPE**: Overall RPE (0-10) multiplied by session duration (minutes) = training load. Useful for monitoring total training load and guiding progression.
