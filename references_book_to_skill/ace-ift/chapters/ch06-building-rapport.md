# Chapter 6: Building Rapport and the Initial Investigation

## Building Rapport
Rapport is the foundation of the ACE IFT Model. It creates open communication, builds trust, and fosters a desire to engage in the exercise program. Build rapport early through:

- **Professional appearance and demeanor**: Dress appropriately; maintain confident, approachable body language.
- **Active listening**: Listen more than you speak. Paraphrase client statements to confirm understanding.
- **Empathy**: Acknowledge clients' feelings and concerns without judgment.
- **Positive first exercise experiences**: The initial sessions should make clients feel capable and successful. Avoid excessive DOMS or failure in early sessions.
- **Consistent communication**: Check in between sessions. Celebrate small achievements.

## The Health-Risk Appraisal Process
Before any assessment or exercise program begins, the trainer must collect and review:

### 1. Health History Questionnaire (HHQ)
Review past and current medical conditions, surgeries, injuries, medications, family history, and lifestyle factors. Key screening areas include:
- Cardiovascular disease and risk factors
- Respiratory conditions (asthma, COPD, bronchitis)
- Musculoskeletal injuries and conditions
- Orthopedic surgeries and rehabilitation status
- Metabolic conditions (diabetes, thyroid disorders)
- Pregnancy status

### 2. Coronary Artery Disease (CAD) Risk Factor Stratification
Using ACSM guidelines, identify positive risk factors:
1. Age (men >= 45; women >= 55)
2. Family history of early heart disease
3. Current cigarette smoking
4. Sedentary lifestyle
5. Hypertension (SBP >= 140 or DBP >= 90 mmHg, or on medication)
6. Dyslipidemia (LDL >= 130 or HDL < 40 mg/dL, or on medication)
7. Prediabetes (fasting glucose >= 100 mg/dL)
8. Obesity (BMI >= 30 kg/m2 or waist > 102 cm men / > 88 cm women)
9. Negative risk factor: HDL >= 60 mg/dL (subtracts one from total)

**Risk Classification**:
- **Low risk**: Asymptomatic with <= 1 risk factor -- no medical exam or exercise test needed before moderate exercise
- **Moderate risk**: Asymptomatic with >= 2 risk factors -- medical exam recommended before vigorous exercise; proceed with moderate exercise
- **High risk**: Known cardiovascular, pulmonary, or metabolic disease, or signs/symptoms -- medical exam and exercise testing recommended before any exercise

### 3. Physical Activity Readiness Questionnaire (PAR-Q)
Minimum seven-question screen for pre-exercise health risks. If any question is answered "yes," medical clearance is recommended.

## Informed Consent
A formal document that explains the purpose, procedures, risks, and benefits of the exercise program. The client acknowledges understanding and agrees to participate voluntarily. While not a legal waiver, it demonstrates that the trainer informed the client of risks.

## Medical Clearance and Referral
When risk factors, signs, or symptoms are identified, refer to appropriate medical professionals. Develop relationships with local physicians, physical therapists, and dietitians for cross-referrals.
