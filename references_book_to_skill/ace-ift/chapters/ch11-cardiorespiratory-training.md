# Chapter 11: Cardiorespiratory Training -- Programming and Progression

## Overview
Cardiorespiratory training improves heart, lung, blood, and muscle tissue function, enhancing the ability to perform sustained physical activity. The ACE IFT Cardiorespiratory Training module provides four progressive phases built around ventilatory thresholds.

## ACE IFT Cardiorespiratory Training Model

### Phase 1: Aerobic Base Training
**Goal**: Build an aerobic foundation. Improve cardiorespiratory health parameters.
- **Intensity**: Below VT1 -- client can talk comfortably throughout (RPE 3-4/10, or 12-13 on 6-20 scale)
- **Duration**: Start low (10-20 minutes) and progress to 30-60 minutes
- **Frequency**: 3-5 days/week per ACSM guidelines
- **Method**: Steady-state, continuous exercise (walking, cycling, elliptical)
- **Progression**: Increase duration first (by ~10% per week), then increase frequency, then consider increasing intensity toward VT1
- **Exit criteria**: Client can sustain 20-30 minutes of steady-state exercise at HR just below VT1

### Phase 2: Aerobic Efficiency Training
**Goal**: Improve aerobic capacity and efficiency. Increase time spent at VT1.
- **Intensity**: At or slightly above VT1 -- talking becomes more difficult (RPE 5/10)
- **Method**: Introduce aerobic intervals -- work intervals at HR just above VT1, recovery intervals below VT1
- **Example**: 4 x 4 minutes at VT1+ with 3 minutes recovery below VT1
- **Progression**: Increase interval duration, decrease recovery duration, increase number of intervals
- **Exit criteria**: Client tolerates sustained intervals at VT1 for 10+ minutes

### Phase 3: Anaerobic Endurance Training
**Goal**: Improve lactate tolerance and clearance. Increase time spent at and above VT2.
- **Intensity**: At and above VT2 -- cannot talk comfortably at all; "barely speak a word" (RPE 7-8/10)
- **Method**: High-intensity intervals with work:rest ratios of 1:2 or 1:1
- **Example**: 4 x 2 minutes at VT2+ with 4 minutes active recovery
- **Progression**: Decrease work:rest ratio (1:2 -> 1:1), increase total interval volume
- **Exit criteria**: Client tolerates 20+ minutes of total interval time in this zone

### Phase 4: Anaerobic Power Training
**Goal**: Maximize anaerobic power for performance-oriented clients.
- **Intensity**: Maximal/supramaximal effort (RPE 9-10/10)
- **Method**: Short, maximal intervals with long recovery (work:rest >= 1:3)
- **Example**: 8 x 30 seconds maximal with 2-3 minutes recovery; Tabata-style 20s:10s protocols
- **Target population**: Competitive athletes only; never appropriate for general health/fitness clients

## Methods for Monitoring Exercise Intensity

### Heart Rate Methods
1. **%MHR**: Not recommended due to large standard deviation in age-predicted formulas. Use Gellish (206.9 - 0.67 x age) or Tanaka (208 - 0.7 x age) instead of 220-age.
2. **Karvonen / Heart Rate Reserve (HRR)**: Target HR = (HRR x %intensity) + RHR. More accurate because it accounts for individual RHR differences. HRR = MHR - RHR.

### Talk Test
The simplest, most practical field-based method:
- **Below VT1**: Can talk comfortably in complete sentences
- **At VT1**: Talking becomes more challenging; need to pause speaking during exercise
- **At/Above VT2**: Cannot speak more than a word or two

### RPE and Session RPE
- Session RPE = overall session RPE (0-10) x duration (minutes) = Training Load score
- Allows flexible progression: Trainer sets weekly target score; client chooses how to achieve it (frequency x duration x intensity)

### METs (Metabolic Equivalents)
- 1 MET = 3.5 mL/kg/min (assumed resting metabolic rate)
- Light: < 3 METs; Moderate: 3-6 METs; Vigorous: > 6 METs
- Not a direct VO2 measurement; values vary with individual differences

### Caloric Expenditure
Gross VO2 (L/min) x 5 kcal/L O2 = kcal/min. Must convert relative VO2 (mL/kg/min) to absolute VO2 (L/min) first by multiplying by body weight in kg.

## Training Principles for Special Populations
- **Older adults**: Slower training response; limit to 2 high-intensity sessions/week; prioritize cardiovascular risk avoidance, orthopedic risk avoidance, muscle preservation
- **Sedentary individuals**: Start in Phase 1; prioritize consistency over intensity for first 4-6 weeks
- **Pregnancy**: Avoid supine exercise after 1st trimester; moderate intensity; avoid overheating

## Key Principle
Cardio training follows decreasing returns -- the largest health benefits come from modest amounts of exercise (150 min/week moderate). Beyond the minimum, benefits increase with volume, but each additional unit produces progressively smaller returns.
