# Chapter 12: ACE IFT Model -- Practical Application and Case Studies

## Purpose
This chapter integrates all prior chapters by demonstrating how the ACE IFT Model is applied to real client scenarios. Six case studies represent typical client types trainers encounter. Each case study identifies the client's behavior-change stage, relevant assessments, program design across both training modules, and progression strategies.

## Case Study 1: Sharon -- Postpartum Return to Fitness
**Profile**: 33-year-old mother of two young children. Former recreational runner (5K/10K) and soccer player. Has been inactive during pregnancy and postpartum.

**Assessment Findings**: Mild low-back discomfort (postural, not pathological). Slight anterior pelvic tilt and weakened gluteals. Aerobic base diminished.

**Program Approach**:
- **Functional Movement Module**: Begin Phase 1 -- core activation, gluteal strengthening, pelvic alignment, static balance. Progress to Phase 2 -- body-weight squat, lunge, push/pull patterns.
- **Cardiorespiratory Module**: Phase 1 -- steady-state walking building to 20-30 minutes at RPE 3-4/10. Progress to Phase 2 with walk-jog intervals when ready.
- **Behavioral Strategy**: Leverage pre-existing fitness identity. Set short-term goals around consistency (3 sessions/week). Use her social motivation.

## Case Study 2: David -- 57-Year-Old Masters Athlete
**Profile**: Active 57-year-old male with knee and low-back discomfort. Wants sport-specific training.

**Assessment Findings**: Postural deviations including forward head and kyphosis. Weak core endurance per McGill battery. Moderate CAD risk factors.

**Program Approach**:
- Priorities: Address postural deviations and core stability before sport-specific loading.
- Functional Phase 1-2 emphasis with corrective exercise before advancing.
- Cardio Phase 2 training with his preferred aerobic activities.
- Incorporate sport-specific movement patterns after postural corrections.

## Case Study 3: Young Athlete (17F Volleyball Player)
**Profile**: 17-year-old female volleyball player seeking to increase power and strength for college scholarship.

**Assessment Findings**: Movement screen identifies knee valgus during squat, shoulder mobility asymmetry.

**Program Approach**:
- Address knee valgus with gluteal activation and hip external rotator strengthening.
- Phase 3-4 resistance training: linear periodization for power development.
- Include plyometrics and reactive training.
- Cardio Phase 2-3 as conditioning supplement.

## Case Study 4: Stanley -- 28-Year-Old Sedentary Male
**Profile**: 28-year-old sedentary male with no consistent exercise history. Wants to improve overall health.

**Assessment Findings**: Multiple CAD risk factors (sedentary, elevated BMI, family history). Low fitness baseline. Low self-efficacy for exercise.

**Program Approach**:
- Precontemplation/Preparation stage focus. Build self-efficacy through mastery experiences.
- Very gradual introduction: Phase 1 functional stability, Phase 1 aerobic base.
- Early focus entirely on consistency and positive experience, not intensity.
- 4-6 weeks of foundation before any moderate-intensity work.

## Case Study 5: 64-Year-Old Active Retiree
**Profile**: 64-year-old active retiree who plays recreational tennis. Wants to improve competitive readiness.

**Assessment Findings**: Good fitness baseline. Age-related orthopedic considerations. Some flexibility deficits.

**Program Approach**:
- Address flexibility deficits in Phase 1.
- Phase 3 resistance training at moderate intensity with tennis-specific movements.
- Cardio Phase 2-3 with tennis-specific interval patterns.
- Limit to 2 hard sessions/week with full recovery.

## Case Study 6: 30-Year-Old Professional Preparing for Wedding
**Profile**: 30-year-old female professional wanting to "look her best" for her wedding.

**Assessment Findings**: Time-constrained with demanding job. Extrinsically motivated. Reasonable fitness base.

**Program Approach**:
- Capitalize on high extrinsic motivation while building intrinsic enjoyment.
- Efficient sessions combining resistance (Phase 3) and cardio (Phase 2) in circuit format.
- Set realistic timeline for visible changes (12-16 weeks).
- Transition coaching toward maintenance and intrinsic motivation post-wedding.

## Universal Principles from All Cases
1. Start every client with behavior-change stage assessment before exercise assessment.
2. Address stability/mobility deficits before loading movement patterns.
3. Build aerobic base before introducing higher-intensity cardio.
4. Progression is always individualized -- no two clients follow the same timeline.
5. Rapport and positive experiences are prerequisites for all training success.
6. Each client may be in different phases within the two training modules.
