# Chapter 10: Resistance Training -- Programming and Progression

## ACE IFT Phase 3: Load Training
Once clients demonstrate competent movement patterns with body weight (Phase 2), external resistance is applied. This phase addresses muscular endurance, hypertrophy (strength-endurance), and strength depending on client goals.

## FITT-VP Guidelines for Resistance Training (ACSM)
- **Frequency**: 2-3 days/week per muscle group
- **Intensity**: 
  - Beginners/novices: 60-70% 1-RM for strength
  - Experienced: >= 80% 1-RM for strength
  - Older adults beginning: 40-50% 1-RM
  - Muscular endurance: < 50% 1-RM
  - Power for older adults: 20-50% 1-RM
- **Repetitions**: 8-12 for strength/power; 10-15 for older beginners; 15-20 for muscular endurance
- **Sets**: 2-4 sets for strength; single set effective for beginners/older adults; <= 2 sets for muscular endurance
- **Rest**: 2-3 minutes between sets for strength; >= 48 hours between sessions for same muscle group
- **Progression**: Gradual increases in resistance, reps, and/or frequency

## Program Design Variables
1. **Needs analysis**: Client goals (endurance, hypertrophy, strength, power), available equipment, time constraints
2. **Exercise selection**: Multi-joint exercises first (compound movements), then single-joint (isolation). Address agonist-antagonist balance.
3. **Exercise order**: Large muscles before small; multi-joint before single-joint; high intensity before low intensity
4. **Training volume**: Sets x reps x load; total volume drives adaptation
5. **Rest periods**: Shorter (30-90s) for hypertrophy/muscular endurance; longer (2-5 min) for maximal strength/power
6. **Tempo**: Expressed as eccentric/pause/concentric/pause (e.g., 3/1/2/0). Eccentric emphasis enhances strength and hypertrophy.
7. **Training frequency**: 2-3 days/week per muscle group; split routines for advanced clients

## Types of Resistance Training
- **Body weight**: Calisthenics; can be progressed via leverage changes
- **Free weights**: Barbells, dumbbells; require greater stabilization, functional carry-over
- **Machines**: Fixed path of motion; reduced stabilization demand; good for beginners or isolation
- **Resistance bands/tubes**: Variable resistance throughout ROM; portable
- **Cables**: Constant tension, multi-planar movement options
- **Medicine balls**: Power and rotational training
- **Kettlebells**: Offset center of gravity challenges stability

## Periodization Models
- **Linear periodization**: Gradual increase in intensity with decrease in volume over time (classic approach)
- **Undulating periodization**: Daily or weekly variation in training volume and intensity
- **Block periodization**: Focused training blocks on specific adaptations (e.g., endurance block, strength block, power block)

## ACE IFT Phase 4: Performance Training
For athletic clients who have successfully progressed through Phase 3. Focus on:
- **Power**: Rate of force development; Olympic lifts, plyometrics, medicine ball throws
- **Speed**: Sprint training, resisted/assisted running
- **Agility**: Multi-directional movement; change of direction drills
- **Reactivity**: Quick response to stimuli; depth jumps, reactive drills

## Special Considerations
- **Youth**: Focus on technique and movement literacy; avoid maximal loads before skeletal maturity
- **Older adults**: Lower intensity (40-50% 1-RM initially), higher reps (10-15), prioritize balance and functional strength
- **Pregnancy**: Avoid supine exercises after first trimester; avoid Valsalva maneuver; moderate intensity
- **Hypertension**: Avoid heavy isometric contractions and Valsalva maneuver; monitor BP

## Progression Principles
Progress clients by manipulating one variable at a time:
1. Increase reps with same load (endurance phase)
2. Increase load with same reps (strength phase)
3. Increase sets (volume)
4. Decrease rest intervals (density)
5. Increase movement complexity or ROM
6. Increase training frequency
