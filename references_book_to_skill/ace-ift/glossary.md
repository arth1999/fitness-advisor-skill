# ACE Personal Trainer Manual -- Key Terms Glossary

## A
- **ACE IFT Model (ACE Integrated Fitness Training Model)**: A comprehensive exercise programming system with two training modules (functional movement/resistance and cardiorespiratory), each with four phases, built on a foundation of rapport and behavioral strategies.
- **Aerobic Base Training (Phase 1 Cardio)**: Steady-state exercise below VT1 designed to build cardiorespiratory foundation.
- **Aerobic Efficiency Training (Phase 2 Cardio)**: Interval training at or slightly above VT1 to improve aerobic capacity.
- **Anaerobic Endurance Training (Phase 3 Cardio)**: High-intensity intervals at/above VT2 to improve lactate tolerance.
- **Anaerobic Power Training (Phase 4 Cardio)**: Maximal-effort short-duration intervals for performance athletes.
- **Angina**: Chest pain or pressure caused by inadequate blood flow to the heart muscle, often a symptom of CAD.
- **Atherosclerosis**: Hardening and narrowing of arteries from cholesterol and calcium deposits.
- **Autonomy**: The psychological need to feel in control of one's own behavior; a key component of Self-Determination Theory.

## B
- **Bend-and-Lift Pattern**: One of the five primary movement patterns; includes squatting and hip-hinging movements.
- **BMI (Body Mass Index)**: Weight (kg) / Height (m2). Used to categorize underweight, normal weight, overweight, and obesity.

## C
- **CAD (Coronary Artery Disease)**: Atherosclerosis of the arteries supplying the heart; major cause of heart attacks.
- **Cardiorespiratory Training Module (ACE IFT)**: One of two ACE IFT modules; progresses through aerobic base, aerobic efficiency, anaerobic endurance, and anaerobic power.
- **Contemplation Stage**: TTM stage where person intends to change within six months but remains ambivalent.
- **Core**: The lumbopelvic-hip complex; the body's center of gravity. Includes abdominals, spinal extensors, hip musculature.
- **CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)**: Emergency procedure combining chest compressions and rescue breathing. Required certification for ACE trainers.

## D
- **DOMS (Delayed Onset Muscle Soreness)**: Muscle soreness appearing 24-72 hours after unaccustomed exercise; common in the first 1-2 weeks of a new program.

## F
- **FITT-VP**: Frequency, Intensity, Time, Type, Volume, Progression/Pattern -- the variables of exercise program design.
- **Five Primary Movement Patterns**: Bend-and-lift, single-leg, pushing, pulling, and rotational (spiral) movements.
- **Function-Health-Fitness-Performance Continuum**: Sequential progression model where training moves from restoring function to improving health, developing fitness, and enhancing performance.

## G
- **GXT (Graded Exercise Test)**: Progressive exercise test used to assess cardiorespiratory fitness and estimate VO2max.

## H
- **Health History Questionnaire (HHQ)**: Comprehensive form collecting medical history, risk factors, medications, and prior injuries.
- **HIPAA**: Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act; protects patient health information.
- **HRR (Heart Rate Reserve)**: MHR minus RHR; used in the Karvonen formula for determining exercise intensity.
- **Hypertension**: High blood pressure; SBP >= 130 or DBP >= 80 mmHg (ACSM classification).

## I
- **Informed Consent**: Document acknowledging the client understands the risks, benefits, and nature of the exercise program.
- **Intrinsic Motivation**: Motivation driven by internal satisfaction and enjoyment of the activity itself.

## K
- **Karvonen Method / HRR Method**: Target HR = (HRR x % intensity) + RHR. More accurate than %MHR because it accounts for individual resting heart rate.

## L
- **Load Training (Phase 3 Resistance)**: ACE IFT phase applying external resistance to established movement patterns for endurance, hypertrophy, and strength.

## M
- **McGill Torso Endurance Test Battery**: Three-test protocol (flexor, lateral, extensor endurance holds) assessing core muscle endurance and balance ratios.
- **MHR (Maximum Heart Rate)**: Highest heart rate an individual can achieve. Estimated via Gellish formula (206.9 - 0.67 x age) or Tanaka formula (208 - 0.7 x age).
- **MET (Metabolic Equivalent)**: 1 MET = 3.5 mL O2/kg/min, representing approximate resting metabolic rate. Light < 3 METs, Moderate 3-6 METs, Vigorous > 6 METs.
- **Movement Training (Phase 2 Resistance)**: ACE IFT phase teaching the five primary movement patterns with body weight before external loading.

## P
- **PAR-Q (Physical Activity Readiness Questionnaire)**: Minimum seven-question pre-exercise screening tool.
- **Performance Training (Phase 4 Resistance)**: ACE IFT phase for athletic clients; power, speed, agility, reactivity.
- **Periodization**: Systematic planning of training cycles to optimize performance and prevent overtraining. Linear, undulating, or block models.
- **Precontemplation Stage**: TTM stage where person has no intention of changing behavior within the next six months.
- **Preparation Stage**: TTM stage where person intends to take action within 30 days and has taken small preparatory steps.
- **PRICE**: Protect, Restrict activity, Ice, Compression, Elevation -- acute injury management protocol.

## R
- **RAID (Risk, Assess, Identify, Diagnose)**: Scope of practice reminder: trainers do NOT diagnose.
- **Rapport**: The foundation of the ACE IFT Model; mutual trust and understanding between trainer and client.
- **RPE (Rating of Perceived Exertion)**: Subjective scale (6-20 or 0-10) for monitoring exercise intensity.
- **RHR (Resting Heart Rate)**: Heart rate measured at complete rest; typically 60-100 bpm for average adults, 40-60 for athletes.

## S
- **Scope of Practice**: The legally defined boundaries of services a professional may provide, based on education, training, and certification.
- **Self-Determination Theory (SDT)**: Theory stating intrinsic motivation depends on satisfaction of three psychological needs: autonomy, competence, and relatedness.
- **Self-Efficacy**: Belief in one's ability to successfully execute a specific behavior. The strongest predictor of exercise adherence.
- **Session RPE**: Overall session RPE (0-10) multiplied by session duration (minutes); used to quantify training load.
- **Stability and Mobility Training (Phase 1 Resistance)**: ACE IFT phase addressing postural deviations, muscle imbalances, joint mobility, and core stability.

## T
- **Talk Test**: Simple field-based method for estimating exercise intensity relative to ventilatory thresholds. Comfortable speech = below VT1; difficult speech = at/near VT1; inability to speak = at/near VT2.
- **Transtheoretical Model (TTM)**: Five-stage behavior change model: Precontemplation, Contemplation, Preparation, Action, Maintenance.

## V
- **Valsalva Maneuver**: Forced exhalation against a closed glottis. Increases intrathoracic pressure and BP; should generally be avoided during exercise, especially by clients with cardiovascular conditions.
- **VO2max**: Maximal oxygen uptake; the gold standard measure of cardiorespiratory fitness (mL/kg/min).
- **VT1 (First Ventilatory Threshold)**: Exercise intensity where blood lactate begins accumulating faster than it is cleared. Corresponds to the point where sustained talking first becomes difficult.
- **VT2 (Second Ventilatory Threshold / RCT)**: Intensity where hyperventilation occurs and speaking is limited to a word or two. Represents the highest sustainable intensity for 30-60 minutes in trained individuals.
